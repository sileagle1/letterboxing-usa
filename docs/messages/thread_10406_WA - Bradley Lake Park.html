WA - Bradley Lake Park

From: Deziree Iams (DZpurple@msn.com) | Date: 2003-01-20 21:58:27 UTC-08:00
We've planted a new box at Bradley Lake Park in Puyallup, Washington:
 
Letterbox: "Catch Me!"
City: Puyallup
County: Pierce
Washington
Placed by: Sun Seeker & DZDezi

Location:  This box is placed at Bradley Lake Park in Puyallup.  Just a stone's throw from the Puyallup/South Hill retail madness on 31st Ave. SE, this 59-acre park site includes a 12-acre lake. There are picnic areas, trails, and a place for kids to fish -even a small lake.

"Catch Me!" clues: (DZDezi insisted that the clues rhyme this time) 

Bradley Lake Trail goes around the lake, 
Counter-clockwise is the way to take.
The bridge is too far, the gravel path not enough,
We hope that these clues are not too tough.
Find a burnt cedar without branches on one side,
You'll see a trail to the treasure we did hide.
It's a  low trail, so be sure to duck,
You'll find some tree remnants with some luck.
Left to a multi-trunk cedar in a few paces,
Around behind is the best of places. 
A nook or a cranny, whatever you may call it,
Its Tupperware sized, just the right fit!
 
Placed January 20, 2003 by Sun Seeker & DZDezi

From: dvn2rckr <dvn2rckr@yahoo.com> (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-01-25 00:25:23 UTC
At noon's hour this day, we went to the park at Bradley Lake,
Found a little plastic box with a log and a stamp, for heaven's sake.

Much to our surprise as we walked the lake all around,
We found an old lady who had fallen to the ground.

Her nose appeared broken and several loose teeth, too,
She suffered such a fate, we almost didn't know what to do.

Yet another 911 call and the paramedics soon came,
To take the lady to shelter and restore her to previous health, just
the same.

This being the third letterboxing trek involving paramedics or
police,
We're seeking, desiring future outings with peace.

Yet another day's adventures in which we surely did partake,
We're soon thinking of changing our letterboxing namesake... ;)


dvn2r ckr (Adventure Seeker)
F251 P60 something X35 E2

P.S.

Spoke to the park's caretaker & learned quite a bit,
Seems there are plans for park improvement just waiting to hit.
They'll relocate the parking, build an amphiteater to boot,
They're adding new hiking trails, that will soon follow suit.
Thought about adding another letterbox to the area for fun,
But figured too much work is yet waiting to be done.
Perhaps another location, to hide these little guys,
Keep posted to this board, for those clues, with your curious
eyes. ;)
