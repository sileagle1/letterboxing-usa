CT - White Memorial Foundation Requires Permission For Letterboxes

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2007-11-28 20:07:38 UTC
I just received the following email from a ranger at the White
Memorial Foundation in Litchfield, CT:

It has come to our attention that there are many letterboxes at White
Memorial. You will need to get permission for any box(es) you have
here. You can contact me and I can go out and look at any you may
have here. We are not against letterboxing, but need to know where
they are so they do not interfere with things we have going on here.
Any help spreading the word to others that have letterboxes here
would be appreciated.

Thank you,

Lukas Hyder

Assistant Superintendent/Forester

White Memorial Foundation

PO Box 368

Litchfield, CT 06759

860.567.0857

lukas@whitememorialcc.org


Since he asked for help spreading the word, I decided to post his
information here. I have already replied with clues to my box
(Connecticut Yankee) and I hope he allows it to stay. It sounds like
he just wants to know where the boxes are, so cooperation may let us
keep letterboxing in this park. I don't live in CT, otherwise I
would volunteer to meet with him. Maybe someone up that way can do
this to smooth the way for letterboxing? Just a thought.

Silver Eagle
