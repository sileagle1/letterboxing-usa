From: RayvenMom (RayvenMom@myndworx.com) | Date: 2003-10-21 08:35:46 UTC-07:00
It sets pretty quickly into a "soft stage" in about an hour, but to be sure
of it's 'take', we typically wait 24 hours before using the stamp...

We used to put a thin piece of foam between the stamp and the wood backing,
but soon realized that the foam really didn't have much to do in the
pressing of the image and only served to make the stamp look more like a
store-bought stamp...

As for the wood backing, we do it a couple of different ways... for the
smaller stamps (i.e. 1x2, 2x2, 2x3, etc.) you can get pre-cut pieces at most
craft stores like Michael's which is where we get ours... they are sold in
packages of 5 pieces or so, depending on the size, and are relatively
inexpensive... for the bigger stamps, RayvenDad goes out into his woodshop
and cuts the size needed from scrap lumber and such on his scroll saw... As
a matter of fact, when the ones that we have bought are all gone, he'll be
making all of the wood backings himself from plywood... The 'PNWLb Fright
Fest '03' stamp is backed by a piece of lumber that is about a 1/2 inch
thick so not all backings have to be thin... I guess it's just a matter of
preference...

"On the Mend" RayvenMom

I use a general purpose 10 minute epoxy available almost anywhere to
mount the pink stuff on 1/4 inch hobby plywood cut to size. It sets
quickly, seems to be immune to water, and no warpage problems noted
so far. Hope this helps!
