The Last Dinosaur
Volusia County
nearest city: DeBary
Planted March 24, 2002
Easy

Fee area; leashed dogs ok but do pick up after them

Find the park named for the Zodiac twins. From the pavilion area, cross the
spillway and follow the sidewalk into the closest woods. Pass the shell
midden sign and follow path to clearing in front of marsh. Turn right and
stop when you see two thick fence posts close together on the left. Ahead
are the giant peppermint sticks whose engines eat dinosaur oil, and to the
right a cluster of palms. The Last Dinosaur rests behind the leftmost palm,
covered with palm sticks.

For a wild walk out, continue on that path; it circles the park through a
forest of spanish-moss-draped live oak trees. Listen for the squawk of the
Great Blue Heron. At dusk, a peacock or two may strut past.
