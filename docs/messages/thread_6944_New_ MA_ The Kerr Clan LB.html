The Kerr Clan Letterbox
Chesterfield Gorge, Chesterfield MA
(Hampshire County)

Placed: August 13, 2002 by TehutiKa, AsetKa and kids!
Distance: 1/4 mile, if that
Time: Under a half hour, but you might want to stay a lot longer
Difficulty: Easy walk, some rough terrain.

Take Rt 9 West through Northampton to Rt 143 in Williamsburg. Take Rt 143
until you see the sign for Chesterfield Gorge on the left, just after you
cross over a bridge. Follow this street until you see the second sign for the
Gorge, and drive up to the parking lot. On weekends in season, there is a
$2.00 parking fee, but members of the Trustees of the Reservations are free.
You can get more information about this park, and the Trustees of the
Reservations at http://www.thetrustees.org.

Chesterfield Gorge, in addition to being one of our favorite places to visit,
is also a very important place to two of our family members. Gary and Susan
Kerr, my wife's parents, were married here in 1994 in a private ceremony. So
private that it was just the two of them and the justice of the peace who met
them there!

The stamp here is placed in honor of their wedding. The stamp itself is the
symbol and motto of the Clan Kerr. The Clan Motto is on the stamp, "Sero sed
Serio", which means, "Late but in earnest". I leave it to you to puzzle out
the meaning.
