From: (tmiller633@aol.com) | Date: 2004-02-22 09:01:14 UTC-05:00
Girl Scout Gold is the highest award in Girl Scouts. It is much like the Boy
Scout Eagle except in Girl Scouts you have to earn the Bronze & Silver Awards
before you can earn Gold.

Now some exciting news for letterboxing. My first box that was placed at
National Cemetery will be receiving some local media attention. The Dir. of the
National Cemetery called yesterday and told me that I have to sign a "Memo of
Responsibility" in order to have box on US Gov. owned land. Tues or Wed the
National Cemetery will be hosting a "signing party" and have local media, my
principal, G/S Exec Dir and various other people there. I was told by the Dir
of National Cemetery that my request went to Atlanta & then on to Washington
for approval. My MOR will go back to Washington and he also said that this
could go as far as Congress. The location of the box will not be disclosed. I
think this is great because it will give letterboxing & National Cemetery media
attention. I hope this project will increase patriotism among our citizens.
Patriotism has fallen to very little importance.

Will let everyone know how it goes.

NOW I NEED SOME AVID LETTERBOXERS TO FIND MY BOX SO THAT I CAN REALLY
SHOW EVERYONE HOW IT WORKS!!!

Amanda

Re: [LbNA] Re: New boxes

From: dave & diane (vonderinsel@cox.net) | Date: 2004-02-23 07:33:03 UTC-05:00
Others have done very well to hide boxes in stone walls, on the outside edge (lest well-meaning groundskeepers on the inside throw them out). Some use gravestones as checkpoints to make sure you're going in the right direction, then send us out of the cemetary to the treeline. Some graveyards (but fewer cemetaries) have little prayer/meditation gardens for visiters to use. Of course, this requires stone walls, treelines and little gardens. I recall some fairly stark cemetaries in Oklahoma...

Dave
