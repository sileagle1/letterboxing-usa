From: Mary from Virginia <daughteroftheolddominion@yahoo.com> (daughteroftheolddominion@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-02-26 16:43:53 UTC
I say go with a 43 stamp series. Why not? Some of the responders
have opined that 43 stamps is too much for one park (unless you live
in CT then it's just about right! teehee) But I wouldn't assume that
all of them would be planted in one park - why should they be? I've
seen series spread out in different parks. And I don't think you
should hold up planting until all the stamps are done. Plant as you
go along. It would be like the state quarters - they are being issued
over about 10 years.

I think you should keep it as a series of all the presidents. You can
plant them whenever you finish carving them and have a spot in mind

hidden in the Columbia River Gorge in Washington and Oregon! He has

My preference is that a series be posted by the area where the box
(es) are located.

Usually I decide on where I would like to go for a walk on a day,
and I look on the web site for boxes that are in that park or
recreation area. If you post together on one page all the boxes
that can be reached by walking from one parking lot, that would be a
good way to organize them.

Another way would be to post together on one page all the boxes that
are within one town. That way a boxer could take a driving tour of
the town to look for part of a series of boxes.

Congratulations on going for a long series of boxes. I will be out
searching for boxes again after snow melt.
