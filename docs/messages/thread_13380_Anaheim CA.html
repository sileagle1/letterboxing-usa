Autumn,
While in Anaheim, if the freeways are clear, you wouldn't be 45
minutes from some letterboxes north of you in the Los Angeles area.
I have three Eaton Canyon LBs planted in Altadena, just north of
Pasadena, which are freeway close, and listed on the letterboxing.org
clues list for Los Angeles. There are two other letterboxes in Eaton
Canyon, so you might get five stamps for one hike. My three
letterboxes in the canyon are about a 1.5 mile trek, and will require
you to negotiate some short but steep hills, but if you are able,
it's a nice outing. Besides mine, there are two LBs in the Los
Angeles Arboretum in the city of Arcadia (5$ entrance fee; one was
reported still there as of two weeks ago), three LBs in Descanso
Gardens, in the city of La Canada Flintridge (5$ entrance fee), and
some free treks in Whittier, planted by Jam n Jelly, that will net
you a bunch of LB stamps. Just check out the Los Angeles letterbox
listings in the LBNA Website.
--Buzzard
