Owwww!
Hi, Went letterboxing with my two kids yesterday. We still have a
very little bit of snow here. Had my son in a carrier on my back and
a hold of my daughters hand. The trail was pretty dry, but there
were a few icy patches. I kept telling my daughter to hold tight to
my hand and be careful of the ice patches. Well, I was trying to
avoid them, but alas I stepped on a small one and went down. My left
leg went behind me and I landed kind of sideways. The bottom of the
pack held me up so that I didn't break my leg. Both kids are just
fine. I called my husband and he asked me if he needed to come and
get me (he was at work). I told him let me try to stand up. I was
able to get up and very carefully limp my way back to the car, about
a half mile away. Went to the ER and I avoided telling them about
letterboxing. Just explained I was hiking. I sprained my ankle and
am currently icing my ankle. Can I get this PLB?

I actually got nominated and AYED! twice.

1. Going out to confirm that a friend's letterbox really, really,
really, REALLY was missing (Pirates Past Noon, The Treasure, RI), I
stepped barefoot on a large buried oyster shell that was wedged in
the sand at a 90 degree angle to my foot. It lodged into my foot so
deep that when I lifted my foot, the oyster shell was *still* stuck
into my foot! After I yanked the shell out of my foot, I had to go
back along the rather long trail to my car - at which point I knew I
needed medical attention, and had to say to myself "what would
MacGyver do?". I whipped out a bottle of Windex to wash and
disinfect my foot, then wrapped duct tape around it to keep it
covered. Then I went after another letterbox in the same park. It
healed just fine, but I do have a wicked scar.

2. Hunting down boxes in Hopkinton State Park, MA (Danielle's 15th),
yes, that was me, covered in yellow jackets, screaming and ripping
off my clothes. Although I was welted up for some time, I only have
about 5 nasty scars from the 20+ stings I received.

That's why I was wondering if the Order of the Purple Heart PLB was
an urban myth. It never came my way either time.
