Stamp Retirement Relocation Program

From: Frank-Kate Smith-Holmes (pyratemates@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-12-07 16:59:47 UTC-08:00
Winter is now upon most of the country. The snowbirds
are starting to flock and migrate toward us here in
the "Sunshine State".

Why should they have all the fun. If you (or anyone
else you know) have any stamps that would like to
retire to the "Sunshine State" just send them along
(carvers will be acknowledged as such) for planting by
us. There is plenty of room for starting a retirement
relocation program here. We have many locations
available for these tired, retired postal, elderly and
arthritic stamps that wish to retire and move south to
enjoy life in Florida and have locals as well as
tourists stop by for a visit.

Just contact us for information as to where to send
the retirees.

Merry Christmas to all
Frank and Kate your,

Re: [LbNA] Stamp Retirement Relocation Program

From: Frank-Kate Smith-Holmes (pyratemates@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-12-11 09:04:48 UTC-08:00
Of course they will. It is the only way to get here.
Today is nice and balmy with temps in the lower 70's.
Winter only comes here in waves lasting no more than 3
days at a time and only for a few hours of each of
those days. Hurricanes are no problem because the
homes for the retirees are checked during the season.
If an impending storm seems likely then the poor souls
are moved to a more secure location until the danger
is past.

So if you wish to send along your tired, re-tired,
arthritic, postal and/or other type stamps just ask
for our address (off list from your own email) and we
will comply. No container necessary as we will
provide. Logbooks will also be provided if you wish,
or you can send your own. Please include any story
about the stamp, it's travels, problems, history, etc.
This information will be included in the clue
information to peak others interest in visiting the
retiree.

Frank and Kate your,
Pyratemates
