lletterbox clue

From: jmastroe <jmastroe@yahoo.com> (jmastroe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-01-14 00:33:44 UTC
Start at the intersection of Belle Terre Parkway and RT 100.
Turn in the direction of the Atlantic Ocean and the rising morning
sun.
Travel 4.1 miles on RT 100(East) passing the base of the high flying
machines on your right and further along, the pink home of the angels
of mercy on your left.
At Colbert Lane, turn left and proceed 3.1 miles.You will know you
are headed in the correct direction when you pass the two giant
concrete sentinels on your right and Graham Swamp further on your
left. After passing the new Palm Coast Plantation turn right at the
unmarked street, but identified by the Herschel King Park sign
opposite the turn. Venture on to the park entraance, enjoying the
huge water fountains on on your right.
Park anywhere in the lot to the left. Follow the sidewalk to the
immediate right of the boat launch for the intercoastal waterway.
Stay on the walk past the pavilion and restrooms to where a crushed
seashell path turns off to the right. Take that path, veering to the
left, continue until you see a paved road to your left. Look to your
right, you will see an extremely large tree with a short stumpy palm
tree right next to it. The box lies behind the trunks; it is covered
by two long stemmed palm branches. To play it safe, in case
something has decided to keep the box company, carefully pick up
the branches by the stem end. While you are in the park you may want
to ennjoy 1.) the walk out to the edge of the intercoastal waterway
and 2.) the trail at the far end of the parking lot where you parked
your car.
