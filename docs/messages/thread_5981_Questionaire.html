Any time you get a file, Make sure you know the person that it's from.

From: arlen fletcher (arlenf@earthlink.net) | Date: 2002-07-04 07:51:13 UTC-07:00
Hi Lisa,

Best thing to do is buy an anti-virus program - McAfee, or Norton, or
TrendMicro all make them. Then, make sure you set it up to automatically
get updated virus signature files weekly - or even more often. Finally, as
coolwan said, if you get an e-mail w/attachment that you weren't expecting -
don't open it! Call or e-mail the person that sent it (if you know them)
and ask if they sent the e-mail w/attachment. If not, delete the e-mail and
move on.

Regards,

HangGlider
