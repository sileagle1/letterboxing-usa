Hi,
 
This one is probably short-lived, because it's in a very public park and not very hidden.   But, the stamp didn't turn out as well as I'd hoped either, so... Hey, it was an excuse to explore a park that I hadn't been to before! *sheepish grin*
 
Anyway, I planted a letterbox at Forest River Park in Salem, MA.  It's the first of a series that I'd like to do, commemorating the men and women who were accused during the Salem "Witch" Trial hysteria.  (My husband's ancestors were on both sides of the gallows.) 
 
For the very easy clues, see my webpage:
http://www.aisling.net/lb/MA/salem.htm
 
(If you can't find it, then it's already missing.  I'll find a better hiding spot for the next one.  Really.)
 
This is in a park with gorgeous water views, a small beach, swimming pools, picnic tables, multi-laned kiddie slides, swing sets, and there were plenty of pets on leashes there, too.  And, although the sign says "no charcoal fires," the BBQs were cooking anyway.  Oh, the aromas!  (Yes, I drove directly to Brothers' Deli in Salem after hiking, I was that hungry for some shishkebab! *grin*)
 
Forest River Park is right next to the living history museum, the Salem 1630 Pioneer Village. 
 
Cheerfully,
Ais
