Re: carving boo boo

From: StarSaels (steves_1701@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-17 04:34:12 UTC

Hi folks, and welcome to this edition of This Old Stamp. I was
carving a rather complicated stamp earlier tonight after reading
some posts on repairing stamps. I thought about having to repair the
stamp if I messed it up. And...

...perhaps an approach similar to that of repairing drywall might
work...

When you patch drywall, you remove a (preferably square) section of
material that includes good, bad, and ugly. You replace that with a
good square cut to an exact fit. A little compound, some tape if
it's a large patch, sand a little, some primer and a coat of paint.
Good as new!

If had to repair this stamp, I might ink a square area around the
trouble spot and stamp that directly onto a fresh piece of rubber.
After carving, I might trim it to size (including reducing its depth
to just a few millimeters) ink the back (uncarved) side of the
patch, and stamp that onto the stamp in the proper place. Then I
might carve out that freshly-inked square area so that the patch fit
snugly and its surface was flush with the original surface. A bit of
glue meant for rubber should hold the patch in place for a long time.

That's all the time we have for now. See you next time!

SteveS

From: Brian, Ryan & Lori (teamgreendragon2003@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-17 06:01:39 UTC

I have repaired a bad section of stamp before. I being the anal
person I am (can i say that here?)found error in the POE gathering
event stamp that I carved. I was able to re-attach a small (I'm
talking a piece maybe 1/32 by 1/32) back onto the stamp with some
rubber cement and a pair of fine tweezers. With fingers crossed I
watched it make it through the gathering and then some. I think it
has been retired since then but still I believe that repairs can be
made. I used the rubber cement that comes in the can with a brush
attached to the lid. I aplied it with a toothpick to both sides let
them dry a bit and then re-attached the piece with the aid of a
magnifying glass. I doubt that even if I hadn't made the repair
anybody would have noticed, but I know I would have.
