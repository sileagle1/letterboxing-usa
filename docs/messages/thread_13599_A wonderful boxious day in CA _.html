A wonderful boxious day in CA !

From: (Doublesaj@aol.com) | Date: 2003-06-15 22:24:48 UTC-04:00
Finally had the opportunity to spend 1 1/2 days in the San Luis Obispo
area and came away with 22 boxes for my efforts almost doubling my pre-visit
stash!! As a bonus, I was able to spend time with my aunt I hadn't seen in
many years who, of course, was able to help me with many of the locations of the
boxes. She was with me every step of the way too! My wonderful cousin and her
outdoorsey boyfriend caught on and insisted on going out until almost dark to
find 4 boxes with me and I am happy to say, THEY ARE HOOKED on this great
hobby! So don't be surprised if a whole lot more boxes start showing up in the
Letterboxing Capital of California!!

Here are the boxes I successfully found:
Gandalf the Wizard, Cambria-A Wizard indeed!!
Citizen Kane Series, San Simeon-2 out of 3 as there were picnickers too close
for comfort.
Sweet Springs, Los Osos-almost gave up as the trunk has so very many nooks
and crannies!
Shady Coyote--Such a beautiful bluff trail--full of wildflowers! Lest you're
tempted to give up on this nearly impossible find, DO NOT! It's THERE!!
Alaskan Series in San Luis Obispo--Alive and well (one only 1 block from my
aunt's house!
Mill Street,SLO--Beautiful bi-colored stamp! Placer includes 2 colors of ink
in the box!
Burial Mounds of Edoras, SLO--You'll swear it's not there but such a clever
hiding place!
Night and Day SLO==amazing stamp!
Pandora's Native American Bear--Cute and cuddly
Night Train--Woooooo--Woooooo
Mr. Chong's Candy--So Sweet!
Smaug's Mountain--Baby slugs abound in this wet box. Helped it out as best I
could though
The Bookstore Box--as easy and fulfilling as the Walking Tour box was
difficult!
Walking Tour Box--wins the prize for cleverness. The most fulfilling find
EVER!! You really WORK for this one up to the very end!!
Onion Tree--had to go back 4 times as there was a wedding in progress
Sunflash the Mace--also only a couple of blocks from my Aunt's house!
Los Osos Oaks Series--Without a doubt, THE most hauntingly beautiful
place you'll ever want to play with fairies and climb trees. Don't miss this
series of boxes even if one is supposedly missing. . .

Also found 3 of the 4 Chaos stamps Pandora set loose. I suspect the fourth
was in a box we spent many hours trying to figure out and came away frustrated.
The RUN FOR THE BORDER box, aka Taco Bell box was just too hard for us. But,
we had a lot of TBell food that day!
Another failure was the Moonstone Beach box which seems to have gone missing.
Rocks were there but I think the squirrels got it.

I also managed to place a new box near Santa Cruz. Will write clues soon!!

~~Doublesaj & Old Blue~~
