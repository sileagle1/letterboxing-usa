Poorly Rehidden Boxes - "are you ready to listen"

From: sue_sojourner (sldp@optonline.net) | Date: 2006-08-10 14:44:03 UTC
This morning I read with dismay Mark Pepe's article, "Are You Ready
to Listen" that shows a photo of a so-called "rehidden" Letterbox
here in CT. For those who didn't see it, check the link below...

http://markandsuepepe.blogspot.com/

This is a peeve of mine that really burns me - people (and I don't
refer to them as Letterboxers, as they haven't earned that title)
who don't take the time or effort to rehide 'boxes properly! This
subject comes up constantly and I truly believe that these posts are
never even read by the culprits because they don't even subscribe to
the lists let alone take the time or effort to LEARN about
Letterboxing before venturing out all gung-ho to look for 'boxes.

Very often I've found my own 'boxes poorly rehidden (never as badly
as in the photo though), and when I look online to see if the last
person who signed into the logbook (presumably the one(s) who rehid
them so badly) is a member of Letterboxing.org or a Yahoo group, 99%
of the time they are NOT. There's no way of contacting them to
explain the proper way to rehide a Letterbox!

SO THE POINT OF MY POST is to make a suggestion (it may have already
been suggested, and I apologize for not having read it before).
When accessing Letterboxing.org for clues, it is open for all to
read and obtain clues. Perhaps if everyone has to "register" with a
Trailname and Password as well as e-mail address in order to access
the clues, then there would be a record of everyone who is out there
looking for our Letterboxes. If all these names were in a "members"
list with e-mail access, then we could contact them about their poor
habits. (Of course, this totally depends on whether they sign the
logbook with the trailname that they registered with...)

Choi, is this possible? And Choi, THANK YOU for all the hard work
you continue to do for us as well as getting Letterboxing.org up and
running again!

sojourner - whose feathers don't often get ruffled ;-)

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-10 11:59:33 UTC-04:00
To build on that, and for some this may go without saying while for others it may be disagreeable, but often a poorly rehidden box is as much the fault of the placer as a [perhaps careless] finder.

If the box was not in a secure hiding spot in the first place, rather just sitting behind a tree with a pile of loose sticks and leaves covering it, the chances are greatly increased that animals and weather will contribute to the box being exposed. Not to mention a suspicious pile of sticks behind a tree is an invitation for a non-letterboxer to stop and investigate.

When attempting to *rehide* a box that was not hidden well to begin with or is located in a place that offers little in the way of a natural, secure hiding spot, you do the best you can, but there is only so much you can do with a pile of twigs and some leaves. On the other hand, if the box is tucked inside a hollow log or stump or a crevice in the rocks, etc. it is much easier to rehide because there is a natural hiding spot to work with.

I realize that there are many reasons that boxes end up laying out in the open -- vandals, animals, weather, muggles, careless letterboxers -- but I really feel the hiding spot itself is also a significant factor.

SpringChick

From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 16:08:48 UTC
> I realize that there are many reasons that boxes end up laying out
> in the open -- vandals, animals, weather, muggles, careless
> letterboxers -- but I really feel the hiding spot itself is also a
> significant factor.

I agree with SpringChick on this count. I'm not really convinced that
requiring people to log in will discourage people that do a lousy job
of replacing letterboxes. The hiding place that the planter selects
will make a *far* bigger impact for the longlevity of a box than the
finder's replacement skills will.

A good hiding place is idiot proofed. Animals can't push or move the
box from the hiding place. It doesn't require people to camoflauge the
box once it's replaced. It's planted on a location that if the box did
become exposed, it won't be in plain view of a trail or where people
will be. It's not planted at natural "stopping points" along a trail
such a beautiful view or scenic waterfall where people are more likely
to explore and discover the box.

If you want to get upset at those who don't replace letterboxes
properly, you also need to get upset at the people who don't *plant*
letterboxes properly as well. There's a lot of them out there, and the
scary part is, they're already signed up with accounts and have their
time, money, and efforts invested in the box already!

-- Ryan

Re: [LbNA] Poorly Rehidden Boxes - "are you ready to listen"

From: Rick Simpson (simpson.rick@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 10:27:28 UTC-07:00
I don't think requiring logins will address the problem for the very reasons
that other people have pointed out, and I won't even go into the whole
notion of LB cops.

But I do want to add that we all put a lot of time and energy into our
boxes, and we should realize that once you plant a box, you loose control.
It's no longer a personal keepsake. It's not on a shelf in your house.
Your box takes on a life of it's own. It's constantly interacting with the
elements, animals and other boxers. Things can and will go wrong, but
things also go wonderfully right most of the time.

The whole LB community is based on a gift of sharing with strangers, which
inherently has risk. There is no reward without the risk. All the "plans"
to restrict the chaos of LBing, (newbie requirements, official rules,
official counting rules, documented find levels before planting, etc.), will
do far more damage to the hobby than a few lost boxes.
