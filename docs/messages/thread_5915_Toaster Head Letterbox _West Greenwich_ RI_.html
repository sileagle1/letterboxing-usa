TOASTER HEAD
(West Greenwich, RI, USA)

Welcome to our first combined Letterbox/Geocache stash which was
planted on June 16, 2002.

The T.H. site has a distinct "off the beaten track" feel, but is only
a short, mile jaunt along (mostly) gently rolling, piney terrain
from where you'll park.

Get out your Hagstrom Map and find the intersection of New London
Turnpike and Congdon Mill Road in West Greenwich (it is accessible
from Route 3 (from the West) and Henry Brown Road (from the East),
both paved. It is also accessible from the South by following New
London Turnpike north from 102, but be prepared for a stretch of
dirt).

Once you're at the intersection of New London Turnpike and Congdon
Mill Road, drive approximately 1/10 mile Northwest on Congdon Mill
Road where you'll see the trail on your right leading into the
woods. Park your car. Head Northeast on this trail. After just a
few minutes, you'll reach a junction where you'll turn right (a
huge "climbing" pine sits smack dab in the middle of the
intersection). After turning right, walk another 10 15 minutes,
crossing a small bridge along the way, until you reach a "T" junction
in the trail where you'll meet Toaster Head.
