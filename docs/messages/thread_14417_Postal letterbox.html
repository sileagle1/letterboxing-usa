From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2003-07-29 20:51:15 UTC
There are a few letterboxes that travel from person to person by
mail, rather than hiding in the woods somewhere. Folks that receive
any of these boxes post here to let folks know the whereabouts of the
box.

There are two boxes currently - the Letterbox that finds you, and the
Snail Mail letterbox. The original box was called "Flutterby" but I
don't know if that box is still fluttering around or not. There are
also event specific postal boxes like the Purple Heart (mailed to
someone injured in the course of boxing) for example. You'll see
them posted about.

Hope that answers you! :)

From: Rayvenhaus (rayvenhaus@myndworx.com) | Date: 2003-12-09 12:22:05 UTC-08:00
I sent out Aztec Gold with pre-made mailing labels using my address as the
return address and a list that showed each person and who's next. According
to the USPS, UPS and FedEx, if they are unable to deliver it to the address,
they are to return it to the return address. I didn't think about putting
"Return Postage Guaranteed" though I'd be happy to receive it back if it is
undeliverable.

Aztec Gold has made it to it's first recipient and is off to the next on the
list, details are available at the website.
