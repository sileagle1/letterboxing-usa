From: hoopoe4me <scribenbirder@msn.com> (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-02-26 01:48:57 UTC
I agree www.enature.com is one of the best on line resources for
plants trees birds and all sorts of critters. If the plant is a non-
native you might have some challenge.

Google searches often turn up pictures on the "Images" page.

I like the Audubon regional guides as well . They are handy and just
right for the car.

For the Western half of Oregon and Washington I favor "Cascade
Olympic Natural History" by Daniel Mathews. It doesn't get much
better than this handy book.

For plant intensive book Plants of the Pacific Northwest Coast (
includes BC and lower Alaska) By Pojar. All of Pojars books are
impressive. There is one for the dry side that is on my wish list.
