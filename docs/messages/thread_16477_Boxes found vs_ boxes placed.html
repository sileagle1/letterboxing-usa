From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2003-10-20 17:21:38 UTC
I think the 'ideal' number of boxes planted for each box found
depends on where you live and the number of letterboxers in the
area. In the poll, I chose as the ideal, 1 box placed for each 4 you
find, but if I (or anyone else) followed that rule in my area, we'd
have almost no letterboxes. But if every letterboxer followed that
4:1 rule in, say, CT, there would be a serious, almost hazardous
glut of boxes! LOL!

And as to the other poll about how many boxes you have placed vs.
how many boxes you have found, I chose the one closest to my exact
numbers.... 1:1. But in reality, I have placed over 2 for each box I
have found. Really, it all depends on what your situation is in your
own locality, I think.

Happy Letterboxing!
Busy Bee (Wanda) P24 F10 (who will probably have another 3 boxes
planted before the week is over!)

We think you will see a lot of folks out west with more plants than
finds. Those of us here in Conn have a laundry list of "someday when
we have time we'll have to go find....". We'd also like to see the
PFX # reflected in the poll...meaning someone who has 1000 finds
might have a different ratio than someone with 50 finds.

Obviously, the higher your PFX, the more "accurate" your ratio will
be in that you will have shown some endurance to the sport -- the
same way that major league baseball has a minimum number of at-bats
to determine leading batting average. Not that this is a race or a
percentage war, but the theory behind the averages is that it shows
consistency!

What does this all mean? NOTHING! Some folks plant 1:1, some might
not. Either way, THANK YOU to everyone who plants boxes!!! Without
you, there'd be nothing out there to go find!

-Steve and Heidi
