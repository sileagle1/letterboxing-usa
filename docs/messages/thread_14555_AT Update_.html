Watch out Connecticut, I've almost arrived! I'm in New York now, already crossed the Hudson River, and should be arriving in Connecticut in another day or two!

Beth--I don't have your e-mail address. Please e-mail me if you still want to meet! How is the gerbil doing? Can I still pay my last respects?

If any of you other Connecticut Yankees want to meet, be sure to e-mail me. I'll be happy to try to figure out a time and place we can all try to meet at some point in the next week! =)

At the moment, I'm planning on passing through Kent on the 28th and passing by Cornwall Bridge on the 29th.

There are only about 50 miles of AT trail in Connecticut, so I won't be around for long!

Then it's into Massachusetts for 90 miles or so. A bit longer than Connecticut, but still, I should be through that state in less than a week too!

-- Ryan

From: (RiskyNil@pocketmail.com) | Date: 2003-09-02 16:01:21 UTC-07:00
For those of you following my travels, I am now in Gorham, New Hampshire, a few miles from the Maine border and less than 300 miles of white blazes left to follow. =)

It seems Internet access is getting harder to acquire, so I fear I will not have a chance to post more of my trail adventures until after I actually finish--perhaps in late September. I'll try to get in one more before I finish the trail, but no promises.

For you letterboxers out there, the White Mountains are simply incredible! Get out there before the snows start! =) Not recommended for children and people with weak hearts, however. Those trails kicked my butt! Haven't they ever heard of SWITCHBACKS in this part of the country?!

Carpe diem!
