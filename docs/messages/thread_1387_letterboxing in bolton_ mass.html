Re: letterboxing in bolton, mass

From: Jay Drew (drewclan@aol.com) | Date: 2000-10-16 21:16:00 UTC
Thanks for posting the url, Betsey! Worked perfectly. We had the luck
to find a couple of the Bolton letterboxes too (Powderhouse Hill and
the one about the "only child"). They were great: fun, historic,
scenic, and the stamps were nicely handcut. Some are puzzlers for the
mystery-lovers, and others require a real dose of local knowledge.
Also, they aren't very far from Betsey's Pisgah Fledgling letterbox,
which we HIGHLY recommend.

I've written a couple of times asking if they minded if we linked
them onto the Massachusetts map, but haven't gotten a response. Think
I'll try again with this little reminder. Makes me wonder how many
other letterbox series are out there that we could add in to the LBNA
website.

Enjoy! Jay

The Bolton link has been on the faq for a while at
http://www.letterboxing.org/faq.html#016

I try to keep links to all known NA letterboxing sites there.
I'm sure many of us know about all these sites, but if you're looking
you can check there, and if you know of the many that aren't there,
drop me or the list a note. Also, many are redundant to sites whose
clues can be accessed directly from the maps, but some are not.
