I'm typing my long-winded post for the second time, as after about 4
paragraphs my one year old just walked up and hit the escape key
deleting the entire post! ARGH!!!
Had a wonderful day in Yellow Springs today. It is a lovely town
with great opportunities for letterboxing. John Bryan is a pretty
area.

We found Flyfisher's proto-tree frogs: 2/3 but for the life of us
could not locate number 2, the black cocoon. We spent a lot of time
trying but to no avail. Found Horace Mann and Covered Bridge.
Franzsolo's Trailside box was also found today. :)Bravo on your
stamps gentlemen, they are lovely.

We enjoyed the hikes, especially the Tree Frogs, even without rain
there was some nice water flowing and it was a relaxing way to
disconnect today. The only sober note was an incredibly low-flying
fighter jet this afternoon.( I imagine due to the proximity of Wright-
Patterson)

Can't wait to get back there to find the other boxes!
