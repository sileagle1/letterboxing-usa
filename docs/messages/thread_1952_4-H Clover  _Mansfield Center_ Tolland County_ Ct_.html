4-H Clover ,Mansfield Center, Tolland County, Ct.

From: Carolyn Stearns (cstearns@neca.com) | Date: 2001-02-19 22:14:46 UTC-05:00
Rated: Medium a 2 hour walk mostly down hill.
 
Planted: Feb. 19,2001 Presidents Day
 
By: Cock-A-Doodle-Moo 4-H Club
 
The 4-H Clover Box had its first adventure at the Winter Gathering  and has many wonderful stamps from that outing. Now it has a permanent home here on Mansfields Nipmuck.
 
This makes this piece of Nipmuck trail a threesome as follows:
 
Folow clues to Wolf Rock box as posted
After   that box ,continue down the trail
 along a well travelled and clear path the walk takes you down hill to Sawmill Brook. Here trip along the stone crossing carefully . After the brook look for an Arrow denoting the turn in the trail, face this arrow and take a compass reading of 260  drgrees. the gnarled twisted cyclops guards the  4-H Clover box you seek.
   If you would like to pursue Fuzzy Wuzzy follow the trail down through and across the power line. Enter the woods cross a couple of wet areas then when you come to a large stone chair on the right backtrack with the posted Fuzzy Wuzzy clues from that point.  After the gather of the  third box  turn around and the trail takes you all the way down to Puddin La. A car can be left at the big rock there to ferry you back to Crane Hill and the Wolf Rock parking area.
   If a one car adventure, do  the first two boxes and turn back, drive to Puddin La. and hike up to Fuzzy Wuzzy.
