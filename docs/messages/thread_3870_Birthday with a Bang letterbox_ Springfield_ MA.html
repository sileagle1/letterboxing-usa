Birthday with a Bang letterbox, Springfield, MA

From: Debbie Varrell (livnletlrn@juno.com) | Date: 2001-12-04 09:22:16 UTC-08:00
A few days ago we celebrated my son, Jesse's, 7th birthday. He loves
history and weaponry, and chose to spend his special day exploring the
Springfield Armory in Springfield, MA. While there, we placed our first
letterbox.

Birthday with a Bang letterbox
placed November 29, 2001, at the Springfield Armory
Springfield, MA
Easy, less than 1/2 hour

The armory is free, with a donation box near the door. Half of it is
exhibits showing the history of the weapons made there, and the other
half is exhibits about the industrial revolution as it pertains to
firearms production. Even for a peacemonger like me, it's very
interesting! My favorite display was the black powder rifle hit by
lightning, leaving the barrel in a spiral formation!

Travel northwest around armory.
Continue counterclockwise around paved circle toward the Magnificent
Mansion.
Pass two bright sentries on your right.
Line up as if to lead the sentries marching down the hill.
At 250 degrees, a medium size tree blocks your view of a grand tree 2/3
of the way down the hill.
Birthday with a Bang letterbox is cozy in the backside of the base of
this grand tree.

- debbie in CT
P 1, F about 30
