Woe is me! Why can't the snow just go AWAY!

From: (mindizney@aol.com) | Date: 2003-02-17 07:44:49 UTC-05:00
OK.... just when I thought that the winter was winding down, it decides to snow.... and not just 3-6 inches, which I could handle, but 20-30 inches!  I was SO hoping to get 500 boxes by my first anniversary (which is May 23rd) but, with 30 inches of snow topped onto the already six inches of hard glacial ice, I may not see grass till August!

And to top it all off, Washington DC is getting 18 or so inches before its said and done, so there goes LBing in the nations capital later this week!  Those of you who live and box in the DC area, would you please keep me posted on the boxing conditions this week?  I know its supposed to get warmer as the week goes on, but will some of the snow go away so it will be conducive to letterboxing?  Are there any boxes (such as Chinatown, or National Geographic) that I will be able to find regardless of the snow?  (I know I'll be able to find the Smithsonian one..... ;-)

To make matters worse, I had three new boxes to plant in the Maryland/DC area, which I will be unable to plant now that it is snowing 18 inches there!  

Keep me posted.... thanks!

Music Woman
in snowy CT (ugh....)

From: Pam McCarthy (lakeannalady@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-02-17 09:14:44 UTC-05:00
Hi Music Woman,
 
I just went letterboxing last week in that area.  Even with snow (gotta be willing to wear many layers of clothes) we found a few (Watson Mystery Box Series, Buddy Attick Park, Maydale Nature Center).  If you're staying in the DC area (after the blizzard clears) you shouldn't have too many problems; public transportation should be up and running again.
 
I live in central Virginia and we got 4 inches of snow with a few inches of ice on top of that.  Thankfully that has blown (literally) by us for now.  Four wheel drives are doing okay on the back roads.  Its when you get on the main roads that are a problem.  Some folks just don't know how to drive in snow and should stay home.
 
We will be doing some more of the *in town* (Chinatown, Smithsonian, etc.) in a little while.  Trouble is we love to walk rather than drive and park!
 
Lake Anna Lady
loving life
love being snowed in!
