The city of Philadelphia has more park land within its borders than any
other city in the world. One of its well known treasures is the
Wissahickon Park This huge park stretches for miles from the confluence
of the Wissahickon Creek with the Schulykill River at its southern end
to the northwest border of the city. The park gets over a million
visitors a year, many of whom find it a spiritual respite from the
stress of urban living. The Wissahickon is a fine place to visit during
all seasons and in any weather.

The following is the first in a series of boxes to be placed in the
park.
