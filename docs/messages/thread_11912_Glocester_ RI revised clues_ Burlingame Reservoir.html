From: curiouscrane (curiouscrane@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-04-13 23:23:59 UTC
The old wooden footbridge that lead to the letterbox has recently
been removed. We saw it at another stream crossing, probably moved
there by people who wanted to get 4-wheel ATVs across. The old bridge
was kind of scary to cross anyway, so we are changing the clues a
little bit to make the approach to the letterbox safer and easier, if
less exciting. Driving and parking directions are the same as before.

New Clues:
Walk down the dirt road past a hemlock grove on the left. In time you
will start ascending a small hill. You will notice the old confields
on the right. Listen for the sound of the waterfall on your left. The
only telephone pole that has spikes used for climbing protruding out
of either side is your first stopping place. At 210 degrees you will
spot an old maple tree with one large dead limb. Walk to the tree,
and then take several steps to the left and forward to the part of
the stream at the top of the waterfall. Look across the stream here
and spot the stone wall whose southwest corner contains the
letterbox. Remember what things look like from this side of the
stream and imagine how it would look from the other side.

Go back to the dirt road, turn left, and continue up the road to the
next intersection. Turn left and cross one span of a paired I-beam
bridge, which provides good footing. Take a quick left to follow the
stream back to the waterfall, the stone wall, and the letterbox.

If you want to see the old Burlingame Reservoir itself, get back on
the main dirt road and go left at the next fork.

Latte Man and Curious Crane
