Lucy,
I think you may have unintentionally misled the man you met based on your own misunderstanding. Geocachers "don't disturb the earth" any more than letterboxers do. The two hobbies are extremely similar. The content of the boxes is usually different (though not always) and the clues to finding them are often different but _the_way_the_boxes_are_hidden_is_identical_.
The way people search for the box once they get to the general area of the box is also identical.
Geocachers are not a shovel brigade carelessly digging up the woods. They don't turn over more rocks than letterboxers. They don't do anything substantially different than what you do.

There are simply more of them.

The same rules of etiquette are followed by both groups.
The same issues of concern that the National Park Service has with regard to one apply to the other. Both are prohibited in National Parks.
If anything, geocache locations can be more closely observed/approved/regulated by Park Rangers than letterboxes because of the universality of the clue format (lat./lon.).
I personally like the varying levels of difficulty and obscurity that letterbox clues allow and the artistic aspect of stamping but thought I'd put in my 2 cents regarding the misperception of geocachers as enviromental heathens.

-Eoghan

Lucy,

I am with Eoghan here.  I originally started out letterboxing and have since incorporated geocaching as well.  They ARE basically the same deal,  just a different means of finding the boxes. Why do you have such a bad feeling about something that is so similar?
