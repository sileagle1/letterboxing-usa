From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-09-21 14:10:07 UTC-06:00
Ok, I am going to pose this question to the group and hope I don't get a
mailbox full of nasty emails.....

Would it be out of Etiquette to make a LB to honor someone that would
require a $5 (or more) donation?

Let me explain.

My neighbor's son (he is 2) has a very rare disease that has left him
completely disabled. Horse therapy (I can't remember the correct name)
is a wonderful thing for disabled kids. Thomas was able to go for 5
sessions this summer. However, their insurance company changed their
mind about covering this therapy and they were left with a $500 bill.
Thomas LOVED this therapy, and they saw a wonderful change in his motor
skills. But, having 4 children (10 yr old twins, a 5 year old and
Thomas who is 2) and only one income, they can't afford to continue
sending Thomas.

My thought would be to put a box at the center and ask for a mandatory
donation of at least $5.00 in order to stamp in. ALL of the money would
go towards the $100 sessions for Thomas.

Would you visit the box even if it meant making the donation?

Thank you all in advance for your input.

Just my thoughts...

I think you have a wonderful idea and a helpful heart. I think that
you would want to make sure to spell out the details in the clues.
I would support just such a cause. Here's some ideas that you may
want to consider. With all the letterboxes that go missing I would
recommend maybe asking the finders to send a check for the donated
amount. That way if a letterbox goes missing you don't lose the
ungathered cash. You may also want to make it a postal letterbox
with a little bank in it to place checks. I don't think it would be
a good idea to put cash in the mail or in a letterbox. I would even
hesitate to put a check in a letterbox. Just a thought.

Hope it does well if you do it. If you make it a postal please sign
me up.

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-21 14:32:02 UTC-07:00
I second what Cherokee said about leaving money in
boxes. Aside from the risk, you would probably wind
up with fewer donations because of the perceived risk
of loss. I probably wouldn't leave money in a box,
but I would probably have good intentions of donating.
Honestly, It would depend on how easy it is to donate
whether I would follow through on my good intentions.

One way to make it easy to donate and to protect your
personal information by not having to send folks your
or someone else's address would be to set up a PayPal
account. You could either post a clue page describing
the nature of the box and request that interested
boxers donate $5 to the PayPal account to get the
"real" clues, or you could post a request for donation
in the box with the PayPal account shown., and folks
could pay the $5 for the clue.

I think it's a neat idea - and what a way to help
someone else!

Lucy
