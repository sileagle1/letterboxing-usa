O Letterbox Tree & the Hanukkah Bush

From: cclightningbug (cclightningbug@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-11-23 22:39:51 UTC
We have received our first holiday stamps! They came all the way from
Seattle and are phenomenally carved by South Hill Hendersons and
Triple T. They represent Hanukkah traditions.

Since Hanukkah begins on December 5th this year, they will be
available from that date on in or under the Hanukkah Bush located
between the menorah and dreidel window ornaments and will remain in
place through January 1st. O Letterbox Tree clues will be posted on
December 10th and will remain letterbox-decorated through January 1st.

In case you missed the details in the original post:

2007 O Letterbox Tree & the Hanukkah Bush
The possibility of ocean-effect snow flurries are showing up in our
local weather forecasts, a sure sign that the time has come to think
about O Letterbox Tree & the Chanukah Bush once again. We decided to
give this year's series a theme, a way of getting to know each other
better: holiday traditions from our families or heritage. We will have
both our letterboxing Christmas tree decorated and our Chanukah bush
under the light-up dreidel and menorah ready on the weekend after
Thanksgiving.
