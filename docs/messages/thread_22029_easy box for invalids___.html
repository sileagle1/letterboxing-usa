From: lipsmom3 (lipsmom3@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-05-30 12:15:46 UTC
In CT there is a handicapp accessible one in Middlebury. It's
called Hop Brook Lake Handicapped accessible.

Also, if you try atlas quest you can use a "trip planner" that give
you some boxes [somewhat]on your route AND it has a picture key
that tell you info like if it's a driveby, child/pet friendly, hand
carved, handicapp accessible, compass needed, etc. I like using
it,especially when I"m heading somewhere I"ve never been and I need
some quick, easy, child-friendly, drive-bys.

I find it also let's you know what letterboxes will be near
(within ?? miles) of where you will be staying too.

Just my thougth/suggestion.
