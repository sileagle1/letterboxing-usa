I try to keep my stamps small enough to fit on a 3x5 index card. I use a
spiral bound 'book' of lined 3x5 cards from Wal-Mart as my log book. I just
flipped it over to use the unlined sides of the cards to stamp on :-) I can
use the lined (back) of the cards for notes too. I'm on my third 'book' for
finds/exchanges and have a separate 'book' for stamps I've carved.
I'm thinking that most people probably have log books at least 3"x5" in
size. I'm interested in hearing what others think though.

Stamp size is strictly a matter of personal preference. I have seen
signature stamps from the tiniest 1/2" square to nearly 3"x5". Same
for box stamps, with the largest I have encountered being the Cape
May Zoo box in NJ, which was close to 3"x5". A 2"x3" stamp is
probably on the larger side, but certainly not too large. The
primary consideration is the size of the log books you will be
stamping into.

A lot of the "average-sized" boxes I have been to, use a log book
that ranges from 3" square to about 3"x5". In this case, your 2"x3"
stamp would not have a problem, but it would more than likely be too
large for a full stamp in a microbox log. What a lot of folks do in
that case is just stamp one portion of their stamp, i.e. the
lettering or a significant part of the image, etc. Others carry a
smaller, alternate signature stamp for the smaller log books. You
can also write your comments on the back of the page if your stamp
fills the entire front side.
