From: Stephanie Kamrar (goldentuke@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-31 23:54:57 UTC-07:00
Hey there, nwbulldog -

And welcome to LbNA!!! :D I, too, am orignally from Bellingham - now transplanted to Arizona (Tucson, more specifically). What part of Bellingham are you in? My parents still have a house over by Whatcom Falls and there is a letterbox in there, which I have yet to find. Fellow Letterboxers have done a great job about the placement of letterboxes up in and around town. You know as well as anyone that you catch Larrabee at low tide, the walks on the beach are awesome, especially when you can see the starfish, and there are a few letterboxes out Larrabee way.

My best friend introduced me to Letterboxing and I wasn't sure how things should be done, either, but I am one of those people, too. I started making notes of the weather, impressions of the area I was in, etc. I would make similar notes in the log book that I stamped - granted, there was space available. My first log book was a tiny thing - I have now "graduated" to a sketch book and there is more room to write more thoughts. There is no right or wrong way to stamp your very own log book. That's the beauty of letterboxing is that you let creativity guide you. You make your log book your own. :)

Another great website that you may want to check out, if you haven't already done so, is www.atlasquest.com. Atlas Quest and LbNA - very smilar. And since you are so close to BC, you can always venture out and get those ones close by, like in Abbotsford, Langley, Aldergrove, etc.

Anytime you feel like conversing outside of here, you can always get me at this email address or you can find me under "AZJokester" on Atlas Quest.

Take care and have fun!! :D

~ AZJokester ~

From: Jars Of Clay (jarsofclaylb@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-01 11:19:14 UTC
you were absolutely right. whatever works for you. We (JARs of clay) always travel together but each have our own books and started with small journals (the 4x6) only to have realized we like journaling and space for the LARGER stamps that are definitely out there (the 4x6 ones!) so we now each have a 9x12 sketch book. I wouldn't absolutely count on the 4x6 book holding more than 2-3 stamps per page and know it will occasionally only fit one...there are stamps that large out there, not as many as the smaller ones but there definitely are. Ultimately it boils down to whatever you're willing to carry as you walk or hike or jump in and out of your car. Good luck and happy hunting!
