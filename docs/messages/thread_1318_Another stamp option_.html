From: Bruce Farmer (bruce_farmer@hotmail.com) | Date: 2000-10-05 12:31:56 UTC
I've got to admit that I've been most impressed with the personally carved
stamps, but it's a heavy weight off my shoulders to know that it's really OK
to use a commercial stamp. Might get my planned letterboxes planted quicker
now!

I'm wondering if anyone has any experience with iprint.com. Seems sort of
like a half-way way of doing things. Coming up with the design and
submitting it to iprint to make into a stamp would allow for a little
creativity anyway, with less of a focus on carving skill.

From: Linda Aplin (linda@auc.com) | Date: 2000-10-06 12:03:58 UTC-06:00
And if you find a nice image you want to transfer to a stamp, you can photocopy it, and transfer it off by placing the image face down on a stamp, and rub the back with nail polish. The image will transfer off onto the stamp, then you can carve it out.

Linda
