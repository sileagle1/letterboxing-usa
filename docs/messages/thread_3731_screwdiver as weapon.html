Ammo cases have handles firmly attached and require no
implements to open or seal. I wouldn't open some
mysterious container in the woods either, but if I
were following clues and found a stashed ammo case
where the clues said it would be, I'd open it. This
deterrent for people who accidentally stumble on
letterboxes may be a plus. In the end I agree with
the writer who uses different box types for different
locales. Tupperware in the city and steel out in the
boondocks.
