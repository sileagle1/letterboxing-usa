Wow! Bunches of new letterboxes! Here's another:

Postcard from Maine

Dear Mom & Dad,

Having a great time in South Portland, Maine! We found this terrific
shoreside walk that is perfect for both us and the kids. We have
beautiful views of Casco Bay and two lighthouses while the children
can explore an old fort and play on four different playgrounds! Here
are directions for when you come up next weekend to share the cottage
with us:

On I-95, take exit 6A (toll) and immediately take I-295 exit 1.
Follow it down to Rte. 1 and turn left on Rte. 1 North. Follow it 1.1
miles, past the fire station, and turn right on Broadway. Follow it
carefully to the end after 3.6 miles (don't miss the sharp right turn
near Wendy's). At the end of Broadway, turn right on Pickett St, then
left at a stop sign onto the SMTC campus. Follow this road to the end
and park at the museum.

We couldn't resist walking out the causeway to explore the lighthouse
and get a close up of the harbor. Then we took a gray wooden
staircase behind the museum up to the walkway. This wonderful trail
kept the bay to our left: we walked down on the beach and back home
on the shore. We walked down to the end of the campus and then
crossed the sandy crescent of Willard's Beach. At the far end, we
climbed the stairs and turned left out to the point. There are a
couple of photogenic fisherman's shanties. At the easternmost of the
fisherman's shacks, we hid a letterbox for you. It is tucked into the
foundation in the southeast corner of the shanty: please re-hide it
carefully in this busy and windy/wavy area. On the way home we had
the prevailing wind at our back and really came to understand what is
meant by "down east."

Wish you were here!

JayD in please-no-frost-yet CT
http://members.aol.com/drewclan/seacoast.htm#Postcard

Postcard from Maine

From: Bob Degen (legerdemaine@hotmail.com) | Date: 2001-08-07 04:01:47 UTC
Rumors of Thundahole's demise may be somewhat exaggerated.
 
Munus (a gift, also; public entertainment) apud (at) lacuna (hole, also; (figuratively), a void)
 
A Latin pun...
the back door which will save you a drive to West Quoddy Head. 
 
This will crosslink you serially to other legerdemaine boxes locally, which now enumerate somewhere between Tom Cooch and Jay Drew and his clansmen's totals.  As far as I know, they're all virgin as of this date.  Attempts at various means of allowing the clues to trickle out have not met with any success.  For the record, I prefer that they be disbursed  the old fashioned way, through word of mouth.  Please feel free to distribute any legerdemaine clues with those of our ilk off list.
 
Call this one Flatlander's Folly.  A tribute to the perscitus magister (clever master), Tom Cooch.
 
Tom Cooch's box has disappeared
taken by winter's waves
a flatlander's folly;
and thus he's endeared-
that stamp's in a watery grave
 
You'll note that the location, though high on the beach, is still in the inter-tidal zone.  I'll be clever enough to take this one up over the winter.
 
Here's one more, just to make sure no one goes home skunked.  Please (Perscitus Magister)post this one on the LBNA site, and also, please link it and West Quoddy Head to the lighthouse/letterbox site:
 
 
 
 
Bass Harbor Light
 
At the base of the steps, there's a fork in the track
Take the one that heads off toward the Ducks that don't quack
Where the trail disappears, there's a seat in a hole
Sit and search in the clefts for the requisite bowl
 
legerdemaine
 
 
This box will be taken in during the winter.
 
 
 
 
Have at 'em.  And remember to be safe and have fun.
