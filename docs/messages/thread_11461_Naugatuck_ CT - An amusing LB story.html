Naugatuck, CT - An amusing LB story

From: tempusfugitmd (donnas28@juno.com) | Date: 2003-03-25 20:03:05 UTC
After nearly a year of accompanying the kids and me on our
letterboxing hikes, my husband decided he needed a logbook and
personal stamp. I was happy to oblige. For the last 2 months "Dead
Duck Chuck" has been playing catch-up, sometimes revisiting boxes
we've been to so he can add his stamp. Today he was in Connecticut
for business and found the time to run over to the Naugatuck Cemetery
box where we had visited last fall. He quickly found the monument
where the letterbox is hidden. Just inside was a small gold cardboard
box held closed by a rubber band. This was not the usual letterbox
container, but sometimes replacements are made in a pinch to damaged
boxes, so...he opened the box. Whoa! Someone had "buried" his
deceased gerbil just above the letterbox! Better than finding a live
snake, I suppose, but still a bit unnerving. So, Beth and Mike, your
letterbox has company. And, no, Chuck did not ink up the gerbil and
stamp it into his log.

Donna
(aka Tempus Fugit)
