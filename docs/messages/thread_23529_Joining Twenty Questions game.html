I don't tend to follow the boards much, but I was made aware of the
Twenty Questions game going on here and decided to take a look ...

There is at least one letterbox already at Rocky Narrows in
Sherborn, so I've had occassion to traverse there (as others likely
have also).

Rocky Narrows is divided into two distinct sections that are
seperated by a set of railroad tracks. Officially, it is prohibited
to cross the tracks (although a trail does seem to do exactly that).

It's actually not that large of a tract of land, but hard to narrow
the location down with one remaining question. Sure wish this was
25 questions instead!

Anyway, I think Boston Rott has the right idea by trying to
coordinate the Trustees' intersection referencing system with a map
of stone walls.

As Blax points out, part of Rocky Narrows is also owned by the town
of Sherborn.

A wild guess, but I'd be inclined to say that this box is likely on
the northern side of the tracks (intersections 8 and up).

I concur that Boston Rott should ask the last question ... as with
one question left what better chance do we have then to try to
narrow down near which intersection the box happens to be on?

Fire away!
