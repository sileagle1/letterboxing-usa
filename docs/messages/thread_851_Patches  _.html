I think asking people to commit to something that they haven't seen is too
much. I mean, I'd like to get a patch or something, but I'd like to know what
it looks like first. But, I also think that there's nothing wrong with
stickers. I have tons of stickers for outdoor gear companies and clubs. I
put them on my water bottles, my mountain bike helmet, on everything. It's a
great way to get outdoors people to notice something, and it's less expensive
and easier to do. Especially for something that's just an "I'm a
letterboxer". I'd get a ton of those, just to stick them everywhere :)
But for recognition, a sticker isn't really feasible. But I think there's too
many aspects of recognition, and at this rate, everyone is going to have there
own patch. I'm sure everyone puts a lot of effort into their clues and
stamps, and even picking a perfect spot. Hey, I'm working with a handicap
here, I'm in New Jersey! How about I get a patch for putting a box where you
can't see or smell a landfill! I mean, every box I look for out of this state
is in a beautiful location. I think these things are too open to
interpretation. I mean, if you like someone's stamp or clue, why don't you
just say thanks for a great hunt in a great spot? That's recognition. I only
have one patch, and it's for volunteering for trails. I've never put it on
anything, I don't want it to get wrecked. But stickers, there's always more
stickers :) And if you don't want people in the mall noticing letterboxing
(which I'm not sure why, unless you guys get people from Staten Island at your
mall too, then I can understand), then stick it on something you use outside,
ie. a trekking poles, water bottles, dog packs.
So, in short :) I'm for stickers.
Oh, and for membership. I don't think we should have a membership fee to get
a patch. I mean, that's all we're gaining. We can still look for boxes, and
only have to pay park fees :)
