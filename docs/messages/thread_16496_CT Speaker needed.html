At my Monday night basketball game a friend, the local cub scout pack leader
who is much taller than I and who stuffs me regularly, asked me to give a
presentation on letterboxing. Much as I love the topic, I had to decline. I'm one
of those people with a pathologic fear of public speaking. For hours before a
presentation that steel band around my chest tightens and tightens until the
frantic beating of my heart threatens to explode. My vision tunnels to a haze
of gray and my ears go underwater until I want to throw up and pass out.
Running screaming from the room is not an effective speaker's technique.

So if anyone is willing to do a little cub scout project in southeastern CT,
please let me know and I'll pass the Toastmaster's (oops, Scoutmaster's) name
and number along.

Thanks, Jay in CT
