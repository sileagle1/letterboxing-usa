    Re: [LbNA] Letterboxing Frenzy

    From: fred lorch (lonemasswolf@covad.net) | Date: 2001-05-22 19:25:18 UTC-04:00
    We started out with brave hearts and valiant minds. On Thursday we did find
    "The Little Red School House" at School House Pond, RI; The "Little Bird
    House" and the "Little Bird" at Kimball Wildlife Preserve in RI; "Ninigret
    Pond" (3 miles round trip on the beach in the sand); "Bird House Heaven" in
    Moore Woods, RI; "Watch Hill Lighthouse" in RI; and "Wilcox Park" in RI. We
    also placed the "Point Judith Lighthouse". We saved the other boxes for
    another time, having run out of daylight.

    On Friday, we took the ferry from New London, CT to Orient Point, LI, NY. We
    found the "Orient Point" box but could not locate "Turtle Crossing" (it may
    be missing, we were pretty sure about the site, although we did not scavenge
    around alot due to the POISON IVY). While on Orient Point, we planted the
    Orient Point Light letterbox. We then drove to Montauk Point and planted the
    Montauk Point Lighthouse letterbox. Then we drove to Nesconset, to check on
    my land (I was born and raised on LI). Imagine my surprise to discover
    someone had cleared my land.

    Saturday was busy visiting friends and checking with the neighbors about my
    land. Still, we did find the letterbox on "Jayne's Hill" and "Whisper" as
    well.

    Sunday found us in Western Connecticut (had to discard plans for NY and NJ
    due to land complications). We hiked and found the three letterboxes along
    "Mile of Ledges", and the four boxes around "Lake Compounce". These two
    trails were great warm-ups for the next day. We ran out of daylight so we
    will get the Black Dog and Larry & Barb's Ledge at another time.

    Monday we climbed Mount Monadnock, up the Red Dot Trail, planted "Moon Over
    Pumpelly Pines", and finally found the "Sarcophagus".

    That concluded our little adventure. Some great hiking and wonderful
    letterboxes.

    The clues to our new ones will appear shortly.

    lonemasswolf
    p26 f151 x4
