From: csb_bc (csb_bc@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-10 03:06:03 UTC
I have been letterboxing since 2002, so I am by no means an "elder".
However, I could relate many trips and many hikes that have ended with
just a wonderful walk. The most rewarding letterbox was one that had
us returning 3 times! FINALLY we deciphered the clues! It doesn't
matter how many stamps I accumulate... that one will always be
special. The White Whale! I can say that I have seen places in B.C.,
(Vancouver Island), and Washington, that I would never have seen had
it not been for letterboxing.
CSB bc

my brother, who now boxes, lives in shreveport LA. while i was down there, i visited little rock, AK, houston, TX, and vicksburg, MS. with out letterboxing, i probably wouldn't have gone to any of those places. i have been to portland, ME many times, but never the places that letterboxing has taken me in portland. even in my home state of NH, i continue to visit places i never knew exsisted.

i couldn't agree more with the walk. when i first started letterboxing last year, i was hooked on the boxing aspect of the trip and forgot about the nature part. now i combine them both. i bring a camera everytime i box and always come home with some beautiful pictures.
