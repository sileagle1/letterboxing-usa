One More Box

Hillsboro,Ohio~Highland County
Difficulty: easy,short trail walk
Placed: January 22,2002 ~ by franzsolo...

Fort Hill State Memorial is a nature preserve containing one of the best
preserved Indian hilltop enclosures in North America. The Hopewell
Indians (100 B.C.-A.D. 500) constructed the 1 1/2 mile long earthwork
hilltop enclosure as well as at least two ceremonial buildings and
probably a village in the Brush creek Valley.
Lying at the western edge of the Allegheny Plateau, immediately south of
the glacial boundary, this hilly area contains an impressive diversity
of bedrock, soils, flora, and fauna. There are 11 miles of hiking trails
at the 1,200 acre preserve as well as a picnic area. The museum reopened
in the fall of 2000 after an extensive renovation. New exhibits include
a variety of hands-on and interactive, multimedia displays depicting the
geology, flora and fauna of Fort Hill..

LOCATION
Fort Hill is located off of State Route 41 on Township Road 256, five
miles north of Sinking Springs and three miles south of Cynthiana in
Highland County.

Bethel-New Hope Covered Bridge


New Hope, Ohio - Brown Co.
Difficulty: drive up
Placed: January 27, 2002 ~by franzsolo...

Spans White Oak Creek
A clear span of 170 feet.
The Bethel-New Hope Bridge is the longest covered bridge still extant in
Ohio. This bridge replaced an older one in 1878. There have been
major renovations in 1902, 1932, and in 1977.
The New Hope Bridge is approximately six miles north of Georgetown, Ohio
on Rt. 68. Turn right on the New Hope-Bethel Road (going west).
The bridge is immediately to your right. To get there from
Cincinnati, take the Bypass, Rt. 275, to the Batavia exit (Rt.
32). Drive east on Rt. 32 to Rt. 68. Turn south and drive to
New Hope. The bridge is south-west of the town on New Hope-Bethel
Road.


Letterbox clues:

At the N-E end of the bridge at 3 paces away there are two poles you
will see.
Behind the second pole is a large rock.The box is under the large rock
hidden by a smaller rock at the opening of its resting place.Remove the
small rock and you will see the covered bridge box .

Stonelick Lake

Clermont County ,Ohio
Difficulty : easy
Placed : 11 February, 2002 ~ by franzsolo...


Driving Directions :
Stonelick State Park is located in northern Clermont County near the
Villages of Edenton and Newtonsville. It can easily be accessed from
Interstate 275 via the Milford Parkway exit, State Route 131 and State
Route 727. The park consists of 1058 acres of land and 200 acres of
water.
FYI ~ You can obtain a Park map from the front door of the park office.

Parking: N 39 13.002 - W 84 03.487

Letterbox clues:
Start at the Camp Area parking lot.The Camp Area is at the end of Lake
Drive (which is the main road through the park.)
Proceed past the check - in office to the Southwoods Trail.Go in a
Southerly direction crossing over two foot bridges.At the second bridge
after crossing it.Take a bearing of 160.
Take about 25 paces and you will come upon a fallen cedar tree
approximately 60 foot long.It is lying at a NE-SW direction.You are
interested in the SW end of the tree.

Wetlands

Blue Ash Amphitheatre and Nature Park
Blue Ash, Hamilton County, Ohio
Difficulty - Easy - .5 miles on paved/wood chip trails.
Placed - 19-June-2002 by ~franzsolo...
motofranz@webtv.net

This microbox is a continuation of ~
Team Gray Wolf's
BARC 'n' Howl letterbox

~Please bring a pen,and stamp pad ~

You will find the Wetlands letterbox at the Blue Ash Recreation Center,
4433 Cooper Rd., Blue Ash.
The clues begin at the entrance to the Nature Park, found at the
Southwest Corner of the Rec Center's plot. The entrance is marked by a
sign on the right, with announcements above and a map below.
Enter the trail, and you will immediately come to a fork in the trail
marked by a big yellow PLEASE.
At this fork, stay right. You will pass a picnic pavilion on your left,
a playground and rest rooms on your right. Continue past the trail on
the right, just beyond the place where music can be heard on Summer
nights. Continue past the trails on both left and
right, marked by a plaque commemorating the establishment of the Nature
Park.
Go past dinosaurs on your left, and a rock wall, a police car, a Twin
Flyer, and the General Store on your right.
At the next picnic pavilion (on your left), the paved trail makes a
sharp turn to the right and down a hill.
There is a trail paved with wood chips dead ahead - take it.
There will be a trail on the left, opposite the horseshoe pits - Ignore
it!!
The trail moves ahead and curves slightly to the right. You will come to
a "T" intersection, where you can only go North or West. Take Horace
Greeley's advice to the "Young Man".
Down the path you have taken, you will find a circular clearing with a
small tree in the center, and a wooden bench on the far side.

Take a seat on the bench.....rest if you wish ...
Look to your Right...you will see a large tree on the right side of the
trail about 5 paces away.
(a pace =2 steps; about 5 feet )
~ Bonus ~
Look behind that tree (under some BARC ) and you might find :
Team Gray Wolf's BARC 'n'Howl L'box.

Now on to the Wetlands !

HP SQ.

Adams County
Difficulty: easy, short gravel trail
Placed: August 10, 2002 ~ by franzsolo...

This is a microbox ,please bring your own pen and stamp pad.
This stamp subject has nothing to do with this preserve ,but I wanted to
leave a letterbox in return for future seekers of The Prairie Dock Trail
~by kkreations which is also in this preserve.

Driving directions: Adams Lake State Park is one mile north of West
Union on State Route 41. After turning into the park, drive approx. .5
mile and look for a sign on your left: "Adams Lake Prairie
State Nature Preserve". Park in the parking area on your right.
Clues:
Cross the road to the info board next to the "Nature Preserve" sign.
Check out the trail map - you will be following the Prairie Dock
Trail, which begins just beyond the info board. Follow the trail up
the hill. The trail will split off to your right to make a loop around
the prairie -DON'T take the loop, but continue up the trail. You will
pass two info boards on your left as you continue
walking: the first is about oak trees, the second about ant mounds.
About 17 paces
(2 steps = 1 pace : about 5 feet ) up the path from the ant mound board,
you will reach a "Y" in the path. Go Right ( around the loop ) .You will
pass many info boards ... Deep Roots, Edwards Hairstreak,Blazing
Star,and Prairie Dock.

At the intersection go North to the Prairie openings sign on your left.
Go 5 paces from that sign .STOP
Take a bearing of 80 for 3 paces you will see a log lying in a N-S
direction.You want to look real hard at the NORTH end .Its a itty bitty
camoed film canister......good luck !

Please be especially conscious of leaving no trace as this is a nature
preserve.
