Hey, Becky. This has come up a couple of times before: it's one of
the prices we pay for the free service. The couple of ads per week
(or even month) that sneak in don't seem so bad to me. It's not
really SPAM, where someone maliciously sends the list 100 copies of
the same email. Now that's awful. Yahoo actually does a pretty good
job keeping the junk mail off-line compared to some other services we
use.

But there is a new thing that REALLY bothers me. Anyone else have the
option "recieve no email and read on the web only?" If so, you'll get
to enjoy a new function that I first noticed last night----yahoo is
putting up huge flashing banner advertisements right in the middle of
the text of each posting. I didn't so much mind the old ads at the
top of each page, especially since they're static and don't flash and
hop around demanding attention, but these new ones are a DRAG!

I posted (spammed?) half a dozen complaints to yahoo, but it's
actually hard to find a complaint center for them and I'm sure they
could care less what I think.

From: Kokopelli (Letterboxing@Home.com) | Date: 2001-12-13 15:17:12 UTC-07:00
You can't block the address, since all the Letterboxing email comes from a
common address. I've got 1552 LbNA emails in this folder and I think I've
deleted only 6-12 as being junk mail. Of course there is that ad that tags
along from Yahoo at the bottom of each email - as someone noted, just the
'price' of free email service for this type of group.

Generally, if you try to reply to these messages through their links, you
only encourage them. It proves 1) You have an active email box and 2) You
read your junk email and 3) You respond to it -- even if in a negative way.

Kokopelli in AZ
