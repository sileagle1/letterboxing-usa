VA: The VA Sec Is Messed Up

From: mlgainey (mlgainey@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-06-15 05:19:14 UTC
The new Web site is great. A big improvement for all concerned.

But the Virginia section is rather messed up, geographically speaking.
Boxes are in regions that they do not belong in, geographically
speaking. The webmanagers have been alerted to this but as yet, I
haven't received much response nor have the regions been cleaned up.

Having boxes in the wrong regions is frustrating for both
out-of-staters and for locals. The locals may know that a box listed
in Bedford County in the Central Virginia section should more
correctly be in the Western Virginia section, because Bedford is
outside of Roanoke, but out-of-staters may not know that. They're
thinking that Bedford is in central Virginia, when it is in the
southwest part of the state.

Conversely, locals have to look through all of the regions instead of
their respective regions because the regions list incorrect counties.
For instance, I live in Central Virginia, but I've also found some
Central boxes listed in Northern Virginia and Southeastern Virginia.

In one of the few e-mails I've received in response to these
observations, I was told that counties were arbitrarily assigned to
regions when the Web site was being redone. Now that hopefully the
dust has settled and the boxing community has settled in to this new
system, it's time to tweak the organization of boxes on the map so
that the boxes will be displayed geographically correctly.

I'll be glad to again provide a list of the boxes that I've come
across in the wrong spots, if you think that would be helpful.

-- mlg

From: Mary from Virginia (daughteroftheolddominion@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-06-15 23:27:22 UTC
I've noticed this situation too. It's a matter of decidng which
counties are in which section of the state - Northern VA, Central VA,
Southeastern VA, and Western VA. You can see on the LbNA map of the
state which counties are considered in which area. It's all
interpretation. I'd call the entire I-81 corridor Western VA but it
could make just as much sense to put Winchester in Northern VA (where
it is listed now). I, for one, would not extend Northern VA all the
way to Richmond. FYI to you non-Virginians, "Northern Virginia"
means something more than just a geographic region. It's kind of a
political thing.

Maybe other Virginians would know exactly, but isn't Northern
Virginia only Loudoun, Facquier, Fairfax, and Prince William
Counties? Maybe Culpeper and Stafford, but I don't think it would
include anything beyond that.

Trish - if we Virginians can agree where to draw the lines, can they
be changed?

Virginians - rather than tie up this list in emails trying to sort
this out, you can write directly to me -- daughteroftheolddominion
at yahoo.com -- and I'll coordinate the map correction.

Mary, somewhere in VA
