Starved Rock Lodge Letterbox #3

Situated on a high bluff just southwest of the rock itself is the stone and log lodge built in

part by the Civilian Conservation Corps in the 1930’s. The lodge has been refurbished, but still reflects the peaceful atmosphere of yesteryear. The lodge features a caf, gift shop, restaurant, an indoor swimming pool, children’s pool, whirlpool, saunas and an outdoor sunning patio. The lodge also offers 72 luxury rooms and 22 comfortable cabin rooms. The original Great Room is furnished with decorative rugs and art and is centered around a massive two-sided stone fireplace.

Some dates about Starved Rock State Park and Lodge

1673 Louis Jolliet and Father Jacques Marquette passed through here on their way up the Illinois from the Mississippi.

1682-1683 The French built Fort St. Louis atop Starved Rock.

1760’s The Legend of Starved Rock was born.

Pontiac, chief of the Ottawa tribe upriver from here, was slain by an Illiniwek while attending a tribal council in southern Illinois. According to the legend, during one of the battles that subsequently occurred to avenge his killing, a band of Illiniwek, under attack by a band of Potawatomi, sought refuge atop a 125 foot sandstone butte. The Ottawa and Potawatomi surrounded the bluff and held their ground until the Illinwek died of starvation—hence the name "Starved Rock"

1835 Daniel F. Hitt purchased Starved Rock for $85.

1890 Hitt sold Starved Rock and adjacent lands to Ferdinand Walther.

1911 Starved Rock was purchased by the State of Illinois and established as a State Park on June 10th.

1930’s Starved Rock Lodge was built in part by the Civilian Conservation Corps.

1986-1988 Renovation and addition to Starved Rock Lodge included 28 rooms, indoor heated pool and improvements in the original building.

2001 Rocky the Trolley came to the lodge and offered history tours to guests.

 

2003 In January Starved Rock Lodge constructed a waterfall display built in the lobby, using a big brown bear. This is where the hunt begins.
