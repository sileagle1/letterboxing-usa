Subject: Announcing Winter Gathering

Hi all! It's me Rustypuff!

It's been a long crazy year and I thought what better way to end it than
with a winter gathering.

I would like to invite all 'boxers to join us on Saturday January 18th
beginning at high noon (although ongoing after that for late comers) at the
Moose Hill Wildlife Sanctuary in Sharon, MA in the Nature Center for a
winter potluck.

I will provide soft drinks and juice and an appetizer. There will also be a
hot drink table with tea, instant coffee and hot cocoa. Bring along your
favorite dish (and recipes! since that seems so popular!)and your
letterboxing gear. There are five boxes on the sanctuary here and I will
probably be hiding a box for the gathering. I will also carve an event stamp
but would encourage others to do the same.The more the merrier!

The event will be hosted by me (Rustypuff)and CSCM, and though I work at the
Sanctuary, please don't contact them for information. Just post to the list
for either one of us and we will answer any questions.

Please RSVP to me and I will keep a count of who's coming (gotta know how
much soda to buy!). Also please note, pets are not allowed at the
Sanctuary(sorry! - not my rule)

I am excited to see you all (since I missed the Mystery gathering) and I
hope you all have a wonderful holiday!! (directions are below)

Cheers!

Kim Calcagno
aka Rustypuff

Directions to Moose Hill

The Nature Center is the large white estate house located at the
intersection of Moose Hill Street
and Moose Hill Parkway. The parking lot is on Moose Hill Parkway, adjacent
to the Nature Center .

Follow signs with the Massachusetts Audubon Society logo (blue triangle with
a tern in the center) along the way.

We have about 4 to 5" of snow cover. Most of the hiding places will
be burried. Some boxes could be frozen into the ground. Last
winter boxes here were visited as I have seen in the logs, but it
was a mild winter. Unless you hear that the snow has melted away,
postpone the trip. Late March and April would be better. Then you
can see the fabled New England mud season.
