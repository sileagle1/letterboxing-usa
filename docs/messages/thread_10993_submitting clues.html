Sometimes unfortunately clue postings fall through the cracks. I would
resubmit to the group and put in big letters at the very beginning of
the body of the message that this is a resubmission because the first
submission didn't seem to take.

Also, make sure you're titling your subject line in a helpful way. If
you didn't title it something to the effect of "New Box in Bellingham,
WA," it might have been accidentally overlooked amid the large amount
of postings on all sorts of topics that flood this group every day.

I am wondering how long it takes to get it posted.

It can depend on the webmanager's schedule for your region as well.
The webmanagers are volunteers, and life committments may interfere.
The webmanagers for the Mid-Atlantic region usually get ours up in 1-2
weeks.

My kids are dying to go check it and see if anyone has found it.

I understand that eagerness! Ya'll should go on and check on your box
because even though the clues haven't made it to letterboxing.org,
area boxers surely saw your original posting to the group, and surely
some of them printed off the posting and went about their merry way!

Good luck!
