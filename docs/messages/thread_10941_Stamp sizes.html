Eric, I think size matters even in stamps and books. We just planted a stamp that I thought was pretty large. It was 2 1/2" X 4". Normally I try to keep stamps smaller than that. We carry a "stamp in" book that is 7" X 5 1/2". Some folks have a 3 ring binder 8 1/2" X 11" and carry sheets with them on the trail.. We have a two part personal stamp in case we run across a micro box, plus we like to do a two color stamp in when we can. Stamps are personal preference------there are no rules. Don

From: dvn2rckr <dvn2rckr@yahoo.com> (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-02-26 20:01:28 UTC
I stamp into both my log book and my clue sheet when I go out
boxing. Then I file all the 'completed' boxes in a completed
letterbox notebook sorted by state. I also have a notebook for
letterboxes yet to find which I keep in the car along with
my 'letterbox creation kit' I have just in case I get inspired along
the way. These cluesheets are sorted by state and then by region.
I include a trail map from any parks in which the boxes might be (in
the event I have a map on hand or have found a map on the internet
for that region).
When we went to Cincinatti last Nov, I found that using the
rental car map of the region that we got to plot where the
letterboxes were located helped us quite a bit in the 'efficiency'
of trying to tackle several boxes throughout the city in a very
limited amount of time. Now, I'm trying to collect rental car maps
of major cities in which I might look for letterboxes in the future
(it sure beats drawing the maps by hand--which is what my sister
does--but she's an artist). It was the perfect size for including
in my notebook (3 ring binder--folded in half) and really helped
seeing at a glance where & the quantity of boxes in certain regions
around the city. Now, if only I had enough freetime to go out
boxing again... :(

See you on the trails...

We each have a 3/5 spiral bound index card "notebook". Nice size...
fits into a plastic ziplock bag easily to keep the water out. Slips
into a back pocket of a pair of jeans should you have some reason to
carry it. Fits most stamps...and you can always do half on 1 page
and half on the next if you choose.

Our next books will probably be more along the lines of a journal-
type book with nice heavy paper in it.... got that idea from Patrick
and Amy (And Ruth!).
