That's exactly the thought process that's gone into the PNWLb database. Of
course, anything created after something else ahs been created can do it
differently/better.

The current website for letterboxing.org is a phenomenal system, believe me,
and, from a programmers view point, extremely difficult to do unless you've
got a ton of programmers working on it. The website at geocaching.com is
backed by some major money as is evidenced by this from their website:

Get Great Deals on GPS Equipment While Supporting Geocaching.com!
Our partnership with Offroute.com allows us to bring you dramatic discounts
on GPS units and accessories. At the same time, a portion of every sale
comes back to support the continued development of this site!So click on
over and check out hot new GPS units like the Magellan SporTrak Map or
Garmin Rino 110 or find the accessory, map or guidebook needed for your next
geocaching adventure




Those of us, Wes and myself and others, that create the letterboxing sites
do so out of our own pockets. I've no idea what Wes spends on his site, but
mine is covered 100% from my pocket.

Bottom line? We'll not get anything form the geocaching site, the
software/database is copyright protected and not opensource, I've already
checked! (grin) As far as the future of letterboxing.org I can not say, I'm
not involved in that one, but as far as the PNWLb website, I can speak on
it, I am a little bit involved in that website! (grin) Keep an eye on it,
I'm adding new code each and everyday. There are a lot of additions planned
for the near future.
