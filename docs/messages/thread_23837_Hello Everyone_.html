Hi Newbiecat66, welcome! The LBNA webmasters have put together a very
useful page with links to info to help you get started:
http://www.letterboxing.org/GettingStarted/
Also, I highly recommend you join the newboxers Yahoo group in addition
to this one.
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/
It's a list specifically designed for newbies, so you can get any and
all of your starting out questions answered and meet lots of other
boxers who are just starting out (and some veterans as well).

From: (Stellabaker123@aol.com) | Date: 2006-10-03 17:49:13 UTC-04:00
If you like local history, cemetry boxes are good for this time of year. or several letterboxes at at time ( series) or great hikes in parks etc check out the clues and choose what is best for your group. I letterbox with my daughter (42) most of the time on Long Island, NY where we live. But with the others in my family when we visit in NH or Ct or on vacation in Maine etc. Our group ranges in ages from 73- my granddaughters 17 & 10 and my new grandson 3 weeks. Letterboxing is a great outdoor family event for us and a wonderfuf hobby for me now that I am retired.r

Welcome and enjoy.
