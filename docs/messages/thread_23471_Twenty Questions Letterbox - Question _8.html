This mail is being sent as part of an ongoing game of Twenty
Questions which has the purpose of determining the clues for the
Twenty Questions Letterbox. For more details see:

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?
boxnum=22779&boxname=Twenty_Questions

12 questions remain. Questions answered so far:

Question 1: Is it East of 87 degrees West (This is a longitude line
just East of Chicago)?
Answer 1: Yes.

Question 2: Is it South of the Mason Dixon Line (Pennsylvania -
Maryland border)?
Answer 2: No.

Question 3: Is the box located in New England (Maine, New Hampshire,
Vermont, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Connecticut)?
Answer 3: Yes.

Question 4: Is the letterbox located in Southern New England
(Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Connecticut)?
Answer 4: Yes.

Question 5: Does the state where the box is located share a border
with New York?
Answer 5: Yes.

Question 6: Is it on the Appalachian trail?
Answer 6: No.

Question 7: Is the box in Massachusetts?
Answer 7: Yes.

Question 8: Is the box planted in one of the following counties:
Berkshire, Dukes, Franklin, Hampden, Hampshire, Nantucket, Suffolk or
Norfolk?
Answer 8: No.

Now accepting candidates for Question 9. Fire away. Please send your
questions as described in the original instructions.

Sincerely,
The Ephemeral Letterboxer

This is a tough one. Worcester, Middlesex, and Essex are also still in
the loop. But....We are down to only 6 counties. Here are some
thoughts.....

Is it in a county that borders Plymouth County?
Does the county border either New Hampshire or Maine?
Does the county border Buzzard's Bay?
Is it on the Cape? (Maybe a bit premature?)

I do like the idea of asking about whether it's in a state park,
however, if the answer is no, that'll leave about every public area
within each city or town. On the flip side, there aren't many state
parks in the areas that are left, that, if the answer is yes, we'll
dramatically reduce the areas. Again, a tough one.

Maybe we could reduce it by asking if the box is within a certain
distance from the Atlantic Ocean, say 30 miles. That would not only
reduce the counties, but also reduce the area within certain counties.

Any more discussion?

Blax
