Re: [LbNA] Help - deleting one of my find logs so I can fix the year

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2007-08-08 13:15:15 UTC-07:00
On the "Change Status" page, you can select a status of "(None)" and then enter the same date that you entered earlier. From what I can see, this does not completely remove the entry -- you'll still see your trailname and the date listed on the status page -- but it will remove the green arrow indicating that you found the box, and will no longer count it as one of your Found boxes.

From: Gail Metzger (queenofswords110@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-08 18:44:15 UTC-07:00
Reminder places a blue star to the left of the box name on the listing. That way, if there's one you want to find in the future (it looks interesting or one you want to tackle at some point) you can look quickly and see the ones you wanted to do. When you find them and change the status to found, the blue star goes away. I've used it as a way to earmark boxes that I want to do say in a particular area on a road trip.

page, and click on "Show Finds". You'll see an option to "remove find" after

[LbNA] Re:Help - deleting one of my find logs so I can fix the year

From: mama_cache (mama_cache@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-10 13:10:12 UTC
You can change the date of your find without removing the find. Look at the title in a list
of boxes that you have found. On the right, click on the green flag. You'll see that the date
blank is blank. Just put the correct date in that blank, and your find will update to the
correct one.

To completely remove a find, I'd contact the webmasters. That can probably be done from
their end. Just give them all of the details.
