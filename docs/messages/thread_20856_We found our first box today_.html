We found our first box today!

From: Martha Fletcher (mrfletcher21133@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-11-03 16:52:26 UTC


Today my son (age 14) and I went out to look for our first Letter Box.
I homeschool him and thought this would be a nifty activity for us to
get out of the house and do.

We found the site but being in the woods absolutly EVERYTHING was
covered in 4" of leaves! Well I looked in the tree where it was
supposed to be and saw nothing so I started looking in the vacinity.
The DS looked in the tree again and said "MOM, it's right HERE!" Well,
doh!

He was skeptical about this when we first started the drive out but he
got into looking for the box and was just as pleased when HE found it.
He even is thinking about where we can leave a box.

We stamped the clue page we printed and plan on keeping them in a note
book with any other maps etc we collect along the way.

Martha and Paul
Randallstown, MD

Welcome to letterboxing! One of the reasons my family
boxes is that's a family activity. Even my hormonal 14
will up for finding a box. Aside from just finding the
box there's carving and planning the next outing,
writing clues and many more. Why don't you check out
the newbie list and your local area list. I think you
are in the mid-atlantic region. I think I saw on the
list someone e-mailed you those sites. They can be
very helpful and do tend to be a little less intense
than the big list. See you on the trails.
