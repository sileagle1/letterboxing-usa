I think HH's and cuckoo clues are a bit different than a permanent
set of clues. By nature, HH's and cuckoo clues are in one place only
for a short time, so it is very likely that the box owner wouldn't
even know they had been there.

If I were doing maintenance on a box and found an unidentified piece
of paper that I was unsure of, it is quite possible I wouldn't even
leave it there. It is obvious when you find a HH hanging out in your
box -- it is a stamp. My experience with cuckoo clues is limited
only to placing them, but I would think they are clearly identified.

While I wouldn't go so far as to say that permisison is required
before placing a set of permanent clues into another person's box, it
certainly seems like the best way to go about it, just to make sure
they are aware and not wondering when they are checking on the box
and find the clues there.

BTW Eric, a cuckoo clue is a clue to a permanent letterbox that is
not published, rather the clue is located inside another letterbox
and moved from box to box in the same way as a hitch hiker, by the
last person to find it. They are elusive and mysterious and the
specifics are limited only by your imagination -- some are simply a
piece of paper with a clue on, others are in the form of a log book,
which you would then stamp in and out of a box in the same way as a
hitch hiker. Some ask you to relocate them within another box in the
same series or park, others can be relocated anywhere, even in
another part of the country!
