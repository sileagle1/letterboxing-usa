From: Bob LaBelle (r.labelle@verizon.net) | Date: 2003-12-03 17:21:40 UTC
It's a small problem, but the name of a LB does, or doesn't, draw
boxers to it. I only placed this one - at the historic site in
Seneca Falls, NY - three weeks ago, on Stanton's birthday, but the
idea had been in my mind for some time. What to call it, that wasn't
too long, when a number of worthy women and both the locally-founded
Hall of Fame and the Women's Rights National Historic Park were
involved? Seemed to me that Mrs. Stanton deserved as much fame as
Miss Anthony, but wasn't accorded it on the national scene. And my
guess is that a 'hall of fame' might have greater draw than 'historic
park'. Hence the name, now reflecting this in the list of
letterboxes for Central & Western New York.
If you haven't been to Seneca Falls to pay homage to the seminal
activities of 150 years ago, you owe it to yourself. Well, yes there
may already be snow cover there, but it's less likely than at many
other 'snow-belt' areas of NY.
Bob / 'Cock o' the Trail'
