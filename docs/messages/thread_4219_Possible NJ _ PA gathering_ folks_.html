From: nysystem (perfectcd@aol.com) | Date: 2002-01-19 00:24:26 UTC
I've just had a glorious vision: a group of letterboxers from New Jersey and
Pennsylvania (and elsewhere)...all sitting at a bar or restaurant...talking about
their number one hobby: letterboxing....

Is this a crazy idea, or is it feasible? We keep seeing each other's stamps (and
feathers) all over; how about we put some faces to the names? It can be a
casual affair--two or three hours, some banter, exchanging stamps. No big
preparation involved--just a no muss, no fuss night out.

If anyone's interested, let's start suggesting possible dates and locations --
I'm thinking of some time towards the end of February or beginning of March,
somewhere between Allentown and Princeton/Somerville. Then, after we get a
few suggestions, we can come to some sort of consensus (or I'll just put all of
the suggestions in a hat and pick one out).

Also, if you're from some other state (or state of mind), but long car trips
don't bother you, you're welcome to join us as well!

Post your suggestions as a reply to this post. I'm also reachable at
pcletterboxing@aol.com, if you want to write me a letterboxing poem.

Hurray for PA/NJ!

We absolutely loved the couple of letterboxing trips we made down that
way recently, and can't wait to make a few more letterboxing trips and
friends! (Hopefully, more than "sightings" :-) ) Please count us
in as part of the "New England contingent"! Just give us the daylight
hours to drive down (c. 5hrs) and do some 'boxing on the way, and
we'll hope to meet you all somewhere after dark (say sixish?). March
2nd at Raubsville sounds great! Last time we were there (Dec. 23) we
managed to find 14 boxes in one day (both along the Delaware Canal and
amidst a variety of PA rocks) only to find out later, when we got
home, that even more boxes had been planted nearby during that very
same time frame! So, we definitely have to get back down there, and
now we'll be looking forward to meeting as many of you fine folks as
we can at the same time!
