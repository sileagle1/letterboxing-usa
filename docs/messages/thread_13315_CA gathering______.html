From: trentlaudes (trentlaudes@excite.com) | Date: 2003-06-03 23:36:06 UTC
This is a big state, and separate Southern and Northern California
gatherings make a lot of sense, but I like Jugglermouse's idea. The
Central Coast is one of the biggest letterboxing areas in
California. I vote for the city of San Luis Obispo for the gathering
(by coincidence, Juggler's town). There are 20 letterboxes planted
in the city, and another 50 of them in nearby towns. San Luis Obispo
is also located almost exactly halfway between Los Angeles (my town)
and San Francisco, so I think it makes the perfect choice.
--Buzzard

From: Sir Balthazar (neovolatile@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-06-04 00:31:09 UTC
Okay, like Ms. Mouse, I am prejudiced. The San Luis Obispo area has a
large concentration of boxes with more going in everyday. We have
high climbing ones; we have drive-by ones. We have stop in the shop
and ask the proprietor ones; we have do not let anyone in the shop
see you ones.

Adding to that, we have boxes with stamps carved by Amanda from
Seattle and JugglerMouse (who should become as famous as any boxer
out there for her wonderful work.)

Our climate is temperate all year long. The ocean is beautiful. The
accomedations reasonable.

And we are midway between SF (240 mi south) and LA (190 north) on
highway 101.

Do please consider this as a place to visit and maybe even have an
event here.

Thanks,
Sir Balthazar
aka J. Ellsworth Weaver
