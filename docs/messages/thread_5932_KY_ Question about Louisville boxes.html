Went to Louisville over the weekend.  Attempted both boxes, with no success. :(

Search for the first box was confusing for us, as there were so many crosstrails and spurs.  Must be that we didn't follow the correct ones so that the clues would link together.  Yikes!  Would have been more determined, but I was the only stamper and letterboxer.  Others were tag-alongs who weren't as enthralled with the idea of trekking around in the 90-plus degree heat and humidity!

99.999% sure we found the right location for the second box, but it wasn't at the site.

Anyone have any recent experience with these boxes?  My friend lives in the area, and would be willing to try again if the boxes are intact.

Thanks in advance,

From: David (dcassidy@together.net) | Date: 2002-07-08 09:52:48 UTC-04:00
Pam -

I was the first person to stamp into these boxes, about a year ago. I had the
same problems with the first box. The woods behind the turtle fountain is so
criss-crossed with trails. I finally gave up, and in walking up the paved path
from the large bridge, I spotted the smaller bridge and the boxes resting
place. I doubt if it could be found accidentally and would be surprised if it
is not still there.

The second box was much easier. You walk along the river from the parking
area, come to a small open field, and the log containing the box is directly
across the field from you. Of course, there is always the chance that the log
pile had been removed.

This probably doesn;t help much, but at least confirms that the boxes were
there as of last summer.

David
