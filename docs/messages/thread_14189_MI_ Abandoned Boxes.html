I will take responsibility for trying to get in touch with the owners
of abandoned boxes in western and northern Michigan, and a few in the
U.P. I will attempt to get in touch with the original placer for
these boxes and would be willing to adopt them and maintain them if
they remain unclaimed. This includes the following boxes:

Placed by Arbo:
Muskegon Beach Box
Pines Point

Placed by Esperanza:
Interlochen
Cherry Capital
Point Betsie Lighthouse
Leland Fish Town
Top O' the Pickle
Mighty Mac

Placed by Esperanza & Matt the Ratt:
Cairn Highway
Old Mission Peninsula

Placed by the Shorewood Folks:
Mt. Baldy
Mt. Baldy Jr.

Placed by Rainbow's End:
Birthday
First Letterbox on the Lake
Beach View by the Dunes

This leaves a few boxes in eastern MI and a couple in the U.P.
Perhaps there is somebody else that would be willing to follow-up on
these?

SpringChick

Dearest Michigan Letterboxers-

SpringChick's got the rest of the state covered. Here's what's left in Eastern Michigan:

Brighton:

Snowflake- Presumed MISSING

Clarkston:

This Place Rocks

49 Habitat Trail

Dragonflies are Free

Independent Oak

Ladies of the Lake

Royal Oak

Secret Garden- Presumed MISSING

I live in Detroit, so Clarkston is a bit of a hike for me, but I can make it up there once in a while. HOWEVER, (and this is totally embarassing) I've been there twice and could only find "This Place Rocks" and "49 Habitat Trail". "Indpendent Oak" completely stumped me. So much so I never even attempted "Dragonflies" or "Ladies". I'd be more than happy to maintain them, if I could find them *doh* So if you've been there and can direct pokey old me to "Independent Oak", I'm all for taking on the box. *grin*



As for "Snowflake" and "Secret Garden" no one's been able to find them at all- not even people who've been successful in the past. In one case, a whole landmark was wiped out in a storm- it seems that they are MIA.

If there's anyone closer to Clarkston who'd like to take the boxes, great. If not, I'll be more than happy to.

Cheers,

Delirium
