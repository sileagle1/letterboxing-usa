From: Tom Noone (mntnlover@hotmail.com) | Date: 2003-08-26 11:55:01 UTC-04:00
As the Sun Dog wearies of his daily journey and the Autumn Maiden readies
her paintbrush, I again beseech ye to gather together. My people enjoin you
to pursue hidden treasure and share in the bounty of our harvest before the
Psychokiwis depart for the lands of our Maori brothers. Come prepared to
share your carvings and your bounty with my people. We will gather four
hours before the moon sets, ten days after the harvest moon to celebrate a
successful hunt.

The encroachment of the white man has forced us to flee to the northern
realms of the village of the white man's ruler. Those who quest for the
center of our community shall find us gathered at the white man's lodge of
frigid waters beside our neighboring Indian brothers bay, here in the land
of the ocean. At 30 steps down the way of the Beach we shall meet and be
free from the whims of Brother Wind and Sister Rain. Meet us there and
together we will begin new pursuits...

The fair maiden, Alafair, has agreed to share the ancient art of journal
making two hours before the moon sets. She asks only that you contact her
to reserve your place and bring two of the white man's bills for supplies.

For those weary travellers from afar respite can be had at area lodgings or
you may set up camp. I will gladly provide information for interested
seekers.

Tom Noone
