BURNTWOOD LETTERBOX

Dropped: On May 6th, 2002, by A.Rose and The Vet.
Location: Beaverton, Washington County, Oregon.
Path: Paved but very hilly.
Clue: Moving toward clever.
Misc: This might be defined as a microbox. (We used a 1/2 cup container).
Be sure to bring a pen and stamp pad.

Don Quixote dropped by to call on us in Beaverton the other day. He began
weaving a tale about why he had been so delayed. Something about a
mysterious Land of Burntwood and some modern day giants he encountered. The
four-legged ones plagued him with memories of monsters from long ago.
Worried for the fair Dulcinea, he engaged in battle with one and then the
other. He says he accidentally let fall his satchel of spoils at the feet of
one of these monstrous giants. This is where his tale begins to unravel a
bit, for Don Quixote declared that he won the battle despite losing his
fortunes at the feet of his evil foe. Before we could question him further,
he was off to continue his adventures. Poor Don Quixote, we can still see
the monsters standing there undefeated when we pass by.
