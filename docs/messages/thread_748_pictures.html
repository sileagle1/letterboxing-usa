Dear Garth, Kathy, and everyone else...
Thanks to all who sent such nice comments regarding the photo comp of my
nephew and me letterboxing in Kansas.

Since some did not get the attachment, I have also posted it in the
Letterboxing Kids Photo Album on the website.
http://members.aol.com/catsdice/lbna/kids/album02.htm

As for Garth's questions, I did the photo montage on my PC using the layer
masking feature of Adobe PhotoShop 5.5. It's a positively MAGIC piece of
software!!!!

The photo on the left of the composition shows us on the boulder that sits
atop the Waconda Lake letterbox near Glen Elder, Kansas. That's about an
hour north by north-west of Kanopolis. (Both Glen Elder and Kanopolis Lake
are on the Smoky Hills Creek)

On the right, Little Foot is pointing to where the Cedar Bluff letterbox is
hidden at Cedar Bluff Reservoir near WaKeeney, Kansas, which is about an hour
and a half west of Kanopolis.

All six of the NW Kansas Lakes where we've placed letterboxes would be
excellent places for riding horses. Someday, perhaps we'll get to expand the
series eastward to include Kanopolis and the very popular lakes at Milford
and Tuttle Creek.

Sincerely,
Der Mad Stamper
