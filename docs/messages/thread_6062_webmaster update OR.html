webmaster update OR

From: funhog1 (funhog@pacifier.com) | Date: 2002-07-08 02:48:41 UTC
Little did I know that the dry sandy path I walked in February would be a thriving wetland in July, complete
with kingfishers and swimming ducks. Who knew? Please use the following as the revised clue:

On February 13, 2002 Funhog placed the Sandy Delta Letterbox in the Columbia River Gorge. This box is
near the town of Troutdale, in Multnomah County, Oregon.

To the trailhead: From Portland take Hwy 84 east to exit 18 (Lewis and Clark State Park.) At the stop
sign turn right. Go under the underpasses and park at the green metal gate straight ahead. Be careful
not to get back on the freeway! The letterbox is about a forty five minute stroll from your car, one way.
Be prepared for the possibility of wet feet.

To the letterbox: A short distance after you pass through the green metal gate, turn left at a narrow
dirt road. Stay on this road past a couple of seasonal ponds. Once you find yourself beneath three sets
of three go with the flow, some above, some below. Eventually, the path turns to the right. Find a bright
yellow warning post with a riverside view. From this post go back the way you came eighty steps. The
letterbox is twenty four steps away at three times thirty three.
