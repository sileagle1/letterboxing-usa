From: Chuck Straub (woodschuckstraub@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-12-02 16:37:25 UTC-08:00

Trails look different each season. If you are not careful you can easily go off a trail in the fall and snow is worse. You have to really be careful in when in a unfamilier area. I think people cut back on  letterboxing in the winter for several reasons, cold, difficulty walking and the snow making it harder to find a letterbox/. Last winter I went to a letterbox that was under a rock. I found the rock but it was so frozen to the ground I couldn't move it. In the spring the boxes are basically how you left them. There may be some leaves or twigs on them but pretty much just like you left them. The leafs don't build up too much in one season.....Chuck and Molly

From: Janet (moonstone_baby@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-12-03 01:52:52 UTC
oh, boy, now I have an excuse for not finding all the boxes I've
missed lately! One of the biggest reasons why I cut back in the
winter (other than the obvious more foul weather reason) is the lack
of daylight. In the summer I go out letterboxing a lot after work.
I the winter I can't go at all during the week after work because it
is dark. I first started letterboxing in February ... back then I
didn't know it was supposed to be harder in winter LOL.
Janet
