Greetings:
I don't presently have a way of editing my letterboxes (home computer
down with a probable bad hard-drive) so I'm noting verification on
three of them here. Despite some drastic grounds-maintenance by some
kind and understanding souls, two remained in place, safe 'n sound:
Blueberry Patch (Schuyler) and Women's Hall of Fame (Seneca). The
former has had 65 finders in three years, to be the most popular -
and perhaps easiest of mine. Sassafras Pond (Schuyler) is also OK,
tho the trail to it has been kinda wet all summer and into the fall.
I should also mention that a recent trip to Maine has permitted me to
revisit the Mystery LB - "LLB-LB", which has also so far survived
likely grounds-people intervention.
Also. on that trip I stopped at Quechee Gorge in Vermont and found
those two. Since my Dad was born and raised a few miles away toward
White River Jct. these had special meaning for me.
Bob / 'Cock o' the Trail'
