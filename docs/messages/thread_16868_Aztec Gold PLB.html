Aztec Gold PLB

From: Rayvenhaus (rayvenhaus@myndworx.com) | Date: 2003-11-16 10:05:18 UTC-08:00
Currently, as of 9:30 am this morning, Sunday Nov 16th, 2003 there are 32
registered participants in the Aztec Gold Postal Letterbox. The response
has been overwhelming and I wish to thank each and everyone of you, both
publicly and privately, for requesting this box. I only hope that the stamp
is as good as you are hoping it to be.

This box will consist of the stamp and a URL to a private online website
that will contain the clues to my first Virtual Letterboxes. Again, since
this is really meant for those that can not make it to Washington to get
these stamps themselves, I am making the images of the rest of the stamps in
the series as virtual letterboxes for those that wish to
view/capture/grab/whatever those boxes as well.

You may ask what will make the real stamps any better than this? Let me
explain the concept here. First off, the Aztec Gold stamp is broken into 5
pieces and each part is hidden individually. That in and of itself is a
kewl prize, if you will, in my mind, but, since I am a very enthusiastic
letterboxer, I added more. There are also six pirate flags hidden as well.
Some of these are hidden using treasure maps, parts of which are missing and
the missing parts must be found in order to complete the clues for the
flags. Once someone has found the flags and has completed the Aztec Gold
piece and shows me those images, we have a copy of the famed 'Black Pearl of
Manila' necklace, reputedly made for Queen Isabella of Spain but never
delivered due to a Pirate raid (Imagine that!), for each and every one that
completes the series.

On a side note, please let me know privately, don't want to clog the lists
with replies here, what you think of this idea of the 'prize' for those that
complete the entire series. Please reply to this address:
XrayvenhausX@XmyndworxX.XcomX (remove the X's) and not by clicking on the
reply button, otherwise it will go to the mailing list.
