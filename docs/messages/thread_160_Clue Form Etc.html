From: Bonita McLaughlin (bonitasusan@hotmail.com) | Date: 1999-01-11 06:16:47 UTC-08:00
In another message Daniel wrote:

>If I did it my way I would put a "Default"
>choice on the form that would simply create the page with the
>standardcolors, fonts,
etc. That would give you the best of both worlds. You'd
>have the "simple default form" option or the "create a detailed form"
>option.

This sounds best to me. I personally would prefer a default that takes
care of details for me. Not that I'm lazy but it's just not an area of
interest to me.

Also, thught I'd pass on a passage from Saint-Exupery's The Little
Prince, which I circled in the book when I first read it (at age 12) and
which I still keep coming back to. It seems to have relevance to all
sorts of things in life, including letterboxing. It is the secret the
fox tells the Little Prince before he leaves Earth to return to his
planet:

"It is only with the heart that one can see rightly; what is essential
is invisible to the eye."

Cheers,
Bonnie McLaughlin
