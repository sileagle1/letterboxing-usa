From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-05-01 21:47:07 UTC
This is the letterbox labels I created to put on most of my
letterboxes:


YOU HAVE FOUND A

LETTERBOX

PLEASE DO NOT THROW AWAY

WWW.LETTERBOXING.ORG

Some of my boxes are smaller than the labels--in that case, I just
write on the container using a permanent marker (such as a Sharpie
pen).

On the inside of most of the letterboxes, I put this label:


If you are new to letterboxing:

Letterboxing is an intriguing pastime combining map-reading skills,
puzzle solving, outdoor adventure, artistic ability
and delightful "treasure-hunts" in beautiful, scenic places.

Please sign in & carefully replace the
box where you found it (hidden from view).
If you do not know where it was hidden,
please contact the owner via the info listed within the logbook.
Look for clues to this and other letterboxes on
www.letterboxing.org

Of course, they look better when you print them up in a word
processing program such as Microsoft Word--where I highlight some of
the text and use bold letters to emphasize various things.

Hope this helps!

dvn2r ckr

Why didn't I think of that? If you print your notice on sticky label
paper, then cover it with some clear mailing tape it should be
waterproof.

So I just made a sheet of labels and added it to the files section:

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-usa/files/Letterbox%20Materials/

You can open the pdf file and then type in the name of your letterbox
where it says "XYZ Letterbox". Print them on label paper and you're
ready to go!
