Once Upon a Time...

Some letterboxers went out to look for a stamp.
This stamp could only be found between 10:30 AM and 7 PM on Tuesdays,
Wednesdays, and Thursdays, between 10:30 AM and 8 PM on Fridays and
Saturdays, and between 12:00 noon and 4 PM on Sundays, and it couldn't
be found on Mondays and holidays at all!

They drove to W. Commodore Way in Seattle between 33rd Ave. W. and
Gilman Ave. W. and parked in the parking lot on the north side of the
street.

When they got out of their car, they went down to the water and walked
east along the water where they saw some feathered friends of Jonathan
Livingston Seagull, Jemima Puddleduck, and Mother Goose.
They walked toward a ladder that people can't climb, and went up a place
where there were waves on dry land.
They found a lock that has no key, and crossed over it to a building
with three flags on it.
They went north past a sign that said "Welcome".
They kept going north, past an English garden, and through a gate of
metal.
They turned right past a little mermaid and her undersea friends.
Next to a garden of stone, they reached the end of the story.
They went in to a place where stories are found, and asked the friendly
proprietor for the letterbox that comes after the story's end.
And they lived happily ever after.

The End
