From: Baqash (lliving51@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-17 19:34:25 UTC-07:00
In Southern Arizona you dont letterbox much in the summer unless they are
indoors or drivebys.
Or if you do take plenty or water, wear good sunscreen, and go out early in
the morning.
Did a two mile hike this morning (round trip) to one. I was back at the car
by 8:45 and it was already 85 degrees.
It led to a great overview of the city, peace and quiet.

So if you can stand the heat, it never stops. My solution? Go north in the
summer.

Baqash

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-05-17 21:34:56 UTC-05:00
We find and plant throughout the year. Most of our local plants are during
the winter (yes, even though we live in Wisconsin and we had the 2nd
snowiest season in history - nearly 100 inches this year). However we also
plant throughout the year when we travel to other states/countries. We'll
plant one in NY next weekend, two in MN the next weekend, then some in IL
and MO in June.

OUt here on the LEFT coast, some of us stop 'boxing right about the
time the New Englanders start! Too hot and too much poison oak! Unless

getting sweaty and bitten up but I'd rather be quilting when the sun's
blazing.

Oh, unless we're on vacation in one of the 'cooler' states.

Still, we've managed to reach F-1000 in the 6 years we've been at
this.
