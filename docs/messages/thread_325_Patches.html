Hey Lil Sis...

you left the 8 out of your e-mail address in your first post. Looks
like Melissa tried to reply, but it got returned.

The patches you're asking about are letterboxing patches that a group
of members designed, ordered, and are now receiving the first
shipment. They'll put them on their packs and/or hats for the purpose
of identifying themselves to other letterboxers who they might come
across on the trail. The nature of the hobby is to hunt and stamp in
discreetly, and sometimes that means missing an opportunity to meet
and exchange stamps with others who are there for the same reason.

The patches we talked about last weekend were in relation to scouting
and I remembered a post from someone involved with girl or boy scouts
who mentioned possibly coming up with a patch for their scouts to
earn. The scout patch idea and the letterboxing patches are two
different things.

Look in the posts of early January. There's one from Stephen
Hutchinson who posted about finding my Meadow Farm box here in
Richmond. I think he's the one who mentioned scouting. I'll e-mail
him as well. Turns out he's the dad of a family whose kids attended
the school where I worked....small world, huh?

By the way, tell Patrick the clues to the letterbox we planted at
Jayne's Hill are on the map. Let him check the clues out on the
site, then take him up there soon to check on the box and on his
snowman he built while we were there.
