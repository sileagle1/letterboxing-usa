From: jedaryn02 (jedaryn02@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-06 19:28:56 UTC
10 January 2004

<><

Tentative schedule:
1000-Meet and Greet/Exchange Stamps @ 10907 W. Vega Ln (208 286-9417)
1030-Share Letterboxing/carving ideas, etc.
1130-Lunch provided by J&A-Bear (probably chili, chips, etc.)
1230-Letterbox like crazy

Come for all or part and enjoy meeting other letterboxers. Children
are welcome!!!

Please email/call A-Bear (Aryn) with any questions at
jedaryn02@y... or 286-9417.

Directions: Travelling east on I84 take exit 25 towards Middleton on
Hwy 44. Travel 11 miles until you reach Star. After its only
stoplight turn left on Main St. before Star Merc (grocery
story/Chevron gas station). Continue until it "Ts" and then turn
right on Frost St. Take the first left onto Vega. 10907 is the 2nd
house on the left.

Travelling from Boise take State St/Hwy 44 west until you reach
Star. Past the Post Office and Blake Trailers on your right, turn
right onto Knox St. Continue until it dead-ends and then turn left
onto Frost. Take the immediate right onto Vega and 10907 is the 2nd
house on the left.

From: dvn2rckr (dvn2rckr@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-11 14:23:41 UTC
The 10 Jan 2004 Letterboxing Gathering in Star, Idaho proved to be a
roaring success despite the region being blanketed in snow. A great
cast of characters showed up to converse in LB speak including:

A-Bear (who graciously hosted the event)
Doc Peabody
Hannah Oakley
Lori Darlin'
Cadenza
rnrB
trkr
dvn2r ckr

& there were occasional sightings of J-Bear, too...

Lots of fun was had by all, many boxes were found, incredible food
was consumed (simply scrumptious chili & cornbread) and great ideas
exchanged. As the group parted, Cadenza, A-Bear, rnrB, trkr & dvn2r
ckr braved the cold temperatures and the snow to tackle some local
letterboxes in Star & Middleton. Again, we were fortunate enough to
experience another unintended bout of nighttime letterboxing with
zero nocturnal illumination thanks to the cloud cover in the region--
but everything worked out well, nonetheless, and we were victorious
in locating the boxes (just not too great at stamping in the multi-
colored & quite detailed stamps that A-Bear has hidden around these
parts).

Thanks, everyone, for making this event such a resounding success.
It's so great to meet Idaho area folks who are as excited about
letterboxing as we are--and what incredibly nice & interesting people
they are, too!!! I'm especially glad that A-Bear & Cadenza have some
new letterboxing folks in the region as I think they were tiring of
only finding each other's boxes. ;)

Have a wonderful day!

dvn2r ckr
