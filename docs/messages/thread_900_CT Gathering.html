From: John De Wolf (jdewolf@mail.icrsurvey.com) | Date: 2000-05-15 11:50:08 UTC-04:00
Hi all,

Got back home from our second gathering last night after midnight! What a great weekend it was!

Jay, you picked a beautiful spot for us all to meet. It was a pleasure to reconnect w/ letterboxers (Tom, Rae, Jerry, Fred (for the 1st time)) as well as meet the CT/EMS connection (Jay, Ned, Ariel, Sarah, Terri, Bob, Judy, Abbie) and, perhaps most importantly, convert my wife and the boys. Residents of CT & surrounding states are very lucky that the hobby has caught on as rapidly as it has (Now if we could get Jay et al. to travel around the country a little more...). We found a nice variety of boxes (short hikes appropriate for small kids, and longer ones in beatiful surroundings).

These weekends are truly special, and I look forward to the day that they may be held anywhere in the country! We just need to figure out how to get the word out better, and get the boxes out there. I wish you could all have been there around the campfire and out on the trails. As I had had the opportunity to do at the 1st Gathering (VT) with Kim & Royce, I had the joy of searching for some boxes with another boxer (other than my family). I can enrich the experience.

So, my heartfelt thanks to Jay & his clan for all they've done for letterboxing, to the EMS crew for starting it all up there and sharing their clues with us, and to every one else on the list for your contributions in your own way. The future looks bright!

Randy, Kim, Royce, we missed you. Actually we missed all those on the list who couldn't be there. See you

Lone Wolf
P6F42X20

PS: Mitch, nice to see your post. Looking forward to June.

Tired but happy greetings to all,

John summed up our week-end gathering very nicely. It was great to
see again those letterboxers we had already met, and equally
wonderful to meet new ones. Thanks for the surprise, Rae & Jerry.

It wasn't sunny on Saturday, but the rain came before 9 am and after
11 pm, and didn't interfere with the hunt. Although I love the woods,
it has been a special treat the two times I've been letterboxing in
CT/RI to walk along the ocean or the ocean bluff.

As John said, it was really nice to participate in a hunt with some
other devotees; usually letterboxing is a solitary affair.

I think the campfire Saturday night was the high point. It was great
to Sarah & Terri and Bob & Judy. You all at EMS have done so much to
promote our hobby in the northeast.

And Jay, thank you especially for all that you have done and for
setting up this week-end for us!
