I visited Eliza B.'s "Trees of Stone" yesterday. I was the first to
stamp in on this great site with a pretty (if not ooopish) stamp

The hike IS a hefty three miles. If you start thinking you went the
wrong way on the clues keep going it is a long way up over the top.

It was a sunny hot Eastern Washington day. There is a constant
breeze though so it is tollerable. Water is a MUST. The view from
the box, excellent!

Eliza, I have concerns that the particular box is not holding up in
the heat. The plastic feels somewhat mushy. I am not familiar with
they type of box you used and what its original state should be.

Hi guys--

Just a thought about finding solid (yet cheap) LB containers in the
Pacific Northwest. I stopped by Bargain World and Value Village the
other day and found a smorgasbord of very sturdy plasticware (like
Rubbermade or Tupperware or other freezer ware type stuff) that
seemed perfect for letterboxes. The bonus was that the containers
were normally 59 cents but on sale for 75% off--so for the grand
total of 15 cents each I came away with several awesome sturdy
containers worthy of letterboxes!

Hope this helps a bit!
