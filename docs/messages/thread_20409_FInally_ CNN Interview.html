From: Phyto (phyto_me@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-21 11:14:13 UTC
That was super short - but that is what you get for TV.
I'd feel kinda funny showing the logbook of a box, but that's just me.
CNN huh? They didn't play up the history or authenticity of the hobby much, that to
me is the more interesting part - how it started and how it has evolved. Not to
mention that the stamp and letterbox placement usually reflect something unique
in the natural environment. I think that should be emphasized more as there is
definitely a concern about leaving no trace...

So, in my opinion - the short skit was pretty watered down.
Due to the history and natural landscape of some areas in Maine, I would never
consider doing something like that. I've pulled most of my accessible clues off the
big list primarily to keep the traffic in boxes to a good level. That is still relatviely
easy to accomplish being as how I'm all the way up here.

The true test remains to be seen - if the hobby can stand the test of time, tv, media
and general hype and come out the same. I would hate for this to really become a
competing hobby with geocaching, and sometimes I think it has the potential.

Thanks for sharing it with us.
