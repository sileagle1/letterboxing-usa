From: Phyto (phyto_me@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-12-13 15:33:47 UTC
In my experience, I have used "Liquid Nails" glue (found at Home
Depot) for mounting most types of rubber to wood.

I mount to aspen boards. Usually wood will work if it's thick enough
and not for a large stamp. If you get up to 2.5x3inch or larger you
need to make sure you have wood that won't warp. Additionally, to
prevent that, I use a wood sealer and acrylic finish that
waterproofs/seals the wood.

A thin coat of light/clear wood varnish is also an option. Wood looks
very nice, it smooths the edges and you can also preserve a beautiful
impression of your stamp on the back - very professional!

I've also seen small, thin and square pieces of slate used. I like that!

Hope that helps
