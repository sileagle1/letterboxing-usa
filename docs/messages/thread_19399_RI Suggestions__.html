From: cpascott (seh-letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-07-09 13:03:44 UTC
Hi Lisa!

I don't live in RI, so I can't answer most of your questions, but
when I went to college in Providence, I spent an afternoon on
Patience and Prudence islands walking around. They are places that
few travel to, I presume.

Given that there is a ferry that travels there, I think it a great
idea to plant a box one of the islands ... in fact, I'd go so far as
to plant TWO -- one on each! What that will do is encourage people
to take time to travel to these islands and explore -- something I
bet most have not done. After all, isn't travelling to neat places
you've not been to before half the appeal of letterboxing?

Best of luck!

CPAScott

Prudence Island is really a neat little place. There's a couple of
sanctuary type places and there's some trails that you could plant
boxes on.

One caution -- There's a type of tick on Prudence Island called the
LoneStar tick. They're kind of an orangy color and look way different
than the usual critters found in the area.
