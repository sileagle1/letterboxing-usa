From: Pamela Smith (smith_pamela@verizon.net) | Date: 2006-08-08 07:22:43 UTC-04:00
The thing to remember is that they do not actually come from paypal
or the bank or whoever, but from someone trying to look like them to
scam you. Poor grammar and/or spelling are often a clue. Never click
on anything in one of these emails. If you have doubts and think it
might be real or are worried about your account, don't click on the
email, but type the relevant website directly into your browser - for
example, type paypal.com in and check your account there. If there
really is a problem, it will also be noted in your account somewhere.
If not, then you can rest easy, delete the email, and go on with your
day.

received suspicious emails about a new name being added to my
account. Checking with PayPal, these were indeed phishing attempts.
I never open these but immediately delete them. Read PayPal's
security policy. They say they will always address you by name if
the email is from them. The phishing attempts do not use your name.

PayPal. So easy, so convenient.
