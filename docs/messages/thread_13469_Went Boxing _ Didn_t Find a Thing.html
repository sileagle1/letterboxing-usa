Funny, I use A to denote the number of Anacondas (consentual reality
or not) I have overcome in my quest for bits of handcarved rubber.

Everyone knows L is for Lizards. Wait, A is for Amy who fell down the
stairs; B is for Basil, devoured by bears... A bit of whimsy from
Edward Gorey for all you doubtful guests out there.

Went to a delightful party last night (thank you VERY MUCH, Sadie
from Nebraska and Dave!) whose hostess had carved and planted a stamp
nearby. This is the Mill St. one in San Luis Obispo, CA. Incredibly
pretty carving, well worth the find. Easy clues and wheelchair
accessible.

We returned the favor by carving a stamp (I designed and JugglerMouse
carved) at the party. Since we had six of 100**100D (Googled) there,
we are counting it as our first E stamp for event. Sade from Nebraska
will be planting it soon so you all can get an impression of it.

No one at the event broke anything except a non-boxing guest who went
into a Koi pond to try to save a lighted juggling ball. His cell
phone looked broken thereafter.

100**100D are now mostly okay with an early October date for a
gathering open to all 'boxers who wish to attend. Later in October
and we get rain. If Ryan is here, we will advertise it that way.
Otherwise, visitors may have to just content themselves with hunting
in a very densely populated letterboxing environment in a very lovely
area.

Just reporting the happenings. Will let you know about that smiling
turtle guy as soon as I hear.

Sir Balthazar of 100**100D
P15 F54 X17 E1 D30 L23
