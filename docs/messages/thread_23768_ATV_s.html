Region. If law enforcement catches them they are fined and their ATVs are
impounded. But they still ride in the woods. There are some plans for a ATV Park.
We have one in Riverhead, I think it cost $10 and is too much and too far for
the under 18 year old who should not be driving without a driver's license
anyway.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-09-08 18:19:23 UTC-04:00
It is stange that some places that frown on letterboxing and geocaching allow ATV's and snowmobiles, which to me seem much more harmful to the environment. Who knows?

In answer to your question about GPS. I see this as just another way to provide varied clues. While not everyone will have a GPS, not every box is for every person. There are some boxes that require a boat and not everyone has access to a boat. There are some that require one to be a master diver and not everyone qualifies for that. I would seek a letterbox that uses GPS coordinates for clues. My disinterest in geocaching is not the GPS, rather it is primarily the trade trinkets versus the stamp.

Go for it!

SpringChick
