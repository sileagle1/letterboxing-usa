Re: [LbNA] Log Book?

From: Rayvenhaus (rayvenhaus@myndworx.com) | Date: 2004-04-10 22:37:10 UTC-07:00
We've not yet had a log book fill up, either personal or one placed with a
stamp, but I plan on memorializing them when they do. In the case of our
personal log book, I'm getting ready to replace it, so I'm going to take and
make a new log book, handmade cover and all, and carefully cut the stamps
from the existing book and then mount those, one to a page, in the
handcrafted one. Add notes and such on the find and make a logbook that
will keep well, and use the old one for the trails. I'm also going to scan
in the stamps so that I have a permanent record of them.

As far as the letterbox log books, I'll more than likely scan those in to
immortalize them and may move them to a new log book as well for future
keeping.

Being new and all, I wasn't quite sure what to use as a logbook. I came
across a zippered 6-hole daytimer-like binder. I punched my own sheets from
cardstock to fit in it (as a bonus I found a Stickapotomous zippered pouch
to keep my stamp and a mini-pad in the front of it. I really like it
because I can add or remove sheets at will, including my stamped pages which
I leave at home to keep them clean when I go out.

From: gryandrowansmum (gryandrowansmum@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-13 03:22:47 UTC
I started out with a strathmore pocket size spiral notebook as a log,
but the cover quickly fell off it. So I cut out the stamps and put
them into another book. Some were stamped front and back (duh!) so I
taped a "flap" thingy so you can lift it and see both sides. I
thought about scrapbooking them (my other hobby!) but really, with 12
scrapbooks and a neverending supply of photos already-do I need one
more!?

Now the logs of boxes, I might. My dad just picked up 2 logs from
boxes in Washington for me and I may scrap those in a small book, but
who knows. I kind of hate to rip up the original book, though.

From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-04-13 16:24:58 UTC-06:00
We bought little notebooks at Target that are a great size and have a
soft plastic type cover. We stamp the stamp on the front side of tha
page and then I print out the clues, which I attach under the stamp. On
the back of the page, we attach a picture of the kids stamping in. When
the book fills, we will start a new book and keep these for memory.

As for LB logs... not sure what we will do with those... good question
=]
