A couple weekends ago I got an E-mail from a fellow letterboxer
asking if I was the one who had planted the Mercy Brown and Gift to
Renee boxes. She had been out and found the Gift to Renee journal had
been filled and the waist high rock under which Mercy Brown had been
buried had been excavated to the other side of the stone wall on the
grounds. A little exploration by her, and she spotted the blue cover
of the Rubbermaid box in the dirt, and from there made arrangements
to get Mercy Brown back to me, still in great shape. I thank her for
what she's done.
My intention was to pick up the Gift to Renee box last weekend,
retire it, and plant Mercy Brown there in it's place. Instead, I
walked the trails on the other side of the road in search of a new
burial spot for Mercy. Some say the trails on the other side don't
offer much for the hiker. Soon they will. There were many places for
Mercy to finally find some peaceful sleep, except for the occasional
letterboxer, and hopefully next weekend I'll get the chance to get
her out there and the new clues posted. Also at that time, Gift to
Renee will be retired. She and I would like to thank all of you who
stopped by, and we hope you will visit Mercy Brown after she's
relocated. Watch here for the new clues.
Bob
