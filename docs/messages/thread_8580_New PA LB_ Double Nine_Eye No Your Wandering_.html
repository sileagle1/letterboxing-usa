NAME: Double NINE (Eye No Your Wandering)
Third Box in the Nolde Forest Series

LOCATION: Nolde Forrest Environmental Education Center, located on
PA Route 625 south of Reading, Berks County, PA.

DATE PLANTED: October 2002

Nolde Forest Environmental Education Center is designed and
maintained to promote environmental education. Bicycles and
motorized vehicles are not allowed. Please hike only on designated
trails. All route choices use existing Fire Roads, Trails, or Paths
(FR/T/P). All boxes in this series are located close to trailsides.
All routes involve elevation changes. Maps of the center are not
entirely accurate.


START HERE: From the location of Double Eight, return to the point
(P). In this area locate a wooden structure and a water source. Stand
facing the water source so that the wooden structure is directly
behind it. Head right, following this named (FR/T/P) through the rows
of Double Pines to a water source on the right. Locate and note the
Heading of the Double Outlets (HDO) at the base. Continue on the same
named (FR/T/P) until it meets another. Zig right, zag left, and
continue on the same named (FR/T/P) passing the Laurel PP on your
left, until you reach some macadam. Follow a heading of 40 degrees to
a stone structure. Find a sign of --TT-- --SS --FF-TT and then
find --TT-- --SS --FF-TT above a clock. Note the number of the
hour hand (NHH) on the clock. Stand with the clock at your back and
note the heading(CH). Go from nearby brass to nearby shiny silver,
then 205 degrees to an area where -cC-NN-LL --LL is at 300 degrees,
shiny silver is at 40 degrees, and a light post is at 280 degrees.
Follow heading 160 degrees to a (FR/T/P) entrance on the right
located before EE 15 and across from a water source. Follow this
(FR/T/P), ignoring both the first (FR/T/P) coming in on the right and
the next coming in on the left until you reach a Y. Go left until
this (FR/T/P) crosses another at an X. Go left, almost DOUBLING Back,
and continue to Netless (Nt) standing on the left. From (Nt), follow
heading CH for (six times NHH) paces, then follow heading HDO for
(three times NHH) paces to Double Nine.
