Man in Stone Letterbox
Date planted: August 20, 2002
Nearest town: Dorset, Bennington County, Vermont
Level of difficulty: Easy

A great place to picnic, especially during foliage season!

On the horseless carriage path between Dorset and Manchester, a row of pearly white teeth stand, placed by man's hand.
Park not on the road, but on the Kelly side.
Read the history and note the date the quarry was first opened.
Under the bridge you must go, straight to the depths.
Four steps down and turn 180 degrees, add the first and last number of the date previously mentioned and go this many steps to where Otto left his mark.
Sight across the depths at 90 degrees to see a face, Otto?, staring back at you. Turn right and make your way around the quarry.
On your way, be sure to count the number of grooves cut in a large block of marble to free it from the earth's hold. Note this number.
Cross the outlet and follow the trail Otto may have walked so many years ago.
Find the face you saw before, Little Brother's on your right.
