From: ribnag (ribnag@users.tmok.com) | Date: 2000-09-28 13:55:41 UTC-04:00
> I've got to ask: what is a Bored Nocturnal Adventurer?
> Someone who is sick of NBC Olympics and actually out
> on the trails with headlamps at night?

Heh... That would do as an explanation...

Seriously, though, RI has *nothing* to do late at night, and
not all of us meet the qualificaitons for "day person".

Also, myself and a few friends discovered (several years ago)
that RI has a lot of interesting historical sites, such as
abandoned military sites, quarries, 17th century and earlier
ruins etc. Those who currently control access to these
places usually frown on visitors, however (for liability
reasons, i presume, since all the most interesting places
could count as "dangerous" ).

The solution to both problems? Go at night.

We don't plunder, or vandalize, just explore.

Then, one day, we had the idea to make a web page
documenting our findings, maps, techniques of avoiding
arrest for tresspassing, good gear to bring for safety,
and the like, for others feeling similarly impressed with
RI's nighttime entertainment scene. We never got around
to putting anything on the page, though, until now.

Anyway, letterboxing at night really beats the heat in the
summer... We just discovered letterboxing this spring (when
we stumbled across the Wickaboxet Firetower Ledge box,
during the day, incidentally), so don't know how well
night-boxing will work in the winter, but...
