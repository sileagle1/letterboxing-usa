I am mostly a lurker on this list, but thought I would comment regarding box vandalism. I'm a fairly casual letterboxer, and although I enjoy doing it, I don't keep very good track of my F's P's etc, and don't even have all my images in an orderly fashion. I also enjoy geocaching, although I have placed only one cache. That cache has been found about 60 times since I planted it a year ago, and has been vandalized three or four times. Some kind 'cacher has always found the remains and done their best to return the cache to it's original condition and location.
The same is true of the 6 or so letterboxes I've planted. Sometimes they turn up missing, get chewed by critters or are vandalized.
I don't take it personally. I just figure someone just happened across them and decided to have some fun at my expense. I can just picture a group of teenaged kids poking around with a stick, finding a plastic box with "stuff" in it, and laughing uproariously (is that a word?) as they scatter the stuff around and fill it with dirt or sticks, or worse!
A couple of times a fellow 'boxer has found the vandalized boxes and repaired them if possible, just as the 'chachers have done.
I know that when I place the boxes/caches I am taking the chance that something will happen to them. I do it anyway, because I enjoy the experience of planning, carving and placing the items as well as the great feeling of my own finds, and hope to duplicate that experience for someone else.
