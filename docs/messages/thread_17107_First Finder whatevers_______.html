> things do you place in them?? I've very, very interested in

Here are some of the more interesting things I've seen in boxes for
the first finders:

A Lewis & Clark peace coin in a Lewis & Clark themed letterbox.

Funhog had been placing small, glass figurines (in the shape of a
hog!) for first finders. (She must have run out of those years ago,
though, since I haven't heard about them in a long time.)

One box I found on the east coast had a bunch of pins for wearing
(six of them). They weren't meant for "first finders" since the pins
were placed long after the box was originally placed, but it could
have been left for first finders as well.

One of my boxes was found by a geocacher and they e-mailed me to let
me know that found it and was interested in the letterboxing hobby,
but they left a small airplane in it specifically for me the next
time I checked up on it. I never have checked up on it since, but
another letterboxer later e-mailed me saying that they found the box
with their child who was absolutely thrilled to death to find that
little plane in it. (Which was okay by me, because I didn't really
want the plane in the first place and loved the idea that it would
put a smile on a kids face.) Again, it wasn't put in there with the
first finder in mind, but it could have been.

But be creative. Darned near ANYTHING could be left in a box for the
first finders. Or even for ALL finders. =)

-- Ryan

I've created a few boxes with themes kids will enjoy (This Little
Pig, Find-Me Frog, Misplaced Marbles, all in western MA) in
which I've put souvenirs for the first 10 finders. I get the
inexpensive souvenirs (tiny plastic pigs, marbles, and plastic
frogs) at a local store. Sometimes I replenish the supply when I
check on the box!

In my Goodnough Dike box at the Quabbin Reservoir (western
MA), I put small photos (about 2" x 3") of the view from the dike in
the box for the first three finders. The idea was that finders could
paste the photo in their logbook.
