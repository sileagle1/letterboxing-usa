From: (berylandtom@comcast.net) | Date: 2003-02-11 18:38:36 UTC-05:00
Hi Lynne,
 
1.  X = eXchange.  That's when you meet another letterboxer and exchange personal stamps in each other's logs.  I met Mary from Virginia at a microbox and eXchanged.  I went to a letterboxing meet, a brunch, and met a bunch of letterboxers and eXchanged a bunch of stamps.
 
2.  HH = Hitchhiker.
 
Now here is where it gets really fuzzy.  P - Placed and F - Found are very straight forward and defined on the website.  But does a HH mean you found a hitchhiker?  What if you place a hitchhiker?  I have heard various interpretations.  For me HH means found.  The one HitchHiker I placed I count as a P.  But when I was letterboxing with a group that found 3 hitchhikers, which I stamp exchanged with but didn't take or move myself, I counted them as eXchanges.  Go figure.  Psychomommy adds L for lizard encounters and lots of other things.  There was a lot of rancorous discussion on the board on the whole subject a short while back. 
 
3.  Yes!  I have been an Edward Gorey fan since the early '70's when Amphigorey was released.  I have a nice collection of his stuff.  My wife made a two foot stuffed animal Doubtful before we were married.  I have a picture of my family and Doubtful standing with Mr. Gorey in front of his house on Cape Cod from 10 years ago. 
 
Do you know about the Gotham Book Shop in New York City?  Are you a member of the Edward Gorey House organization?  www.edwardgoreyhouse.org  My family were pictured in their first newsletter holding Doubtful.  If only we could get together sometime and see each other's collections and talk Gorey.
 
Do you know about "the dustwrapper secret"?
 
There I asked you three questions back. 
 
Tom Johnston aka Scarab of the Doubtful Guests: Scarab, Beryl, Cozy, Kenny and Tuxie
"It was in the letterbox, presumably."

P F X HH ??

We've all been round and round with this. I am glad the Subject
line of this email will keep most people from getting involved in
this thread. :-)

I went back and looked through the controversy this issue stirred up
last November and left it with my head spinning. Do a search
for "too many stats" if you feel masochistic. I was hoping to find
some answers and I didn't really.

So I reread the LBNA website:
http://www.letterboxing.org/faq.html#025

I recommend you read it yourself but I will summarize what I found
over several paragraphs. I wish they would put it all in one place.

P - Placed
F - Found
Other Counts:
X - eXchange (personal stamps eXchanged with others)
E - Event stamp (Event stamps gathered at letterboxing gatherings)

Hitchhikers count in either Placed or Found.

There are some specific guidelines listed for what counts.

After that you are on your own.

I am going to adjust my numbers to reflect this. I do however like
to keep some track of hitchhikers separately so I will count
hitchhikers in P & F but also list them separately.

Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
F54 P14 X11 E1 - HHF1 HHP1

>
> P - Placed
> F - Found
> Other Counts:
> X - eXchange (personal stamps eXchanged with others)
> E - Event stamp (Event stamps gathered at letterboxing gatherings)
>
> Hitchhikers count in either Placed or Found.
>
> There are some specific guidelines listed for what counts.
>
> After that you are on your own.
