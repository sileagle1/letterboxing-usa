> I'd love to attend a gathering. My zest for the extreme sport of
letterboxing has resulted in an injury placing me on the bench for
the remainder of the winter season. After planting my first box on
Martin Luther King Day (clues to be forthcoming shortly) my obsession
for letterboxing magnified. Yesterday using some comp time owed me
at work, I set out at lunch time for the Bowers Spring LB. No more
than 50 paces from my car (thankfully), compass in my left hand,I
slipped on the snow covered trail that had hardened to a smooth coat
of ice. My right hand reached out to break my fall and my wrist.
This is the 2nd time I have diregarded trail warnings, the first
being the incident with the Nobska Light LB and poison ivy. So with
cast in place next Monday and a directive from my orthopdist that I
can not fall again if my wrist is to heal properly, I must be content
with armchair letterboxing for a while. A gathering would be the
perfect fix for this frustrated letterboxer.
By the way, I am grateful for many things regarding this
accident. 1. I am left handed. 2. It wasn't a leg or hip that I
broke. 3. I wasn't a mile into the woods. 4. Learning to heed my
better judgement and not go hiking alone could have been a much more
costly lesson.--------------------Wing Clipped Heron
