From: gingerbreadjunk (gingerbreadjunk@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-19 02:14:53 UTC
you know, when i figured out that this box was located in ****
*****, i figured that i knew ***** **** like the back of my hand. so
when i drove to ** ******, i looked and i looked and i looked. I
drove day and even night looking for the location of this box. I
searched ****** ** every free minute until i gave up. I asked for
clues, and they didn't help me. I asked friends at work who knew *
******** and they didn't have a clue. A few months past and i
desided to go back to **** *****. not only did i not find the box,
but i think i lost my compass. At that point i had spent enough time
searching for this box. it was frustrating me. stupid ***** ****. i
didn't know ** ******* like i thought.

today i asked for more help. i didn't get anymore, but someone said
something. a friend said "your not looking hard enough." i went back
to **** ***** and realized that wasn't the problem. i was looking to
hard. i should have found this box days after it was placed, but
instead, i let my fat head get in the way.

i appreciated the help everyone gave me. i like to think of myself
as an aggressive boxer. every weekend looking for a new box or
placing a new box. in my head, i feel that i need every box in the
state of new hampshire. i want to have the most boxes planted in new
hampshire. i want to be the best. known like the "gods" of
letterboxing, wanda and pete. as some of you know, i am a great guy.
to others, i still may be remembered for my growing pains. either
way, i showed some weakness this week. all i know is, i'm glad i
found such wonderful people to associate with. i may never be like
wanda and pete, but i can try.

Roar Kebob - *** ****** - F150

rockin and rollin in the granite state
david (team new hampshire)
