Meeting other letterboxers probably does happen more often here in Connecticut. We have met several in the last couple weeks. To take some of the mystery out of it, I wear a fishing vest instead of a backpack. I have the LBNA Patch and a pin I made up on the vest. My Jeep has small but readable signs on the rear side windows, "Chuck and Molly Letterboxing Team" and my license plate is my last name. Having this on my car did get us some exchanges. The most unusual was from a fellow letterboxer that parked next to us at the Mansfield McDonalds, saw the sign and luckely spoke up. The most recent was a couple days ago when another car pulled up next to us at a parking area for a box. They also spoke up first and we got to meet some more boxers. Meeeting people at the box itself has happened but only once or twice. Not very often...Chuck and Molly P77F433X131

From: noreester (SJinNJ@aol.com) | Date: 2003-06-14 05:19:13 UTC
I've never officially run into another letterboxer on the trails.
But, one time, when I was checking on my own boxes on Pyramid Mt. I
passed a couple hiking. For some reason, they struck me as boxers,
but I didn't say anything, I kept on my way. I then ran into another
couple, and I knew they were looking for boxes, but again I didn't
say anything. I had been hiking all day (at that time it was
possible to check all my boxes in one day & still look for other
folk's boxes) and I was tired and just wanted to go home.
A couple days later, reading this board I saw that Forester met up
with (I can't for the life of me remember the names of the folks that
planted the Weiss Ecology box & several others in NJ) a couple
searching for the Pyramid Mt. series on the day I was there. I'm
positive I passed both couples.
That was the closest I've actually come to meeting other boxers, but
who knows what the future will bring...
