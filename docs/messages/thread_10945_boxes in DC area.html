From: thedoubtfulguests <thedoubtfulguests@yahoo.com> (thedoubtfulguests@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-02-27 04:40:34 UTC
Ooops, uh wellll...we have snow. In fact we are getting more right
now. Schools are closed tomorrow. By Sunday, who knows. A lot of
the snow downtown that Music Woman encountered was mostly melted by
yesterday. It all depends on the temperatures between now and Sunday.

The mall circuit includes Smithsonian (if it's still there, not heard
for sure on that one), Orion, Independence, the Inventor, Skull and
Bones, and Mr. Jefferson. You can make a circle in that order. I'd
take a taxi from Smithsonian to Orion and start your walk from
there. Understand that the mall is a desert, a veritable wasteland
lacking in amenties like stores and eateries. Especially down by
Lincoln you can think you're in the middle of a forest. But at least
you are walking around one cool toursit site after another in tourist
land, and you said you wanted to tourist. Walk through the Korean
War Memorial and commmisserate with the statues of cold looking
soldiers.

Independence and Jefferson are in cute tiny little boxes hard to find
under snow. But I understand someone recently found Independence and
baggied it so maybe it wouldn't be so hard.

Taft is a film cannister lying on the roots of a tree. Again,
depends on how much snow is left.

Year of the Ram, Chinatown is up off the ground (hint, hint). It's a
Holiday is a short metro ride, all indoors, to Pentagon City Mall
just across the river in VA. It has a big atrium food court. Egypt
Explorer is off the Mall path, and way public scary, but at National
Geographic which is pretty cool.

The next question is where you are staying, if you are in or outside
the city and if you have a car.

I'd love to meet up with you on Monday or Tuesday for lunch and
exchange since I work downtown. Write me at
theDoubtfulGuests@yahoo.com.

Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
