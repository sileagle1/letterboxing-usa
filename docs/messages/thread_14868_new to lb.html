From: Wanda (wsthm@hotmail.com) | Date: 2003-08-06 15:06:27 UTC
Kat, welcome! :) I am also one of the 'Family Fun newbies'.And like
you and your children, my grandson and I are going on our first hunt
today. We are in South Texas, and Texas doesnt have all that many
letterboxes either...122, I think... and being such a large state,
many are much to far flung to be able to just 'pick up and go' to
get them. In our area, there are only 6 planted within drives of
about an hour in various directions.

If things go right today though I should be able to plant the 7 I
have made though. After we find our first letterbox, we plan to
drive to another area and plant them. Then my #3 son and I hope to
drive to yet another area and plant more later this week, if I
manage to get more stamps carved and boxes filled. He was at the
area overnight yesterday, and scouted around for some good locations.

I can't answer any of your questions, but I 'think' a 'micro-box' is
one that is very small, such as a plastic film canister or an
Altoids box... maybe. The letterboxes I have made and hope to plant
today, are one regular sized one (Rubbermaid square box) and six
film canisters. I was greatly encouraged by the postings about it
not being necessary to include a stamp pad in planted boxes....
therefore, the small film canister letterboxes...

Well, it is time to go pick up my letterboxing partner. I hope to
hear how your hunt went later today.... I know I will report as soon
as I can when we get home. ;D

Have fun!!!!
Wanda (Buzy Bee)
