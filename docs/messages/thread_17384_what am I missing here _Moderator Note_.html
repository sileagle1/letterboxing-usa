Hear hear Funhog (Here hear? Hear here? Here here? Oh, whatever)

It does seem odd that as a letterboxing group, we would be forbidden to
discuss issues that so deeply divide us. Yet history has shown that we are
unable to resolve these fundamental differences through rational discourse
and respectful compromise. Why is this? One reason that leaps to mind is that
we have no formal governing body, nor any rules for resolving disagreements
in the letterboxing world. Thus disagreements are not resolved to anyone's
satisfaction, tempers flare, and those in charge of the various venues in the
letterboxing world are forced to perform judicial duties ad hoc.

Perhaps we need some sort of Letterboxing Loya Jurga??

Something to chew on?

-Isosceles
