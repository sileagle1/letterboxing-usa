From: Karen Ruhl (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-09 17:51:35 UTC-07:00
Here's an old cheer I recall, Chrissy ...

Fight, exuberantly, fight! Compel them to relinquish the ball!

Okay, okay, today's kids have a different vernacular, but I still chuckle
when I hear this one.

Even though kids are already beyond their years, I love hearing them cheer
this one ...

Give me an A (cheerleaders form the letter A; fans cheer -A-)
Give me a double-L (cheerleaders raise both arms; fans cheer -double L-)
Give me an eye (cheerleaders point to an eye; fans cheer -I-)
Give me a gator (cheerleaders curl their hands; fans yell gator and mimic
gesture)

Alligator, Alligator, eat'em up, eat'em up (everyone opens and closes arms
in an alligator-like fashion)

(repeat for effect)

... drives my principal husband crazy; it's good to be a kid, even if it's
only one night a week.

~speedsquare
