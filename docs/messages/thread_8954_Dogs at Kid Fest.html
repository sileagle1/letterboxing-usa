From: Janet (moonstone_baby@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-11-06 18:56:33 UTC
Hi there,

I own 3 black Labs. The black lab named Sky at the Kids Day
Gathering is one of mine. I love taking my dogs with me
letterboxing. I am careful to only take them to places that allow
dogs. I have a paw print stamp and stamp in whatever dogs I happen
to have with me that day. I also have florescent orange vests for my
dogs during hunting season. On Sunday I went out letterboxing with a
friend who brought 3 of her black labs. All total we had 5 black labs
hiking with us that day. It took me a while to stamp in all those
dogs!

From: A.D. (alwayschaos@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-11-06 14:29:47 UTC-08:00
We box with our beagle, Cajun Buddy, who has a
Snoopy-ish stamp of his own. Unfortunately, he's
getting older and is epileptic so we are limited to 3
miles and under while hiking with him. (thank you
everyone who indicates on the clues how long the hike
is!)

Last summer, while doing Randy's Blue Mountain
Mystery, we underestimated how much hiking we would do
once we were in the actual area and my husband ended
up carrying him the last 1/2 mile out. Poor old dog
slept the whole rest of the day. Poor old husband did,
too. ;o)

While stamping in at Laurette's Blistering Blue
Barnacles, Tig and I met a lady whose sheltie had his
feet all wrapped up due to some injury. She didn't
want to leave him behind while walking her other dog
so she pushed him along in a baby carriage!

Buddy loves hiking with us, but we are saddened that
we can't always take him along on the longer outings.

PS- he's a true believer in a little bit of that
powerbar Daddy slips him. ;o)
