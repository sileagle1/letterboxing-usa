From: Gretchen Caldwell (boston.rott@verizon.net) | Date: 2006-06-20 13:31:42 UTC-04:00
I'm not trying to be argumentative, but if you're going by the accuracy of a
map, Chestnut Hill is a village of Newton. It is not part of Brookline.
I know that the mall is right there on the border, but truly, CH is part of
Newton. Newton is one of several cities that I work in as a paramedic, as
are the towns of Natick and Wayland.



Cochituate is part of Wayland. Cochituate state park is in Natick, but
Cochichuate itself is in Wayland.



If you want really good maps, grab one of the Yellow "Arrow" map books
(Metro Boston, in this case)..they have all the villages and boro's listed.
We get the screwiest addresses when people call 911 and you can really bang
your head against a wall at times. If you don't have the right set of
maps (or the right "old timer" in the truck) you'll never find Piety Corner
in Waltham..but we get sent there all the time for accidents.



Boston Rott (who is intimately familiar with most of the stupid nuances of
Metro West)
