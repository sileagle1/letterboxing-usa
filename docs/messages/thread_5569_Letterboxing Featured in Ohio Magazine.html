Letterboxing Featured in Ohio Magazine

From: Randy & Becky Adams (3dognight@cityofbryan.net) | Date: 2002-05-30 22:03:29 UTC-04:00
Letterboxing is featured in the June issue of Ohio Magazine. We have not
seen it yet, except online. Our letterbox, which was featured in the
article, went missing on Memorial Day. Two new, enthusiastic letterboxers
were on the trail of finding their first letterbox and they would have been
the first finders of this box when it was reported "missing in action". This
is very disappointing for the planters as well as the would-be finders. I
know this happens frequently. How do you deal with it? It is especially
disappointing for us after having it featured in the magazine.

Randy and Becky Adams
Ohio

Well...it's something of a catch-22...if there's an article about
letterboxing in a largely distributed magazine...more poeple are
going to go to the web site to see clues and we've got to figure
that, along with introducing more poeple to LBNA...it's also going to
increase chances of vandalism, complaints, and missing boxes.
Some small amount of publicity is ok.....but, in my opinion, the more
visible this sport becomes, the problems we'll have. I'm not sure if
articles like this are a boon and a bite.
