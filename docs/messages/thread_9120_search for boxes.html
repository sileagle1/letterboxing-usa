From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@douglasgerlach.com) | Date: 2002-11-11 14:49:02 UTC-05:00
newltrbxr wrote:
>This looks so cool, I can't wait to get started still have to get my
>materials together...one thing, if I want to look for boxes in my
>area, how do I go about this? I live in the Buffalo, NY area, and I
>did a search of the archives, and turned up nothing.

Go to http://www.letterboxing.org/ and click on "CLUES" -- eventually
you'll end up at the NY state page
-- http://www.letterboxing.org/ny/ny.htm and you can see the clues in your
part of the state. The map shows that the county surrounding Buffalo
has/had at least one box.

If there aren't many in your area, that's your clue to start placing your
own boxes! The oft-repeated mantra is that "if you place them, they will
come."

There are a number in NY State further east of Buffalo, so you may wish to
take a day trip to track down a few of those boxes before you start making
lots of your own boxes, so that you get a feel for how boxes are hidden and
clues are created.

Welcome aboard, and good luck!
