From: cpascott (seh-letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-06-23 20:43:12 UTC
Not being Christian, I wasn't familiar with these quotes, but your
statement that they are misquotes intrigued me, so I looked them up.

Both quotes seem to be from the King James Version of the Bible.

The first quote is from John 1:11 -- "He came unto his own, and his
own received him not." The latter words "but went down" do not seem
to appear in the actual text. This passage is also the title of a
poem by Mary Elizabeth Coleridge
(http://www.bartleby.com/236/255.html)

The second quote is from Proverbs 1:7 -- "The fear of the LORD is the
beginning of knowledge: but fools despise wisdom and instruction."
Again, the latter words "and will not be lead across" do not seem to
appear in the actual text.

Whether any of this is important, I don't know. I'm still struggling
with the five letter cryptics.

CPAScott

cpascott wrote:
Boy, I'm writing alot on this thread, huh?

Here's the thing. This cryptogram was put together using a method
known as columnar transposition. The questions we have to answer in
order to figure it out are:

1. Was it single or double columnar transposition?
2. What is/are the keyword(s)?

I'm certain the quotes are provided to help us figure out what the
keyword(s) is/are. Two quotes may indicate two key words, and thus
double columnar transposition (which is more difficult to decode than
singular). Once we know the keyword(s) however, figuring out the
text of the cryptogram is relatively easy.

Send me an e-mail at seh-letterbox@comcast.net and we can finish this
analysis. Answers should NOT be posted to the group!

CPAScott
