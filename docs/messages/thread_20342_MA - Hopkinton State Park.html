MA - Hopkinton State Park

From: Warrior Woman (warrioringilead@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-15 18:04:48 UTC
I went to Hopkinton State Park today to do a maintenance check on
Marathon (it's just fine) and to hunt for Danielle's 15th and the
Homer Series, since they were planted while I was in RI this summer.

WARNING WILL ROBINSON!

THE YELLOW JACKET HORNETS ARE OUT IN FULL FORCE. PLEASE USE CAUTION
WHEN BOXING IN THIS PARK!

I was on the trail looking for the "Pride Rock" box when I felt
something itching at my ear. I had my nose pressed to the clue
sheet, and just randomly swatted. I was standing still. PERFECTLY
STILL. Another annoying buzz. I finally look up and I am *COVERED*
(and I do mean COVERED - the Park Ranger who found me running and
screaming down the trail into the parking lot can attest to this)
with yellow jacket hornets. 27 bites in all!

I was giving myself this letterboxing tour as my own birthday present
to myself (next Sunday)!

And to think - how am I going to tell Mary(RI)? I am going to be her
bridesmaid this Saturday!

I'm off to double dose on Benadryl, take a shot of whiskey, and sit
in an epsom salt cool bath for the rest of the day. Doctor's
orders. Really. I love my doctor.

I'll finish the series AFTER the wedding.

Warrior Woman
