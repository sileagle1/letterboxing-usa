Cheap source for carving materials and pads

From: zed_boxing (szorzi_1999@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-01-12 03:59:01 UTC
For those fortunate enough to live where there are "Dollar Tree"
stores, I've already mentioned them as a source of great, cheap
carving erasers. (The "Be Sharp" brand, with 5 to a pack for $1 - 1
large and 4 small.) Really unbelievably carve-able for such a cheap
price!!!

They also have, from time to time, some collections of (rather ugly)
rubber stamps for kids, that are a great source for tiny stamp
pads! These pads come 2 to a pack with several commercial kiddie
stamps included. The stamps are 1 inch by 1 1/4 inch and have a
raised pad and are encased in plastic with a clear plastic cover.
Perfect for small boxes! I buy these by the handful and give the
stamps to kids. I buy a larger pad for them to use and keep the
mini's for me.

Right now, they also have a kit of tiny valentine stamps with a pad
that is 1 3/4 inch square in a hinged plastic case. Unfortunately,
the pad is not raised - but you could remove the pad - the pad
itself is about 1 inch square. I'm even going to experiment with
raising it, using some foam material, and putting it back in the
case - I think that might work. This kit is also just $1 for the
whole thing - give the 6 mini stamps to the kiddies.

They also have, in the toy department, a set of 3 tiny pads with a
decorative, brightly-colored foam cover and closed with a snap.
Included are 3 short ink pens, also covered in foam. Both the pads
and the pens have "key chain" fobs which are easily removed. Again,
these are great for small boxes.
