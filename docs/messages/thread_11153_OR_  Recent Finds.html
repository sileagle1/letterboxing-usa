Hey All!

We've been out and about letterboxing here and there. John got Forest
Frog last week, but, alas, I had to work, so he soloed on that hike.

Together, however, we have managed since Saturday to get Sol Stamper's
Nature Books #1 & #2 and Funhog's Oaks Bottom Letterboxes.

Oaks Bottom was a bit tricky, but luckily we trudged on and found where
we were really supposed to be! What a great letterbox. As usual a
great stamp and logbook left by Karen!
Sol Stamper's Nature Box #1 is in an interesting spot. I do worry about
its location, as the "plastic wall" is probably only temporary and is
barely hidden by leaves (we recovered it well, though), plus there is
tons of traffic in that area. I do love the stamp! Not only that, but
this box is mere steps from my Bike Route letterbox, so I was able to
check on that one, too. It has had two visitors since it was placed.
Had I known how easy it is to get to from Oaks Bottom I would have
included instructions from there, as well. Maybe I still can, Webmaster
willing. Then again, if people can figure it out themselves from Oaks
Bottom, then it will be more of a challenge, I suppose. They can just
get to the general area by reading Nature Book #1's clues, and then
figure it out from there. Either way, it's kind of a cute stamp if I
do say so myself. Definitely fits the area where it's placed.

Thanks to Heather and Karen for placing wonderful letterboxes for us to
seek out this past week.

Best regards,

Amanda Briles
