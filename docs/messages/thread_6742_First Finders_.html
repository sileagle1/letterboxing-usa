From: Jesse Vanek (jvanek1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-08-08 19:15:22 UTC-07:00
I'd like to weigh in on the whole "first finders"
discussion.... I rushed out today to find a letterbox
in Suffield, CT to try to be the "first," only to
discover that someone beat me to it! I personally
don't understand why both attitudes can't exist
simultaneously - for those of us who like the
challenge and competition of trying to be the first to
find a box, certificates sound like a great idea.
Perhaps we could add a tag to our signatures that
identifies how many times we were first?

Meanwhile, for those of you who see letterboxing
should be a completely non-competitive activity,
simply skip the first page of the log when reading
through prior entries! If you don't care whether you
are first or fifty first, that's great - but in that
case, if it is to be completely non-competitive,
what's the point of keeping count of how many you've
found? Is that really that much different from trying
to be first to a box?

In my opinion, the flexibility to participate at
whatever level you choose is what makes letterboxing
so much fun. It's an interesting and fun discussion
to have, but also rather unnecessary.

Jesse (aka Chester Copperpot)
