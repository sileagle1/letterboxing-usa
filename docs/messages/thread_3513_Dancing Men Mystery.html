FINALLY! We nailed this one, too, Jay. We found the exact spot this summer
on our first try, but... no box! (I can brag better than you can! ;-p ) We
went back and looked again when we heard that someone else had found it just
a couple of weeks before we tried. Then Pete and Wanda found the missing
box had been tossed into the woods and replaced it. Thankx, Pete and Wanda!
This was a really fun mystery box, and worth bragging about. I'll glue the
Diploma in our book. I wonder what the stamp *says*... :-) I gotta redo
the clues now!

Would you believe the mosquitos and ticks were still out looking for warm
blood? They show up well on a field of blaze orange, though.

We of the Nocturnal Women's Hiking Guild (only 2 members to date ~ and we
haven't decided on the name. Maybe we should just join RIBNAG?) also found
in good condition the new Point Jude box and the Black Rock Point box, and
have a new-to-us hiker for whom we must find a ride (Burr was still hanging
out with the Sherlock crowd and is warming up in my backpack at present).
That means more 'boxing this weekend... in the light of day, mostly! There
are still so many in RI we have yet to get that we will stay close to home
and concentrate on getting them before the clocks are turned back, further
limiting the daylight available.

I can hardly wait until Betz and Batik want to venture out west to CT. Your
state is a letterboxing goldmine!

Mary of Mary and Betz
