Miss Moon - please let me say I was in no way making a personal attack on your position
or insinuating that you were whining. I thought I was speaking in general. I was hoping
there was an obvious solution that I was overlooking and that someone would enlighten
us. I do agree with your position the stagnant boxes should not be on the active list.
Perhaps the webmasters might add a function that would allow the box planters one
mouse-click to put the letterbox(es) into the basement, and attaching a note when the
box(es) would likely be reactivated. The concientious and active letterboxers would
probably clear up most of their own stagnant boxes. We'll never get 100% compliance,
and thank goodness. I would miss some pretty beautiful places. I have about an 89%
finding rate at this point, which I find totally acceptable.

I would have time to maintain my own plantings. I have had some already go missing and
they are not on the list. I have also recarved two stamps. I send an email to the placer
after every find with a report. Some thank me, some don't. I have started to place my
boxes at www.atlasquest.com as well. Some people are very social and others wish for
mystery. I think I would choose not look for more boxes by the same planter if I could not
find their boxes and they wouldn't return an email. I can understand the frustration.
Perhaps you can spend some time planting more boxes closer to home and others will
sprout soon.
