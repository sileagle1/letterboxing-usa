From: rscarpen <RiskyNil@hotmail.com> (RiskyNil@hotmail.com) | Date: 2003-02-28 20:09:25 UTC
San Luis has needed an influx of addicted letterboxers, so I for one
am thrilled to death to have someone else start hiding them on a
regular basis! =)

> What all are folks using for carving material? I got the thick
> white stuff (Staetdler's?) and it carves very nicely (says "it
> carves like butter").

Most people either use erasures, but they tend to be limited in size
and scope so I use the "pink stuff" by Speedball which is also very
common. (Ask Emily if you want to see some--I know she's got a
little!) Staetdler's stuff--more commonly known as Mastercarve
(that's the name of the product--Staetdler is the company that makes
it) is very good. (I especially like that they write their moto, "it
carves like butter" in Spanish too! Se corte como mantequilla.)
It's really nice because it's so thick--you can even carve on both
sides of it if you want. Or even the EDGE of the material! I also
love the smell of the stuff. It's a little more expensive than the
pink stuff--probably for the extra thickness, but it's very good
stuff. If there's one thing I'll say NOT to carve with, it's
the "white stuff" by Speedball. Crumbles faster than you can
imagine. I think it's called Speedycut. Avoid like the plauge!

> I saw the stuff you use UV light to set. Anybody doing that much
> detail?

I was looking at that stuff at Laws not to long ago. I've never
heard of anyone trying it, but I find the idea fascinating. I was
half tempted to try it just to see how it works, but then I saw the
price and thought better of it! But if you want to give it a whirl,
I'd love to hear how it works!

> As far as carving tools, I am using an X-acto Woodcarving Knife and
> the Speedball Cutter Set. Anybody using anything else?

Most people use X-acto knives and/or the Speedball cutting tools.
Myself included. Not sure what else someone might try using.

> Do riddle clues just discourage?

Depends on the person. There's nothing wrong with riddle clues--some
people PREFER them, but other don't. I say make any kind of clues
you want--I for one love particularly clever, witty, or "different"
types of clues. Just keep in mind that not everyone enjoys riddles,
and they might pass by your boxes and look for "easy" ones instead.
There's nothing wrong with that, though. If you make riddle clues,
there WILL be people who'll go out of their way to track them down!
=)

> How about cuckoo clues; folks think this is rude?

Some people enjoy it. Some don't. *shrug* The story of every
letterbox. I'm not big on cuckoo clues, though. I'd rather go out
and deliberately hunt a box down in the first place! But there's an
equally dedicated group of people that wouldn't want it any other
way.

Farewell!

-- Ryan
