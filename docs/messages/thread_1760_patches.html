From: (cstearns@neca.com) | Date: 2001-02-08 22:22:32 UTC
Did you notice everyone has a good point. I am thinking in terms of
patches in different forms. Never to diminish the thrill of victory
but as a momento of a certain number of victories. As a way to start
a conversation, and expand the circle of letterboxing enthusiasts. I
think of girl and boy scouts with sashes of badges, it never
minimizes the need or value of first aid to have the badge declaring
you passed. Young people need all the encouragement to continue to
pursue the educational and healthy pass-time that brings them a new
sense of themselves and the boundries they have pursued and
conquered. I know my son will wear a count badge with the pride and
memory of every step taken in earning it. He'll remember the first
box, the deepest snow, the highest hill, the best stamp,the box he
did with friends and like a trip through a log book the memories
will come back just looking at it. So we should plan and be sure we
get what we want they are going to be very special for some. Thanks
for all the dialogue this is a wonderful forum for all of us to
learn and growq with our sport. Carol
