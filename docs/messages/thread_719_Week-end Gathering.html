[LbNA] Week-end Gathering

From: Tom Cooch (tcooch@mail.sover.net) | Date: 1999-11-04 18:51:46 UTC
Tomorrow evening the first batch of east-coast letterboxers will be
gathering at the Inn at Long Trail in Killington, Vermont. The
weather forecast for Saturday is brisk - 40 to 45 highs, with a
chance of rain or snow in the morning. So bring warm clothes.

The inn will be very cozy: wood fires and a hot tub. Bring a bathing
suit and a bathrobe.

There are three letterboxes there at Sherburne Pass. I will have
clues available for them, as well as for the other 60+ boxes within
an hour's drive. The four of us arriving tomorrow can make our plans
in the tub tomorrow evening or next morning at breakfast. We can hunt
together or alone, as people like.

Saturday night will be the big gathering, as Erik & Susan, Randy,
Bonnie, and Steve of ValleyQuest arrive. Clues for the McGrath's Pub
Letterbox will be given out once everyone is on hand. The Wind That
Shakes the Barley will be playing in the pub that evening, while the
letterboxers stumble about garnishing clues or retiring to table to
recoup. Jay Drew may conceivably arrive around midnight to assess the
state of the first North American Letterboxers Convention. Who knows
- perhaps the Moorland Wizard will pop in to sample the local
microbrews? (There is Guinness on tap as well, Graham, if Long Trail
Ale seems dubious!)

I can't wait.

Tom
