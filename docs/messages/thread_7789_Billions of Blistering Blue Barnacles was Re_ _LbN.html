I headed over to Jerusalem after work yesterday, dog in tow, to check out
the barnacles on the other side of town. It had been a stressful day, and I
was hoping a little boxing would help me decompress. It did. It was
wonderful. The late afternoon sun was hitting at just the perfect angle,
and across the way the hard-bitten port of Galilee was looking postcard
perfect. For the first time in a long time I was able to look at those
docks without thinking about the hard work and endless worries going on over
there, and simply enjoy the beauty of the place. On the other side of the
peninsula a great egret was strutting it's stuff in the shallow waters, and
just as I was wondering if any osprey had made use of the nest in the clues,
one landed on the ground less than 30 feet away. It spread it's wings and
bobbed its' head at us, letting me know just who this little patch of
unspoiled land really belonged to. I've never been this close to an osprey.
It was magnificent.

The box was well hidden, but easy to find. Given my recent work history, I
got a real chuckle out of the stamp. I wasn't the first to find this box,
but I was the first to stamp in. Pete and Wanda must have been in such a
hurry to get there first that they rushed out without theirs. ;>)

BTW, Bosco found the whole experience to be an exercise in frustration.
Bunnies, bunnies everywhere, and a danged human-on-a-string holding him
back. Rats!

Thank you, Laurette, for this chance to get away from it all right in my own
backyard.

Mare
