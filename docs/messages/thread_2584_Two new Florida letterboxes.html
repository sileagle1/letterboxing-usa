Two new Florida letterboxes

From: Eric Mings (elm@letterboxing.com) | Date: 2001-06-17 17:46:57 UTC-04:00
I don't know about others, but I had a very nice day kayaking down a
beautiful spring fed river in central Florida. Saw many gators,
turtles, waterfowl, and other creatures. The highlight of the day was
hearing a rustle in the bushes behind us and turning to see a doe
leading her young fawn across the river not twenty yards back!

Both letterboxes are on Rock Springs Run in Seminole county with the
nearest city being Apopka.

Common clue:

You must find where the king has landed
there you can bring your own or rent a ride from him.
Plan for a day adventure as there is nowhere to get out for about 9
miles of slow wandering river bank.

Rocks Springs #1

follow the flow until you must choose a path or go aground
You could choose wrong but I would choose right
If you make the correct choice you will see a clump of trees growing
in the water very soon. Check them out!

Rock Springs #2

Find where the big buck deer sleeps
when you see some sign of him take 25 paces into the woods
look to your left for a bent palm and rotten tree stump.

These are also geocache boxes, but don't cheat and look up the
coordinates there unless you really think you need to do that ;-)
