Many books are like letterboxing - there can be subtle clues to your destination.

In the first book, Fellowship of the Ring, in the chapter The Council of Elrond, Gandalf says he went to Orthanc to consult with Saruman (not knowing he'd gone over to hte bad side).
"But I rode to the foot of Orthanc (Saruman's tower), and came to the stair of Saruman; and there he met me and led me up to his high chamber. He wore a ring on his finger."
All the other rings were accounted for, and Saruman knew better than to accept a dwarven ring, so I figure he made one to be like the Big Bad guy.

So it doesn't have much to do with letterboxing, just a shameless plug for reading books. No movie could do true justice to these particular books without being a week long.
