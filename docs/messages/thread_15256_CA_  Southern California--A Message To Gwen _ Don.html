Gwen & Don,
I had such a good time finding your Long Beach State letterboxes (on
7/5/03). The first two, "49er Mascot of CSULB," and "Catching Some
Sun In The Quad," at over two years old are by far the oldest
letterboxes in my stamp book. When I pulled the "Catching Some
Sun..." letterbox from its hiding place, it was covered by snails.
As for your newer (planted 10/29/02) Cal State letterbox, "The Real
49er," I'm proud to say that after more than 9 months planted, I'm
the first stamp. The clue to this one is very tricky, but that makes
it all the more fun to find.

Last Sunday, I went after your "Seal Beach Town Tour." Decoding your
clues was brutal; I'm sure it was just as brutal writing them. But I
can't think of a better place to be sitting in front of historic
plaques for 20 minutes at a time on a sunny day in August then in
this lovely beach town. I spent all day and found all of your LBs,
and even had time to take a side trip to find a nearby Mystery Box
planted by another LBer (mums the word). This after arriving at the
pier at 7:30 AM, and having a mid-day break in an Irish Pub on Main
Street. I was 2nd stamp in all of your letterboxes save one, in
which I was 1st stamp"Fish In The Reads"! Congratulations on your
100th place!! Your "Smooth Sailing On 101" was my 50th find.
--Buzzard P6 F55
