From: (RMORGAN762@aol.com) | Date: 2006-05-11 09:36:18 UTC-04:00
Medallions are made to be nailed onto a wooden hiking staff and
sometimes are sometimes painted with a nice enamel design.

They remind me of the time way back when I would buy a patch for each
state visited and folks would have them sewn onto their puffy goose
down vests..LOL.

The ones I see in parks are usually a little curved to fit a pole but
it looks like this one is flat, all are around an inch square and some
have a couple nail holes.

While I have a nice staff I made last week, I'm still using the
right ski pole I bought as a pair for $2.93 at the Salvation Army store
as I like to stick the point into the ground when I need two free hands.
