From: Sarah Davidson (sadie_russ@msn.com) | Date: 2003-01-28 23:39:10 UTC-05:00
I'm not sure 'Boxing would stand on its own as a Merit Badge. You would at least have to add a short history of Letterboxing and discussion and demonstration of different carving mediums and tools. I think planting at least 2 boxes - one involving a hike with map and compass, another perhaps at a site of historical or civic significance should be required.
Maybe as a start, it could be an alternate requirement in the orienteering MB.
I do think Letterboxing would make for a fun event at an orienteering day or camp-o-ree.

I doubt that a merit badge would be approved. I think the scouts are
trying to keep the number of merit badges from growing too much. It
looks like they will delete older badges to make room for newer ones.
The Signaling and Sheepfarming badges are some of the casualties.
There would likely be too much overlap from existing badges. The
orienteering badge comes to mind. They (BSA) might approve a patch or
other award as they do for snorkling, historic trails, 50 miler, etc.

However, Letterboxing could certainly be an outlet for some of the
applications of skill and knowledge. Is that part of the process of
gaining an award? Not to mention mentoring and activity direction
for the olders Scouts.

I know nothing of the system for the Scouts, but if you read my clues
for the three boxes I planted for the Kennedy Creeks series, I tried
to use skills and knowledge of Botany, Native Culture, Compass use.
