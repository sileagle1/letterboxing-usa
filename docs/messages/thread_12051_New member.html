From: pkleingers (pam@kleingers.net) | Date: 2005-08-16 07:36:56 UTC-04:00
Kathy,



This is just my personal opinion, but I would suggest you wait to place a
box until you have found a few in the area you are considering. I think it
is helpful to see what "works" best as far as concealment and packaging.



I know you have read the FAQs (good for you!) but nothing replaces
experience. I learned the most when I was able to hook up with another
local boxer or two. When you do start hunting, be sure to rehide the box
carefully for the next lucky treasure hunter!



You will have a great time in Connecticut-the mesa of letterboxing. I just
returned from a trip up there.



There is a newboxers group that you would probably enjoy; I am sorry I do
not have the link. It is another yahoo group. And I bet there is a local
group for the Florida or South East as well. Enjoy yourself and welcome to
the community!

*****While I agree with Mama Stork wholeheartedly - I
had big plans to plant my first box before I ever
found one, then I found one and realized I may not be
as quick as I thought I would be in planting - but I
do believe that you shouldn't wait TOO long to plant
one.

We had a discussion on the Texas list not too long ago
about how planters make better finders and re-hiders.


Once you check out the local situation and see what's
customary in your area, go for it! Don't be
heartbroken if you lose some at first, because you
probably aren't as good a hider as you will become.
Just persevere!

Lucy
