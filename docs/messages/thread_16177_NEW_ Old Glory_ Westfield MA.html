Old Glory
Parker Memorial Park, Westfield MA
(Hampden County)

Placed: October 3, 2003 by TehutiKa, AsetKa and kids,
and Rayne and Gillian, too!
Distance: Irrelevant
Time: 15 Minutes
Difficulty: Easy

Parker Memorial Park is on West Silver St., across
from Westfield Middle School South.

Parker Memorial Park is a small urban park dedicated
to military personell from previous wars. There are
multiple monuments on the grounds, from conflicts as
recent as Vietnam all the way back to the Revolution.
This box is dedicated to all of the men and women from
Westfield that served their country.

As you walk into the park, you will see a fountain
towards the back. During the winter months, this
fountain is closed down. To the right of the fountain,
slightly behind it, is a pair of trees that have bent
over backwards for each other. Standing next to these
trees, with the trees on your left, walk straight
ahead 15 steps. Directly on your left will be a
retaining wall for the fountain. Under the top lip of
rock, concealed by a rock and a piece of moss, is the
box. Please rehide this box very carefully. You'll
understand when you see the hiding spot. It is
practically the only place in the entire park that
could conceal a box for any length of time. Happy Hunting!
