Re: [LbNA] Northern CA gathering

From: Lea Shangraw Fox (princesslea@alamedanet.net) | Date: 2003-06-04 16:50:32 UTC
I too am extremely encouraged by the enthusiatic responses regarding a Northern California gathering. I just returned from a visit with Amanda from Seattle because I was chomping at the bit to go letterboxing, and have almost exhausted the supply here in the Bay Area. To give you an idea, I had 32 boxes found when I left (and many of them hard won), and I added 37 more in the span of 4 days while in the Northwest! More than doubled my count.

Personally I see the Bay Area as a half-way point (sort of) between the Sacramento and San Luis areas. I also believe that a gathering is the kind of thing that is needed to give a developing area a little push to start growing even faster, which is just what we need here. San Luis is already well established, and could certainly support a gathering of their own in addition to something up here (they are separated by 4 hours, so really two different entities in my mind).

I too would vote for an East Bay site, primarily because that's where I've planted most of my boxes, and also I've heard that there was an article in the local Contra Costa newspaper so there are several newbies out there. I am personally in Alameda County, right next door and we have lots of good parks here as well. Can I be on the planning committee? One of the places Amanda took me to was Fort Steilacoom where the Seattle gathering had taken place. There were 20 boxes planted in one park - heavenly! There is hardly anything in San Francisco itself or the Peninsula, so they don't seem like good candidates for the gathering.

-- Princess Lea

P.S. We can probably get Amanda down here for the gathering too!
