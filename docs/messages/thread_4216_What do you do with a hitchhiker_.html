Today I found my first hitchhiker and have several questions on what do I do next. This hitchhiker had it's own separate box planted with the one I was looking for. The person who planted it wants me to email him as to it's new location. Do I tell them where I am going to plant it or where I found it and keep the new location secret? From: rscarpen (RiskyNil@hotmail.com) | Date: 2002-01-19 00:16:31 UTC
> Today I found my first hitchhiker

Congrats! I still haven't found one yet.... *sigh*

> The person who planted it wants me to email him as to it's new
> location. Do I tell them where I am going to plant it or where I
> found it and keep the new location secret?

It doesn't *really* matter. At the very least, you should let him
know where you found it. It's nice to tell where you hide it too,
though, just so the person who started it can keep track of where it
is and if it went missing or not. I like to know where my
hitchhikers are, but I never tell anyone where the person hid them
until after I know it's already been picked up again.

> I also would like to know if I can place it with a box I already
> found or with one of the ones I planted already?

Absolutely. No letterbox is safe from a hitchhiker! =)

> When I put it with another box does it count as a plant for me?

Nope. You can count it as a plant if you started the box. Carved
the stamp, created a logbook for it, and that sort of thing. But
to 'place' a hitchhiker that someone else made shouldn't count!
(Sometimes, I think the term should be 'created', not 'placed'.)

Farewell!

-- Ryan
