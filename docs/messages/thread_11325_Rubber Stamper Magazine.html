Rubber Stamper Magazine

From: Fudrick (fudrick@attbi.com) | Date: 2003-03-21 00:10:41 UTC-08:00
Was in Michael's today, using my 40% off coupon to get another sheet of pink stuff.  As I was leaving, checked out the magazine rack.  Looked thru a copy of Rubber Stamper magazine.  Although it is oriented to buying and using stamps, with no info on carving your own that I noticed, it has lots of pictures and artwork that could be used for ideas for your stamps.
 
What also caught my eye were the advertisements for Stamping shows and Stamping and Scrapbooking shows, and Scrapbooking and Stamping shows, all around the country.  Didn't get the details, but they seem to be all over.  A good place to have a mini-gathering, possibly inquire about having a (hopefully free) table on Letterboxing. 
 
There is one in Portland at the Oregon Convention Center in August, must be pretty big to warrant that venue.  One at the Puyallup Fairgrounds in March or May (was in a hurry to get back to work so didn't look to closely (just like I read the clue sheets) (or my forgetter was working well). 
 
The magazine was $5.99 (more in Canada), and should likely be at your local craft supply outlet.  If not, you can check them out at www.rubberstamper.com but there isn't much info other than subscriptions and back issues and a few projects.
