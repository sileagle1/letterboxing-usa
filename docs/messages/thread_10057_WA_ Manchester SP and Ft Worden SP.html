From: hoopoe4me <scribenbirder@msn.com> (scribenbirder@msn.com) | Date: 2003-01-06 01:35:10 UTC
I was feeling mighty pleased with myself upon finishing Trial and
Errors Manchester SP boxes (3)

I ventured out without my compass today. Foolish when tackling a T
and E box.

The second box required to select a tree on a given bearing. I used
the sun and guessed and found. Trying to remember that degree value
I tackled the last box, which required a triangulation. I had two
possible spots and a distant third. The first spot was a no go ( and
too "public" for digging around IMHO). I looked at the clue sheet
and at my second spot and BINGO.

One of the most cunning placements I have found to date!

I drove on to Ft Worden and am sorry to report that all of the boxes
placed by Candlelight and Amanda from Seattle are gone. I have
written both privately and I am sure they will check ( or take my
word for it) This venue is far flung and required a bit of driving.
Those coming from the Seattle area would likely encounter a ferry
toll. I would encourage folks to check with the placers before
tackling this venue. The park is very nice with wonderful views of
the Straight of Juan de Fuca and plenty of bird action.

But no boxes.

The drive home was glorious with one of our rosey sunset in winter
afternoons. I even briefly glimpsed Mt St Helen's , her snow lit
just right in the far distance.

Hoopoe
Federal Way WA
