What makes us do it?

From: Donna Magner (donutz716@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-22 17:40:15 UTC-07:00
I dragged myself out of bed this morning, rubbed the sleep from my eyes, and sipped my first cup of coffee while checking my e-mails. I had a list of chores as long as my arm and wasn't in a hurry to start the day. Just before I logged off, I went to the Letterboxing website and checked "What's new." To my delight, there were 2 boxes in the same area that were just planted yesterday. I jumped up, ran and got dressed, brushed my teeth, : ) grabbed my keys and took a ride hoping that I would be the first to find them!

Now let me tell you something about myself...Normally I wouldn't be caught dead going anywhere without a fair amount of makeup and primping. Prior to starting letterboxing, my car was always nice and neat inside. Now I have papers strewn everywhere - directions and clues. I always have a backpack, a change of clothes, hiking boots and bottles of water on the back seat ready to go for my next adventure. I watch the weather - days in advance and plan where I can go that will allow me to find the box and get home before dark and thunder storms.

I drove feverishly, following the directions to find my prize, wondering what I would do if a letterboxing buddy was there already taking the compass readings and searching. Would I be polite and not interrupt them or would I have to wrestle them down for it? Might it be one of the members of this site who will tell the story of a wild woman that snatched the box from them and ran?

Phew, none of that happened, thank God! I found both boxes and got the first finder's certificate for both. I can't explain the excitement and passion of this "sport". I've learned about Crazy Lorenzo Dow, Pomp Equality, Eunice Stocking, and historical sites in the area. I've found trails that I didn't know existed as well as visited pet cemeteries and State forests in other parts of the state. I just can't explain exactly what makes me do it, but I thank all of you people who have made it possible and especially Suz who introduced me to it!
