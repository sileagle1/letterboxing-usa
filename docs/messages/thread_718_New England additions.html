Good morning to all! Fall continues glorious in southern New England,
although much of the color is carelessly scattered on the trails, as
though a reckless prodigal had swept through with a rash slovenliness.
Huge winds rush over the rounded hills, brushing above the dry and
leafless branches like a river.

While planes fall out of the sky on the coast, we've been hiking deep
into the woods to seed treasures near glacial erratics with sneaky-boxy
smiles on children's faces. This week's new crop has names like
Quonochontaug, Narragansett Ledges, Green Falls Pond, and Tribal
Nation. See them on our site at http://members.aol.com/drewclan/whatsne
w.htm and, please, come pay them a visit!

(Aside to Tom: I've been fiddling with a map and hope to have it up
where you can see it soon.)

Brief golden color, Jay
