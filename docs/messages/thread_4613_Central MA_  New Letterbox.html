Central MA. New Letterbox

From: Barry chase (bcec@altavista.net) | Date: 2002-03-03 18:43:02 UTC-05:00
Name:  Redemption Rock Letterbox
 
Location:  Princeton, MA
 
Date Placed: 3-3-02
 
Time and Length:  Under an hour round trip, less than a mile
 
Difficulty:  Easy walking
 
Directions to area: From Rt. 2 take the Rt. 140 exit.  Follow 140 south towards Princeton.  Approx. 3.2 miles on the right you will see a small sign for the Trustees of Reservation for Redemption Rock.  Pull in off the road into a small dirt parking area.
 
From the Worcester area, follow Interstate 190 to exit 5 Rt 140 Northbound towards Princeton.  Stay on 140 for roughly 7 -8 miles.  The sign is on your left.
 
Clues:  From the parking area follow the Mid-State trail South.  At the first fork bear off the Mid-State to the right trail.  At the next forktake a left over the stream.  Check out the cave on your right.  Follow the trail up the hill.  Bear right at the next intersection.  Notice the stone wall on your right.Follow the trail keeping the stonewall to your right.  Where the wall ends, leave the trail and walk to the end of the wall.  Look back up the wall and you will see twin oaks near the wall.  On the far side of the twin oaks in a crevasse in the wall you will find the letterbox.  Please don't move any stones to find the box. 
 
Enjoy the views and be sure to read the historical markers about Redemption Rock.  It's an easy climb up the far side of the rock.  This is our first letterbox.  Hope you enjoy it.  Barry49
