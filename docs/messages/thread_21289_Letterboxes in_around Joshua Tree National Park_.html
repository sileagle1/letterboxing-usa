From: birder579 (birder579@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 00:08:00 UTC

As with all National Parks, the park service doesn't allow
letterboxing, or geocaching either, in their parks. This has been
discussed in years past, and the park service wrote a policy paper
against it. You will have to enjoy the park and do your
letterboxing outside the park.
The Bird Stamper.

From: rscarpen (letterboxing@atlasquest.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 23:38:07 UTC

If you hide some boxes in or around Joshua Tree, I'd like to suggest
focusing more on the "around" than the "in". The NPS isn't too
receptive of letterboxes and will confiscate them if they learn of it.
So if you want your boxes to live long, healthy lives, planting them
in national parks is not a good idea.

Happy trails!
