From: Sandi Mandi (sexygirlsandi@hotmail.com) | Date: 2002-09-11 15:33:21 UTC


I was at Shawnee Lookout This past weekend looking for our
first letterbox find. I ran into a Park Ranger as we were looking
in the weeds for a letterbox. The Ranger told us that this is why
the park District was going to remove the letterbox because people
were killing the wild flowers searching for the boxs.

Being new to Letterboxing, Could someone please tell me if
there is a problem in parks with letterboxing? Is other parks
doing the same thing?

We would love to know..

 WWW.Greatparks.com

 
I find this to be a surprising shift in attitude!  My family was introduced to letterboxing BY the Hamilton County Park District.  They ran a series of 5 boxes just this past (Gawd, is it really over already?) summer, one in each park, and gave a prize for finding all 5 and bringing your logbook to the Sharon Centre, and showed it to the head naturalist.  They ran an ad in the Sunday paper, put out a special Activities flyer (also in the paper), and had a section on their website http://www.greatparks.ORG.  I think they were stopping the prize giving sometime in August.
 
We were unable to find the box in Farbach-Warner park, and asked at the Nature Barn.  The naturalist there, while getting ready for a butterfly garden tour in an hour or so, said that someone had found the box (again, placed there BY the Park District!), brought it to the Barn, and she asked us if we'd mind replacing it!  We told the naturalist at Sharon Centre about this when we'd filled our logbook, and she was delighted that we bothered to follow up with the staff at Farbach, but also concerned that the message about the boxes in the parks hadn't reached all the nature staff.
 
I, for one, definitely feel a message is in order!  This seems rather hypocritical to run a lbox series FOR THE PURPOSE of introducing letterboxing, and then asking boxers to pull their boxes from the park system.  If we lose the parks for placing boxes, where in the county CAN we box?
 
Cincinnati Boxers:  I saved the Naturalist's card from the prize bag we got for completing her box series.  While I probably shouldn't post her name and phone number on the 'Net, if you'd like to join me in giving her  call, email me privately, and I'll provide her contact info.
