`Bear'ly the top of Hurricane Ridge Letterbox, Olympic NP
near Port Angeles, WA
Clallam County

Placed: 1 Aug 2002
By: DVN2R CKR


Approximately 3 miles round trip (to the box and back to the parking
lot) through alpine meadows with glorious views. Paved trail climbs
to a panoramic view of mountains and saltwater. 700 foot elevation
change. This letterbox is within the Olympic Mountains National
Park just south of the beautiful town of Port Angeles, WA which
overlooks the Strait of Juan de Fuca. Bring your Golden Eagle or
National Park pass or be prepared to pay the approx. $10 entrance
fee at the park entrance station 5 miles beyond the Port Angeles
Visitor Center.

Hurricane Ridge Road: Open 24 hours a day mid May into October.
The rest of the year opens (weather permitting) primarily on
weekends. Call (360) 565-3131 for current conditions. Not sure if
the Hurricane Hill/Ridge trail system is open to snow shoe-rs during
the winter--it might be!

Note: This hike ends at 5,700' elevation. The elevation change
during the hike to the letterbox is approximately 650'ensure
you are healthy and physically able to undertake a challenging trek
at such a high elevation. My almost 4-year old daughter had little
difficulty on this trail but she is quite athletic. Plan
accordingly! This is also black bear and cougar countrybe
perpetually vigilant! No pets are allowed on the trail. Bring
water, food and windbreakers/raincoats as there are no amenities
along the trail. Take a camera, as well, since the views from the
top are absolutely breathtaking!

Start your trek at the Hurricane Hill trailhead (18.5 miles from the
Port Angeles Visitor Center to the parking lot for the trail//1.5
miles past the Hurricane Ridge Visitor Center). From the trailhead
parking lot, it will take about 1 to 1-1/2 hours to hike to the top
& find the letterbox (weather permitting). It will take about
another 30 minutes to descend the mountain.
