You know you're addicted to letterboxing when...

From: hodsthorn (hodsthorn@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-06-07 00:46:30 UTC
This "You know you're addicted to letterboxing when..." thread is
great...but it's time for me to come out of lurking in the shadows
and throw down the gauntlet. I knew I was addicted to letterboxing
when...

...I proposed to Hezziwig, my future wife, by hiding a letterbox with
a proposal in it...

...wrote up fake clues using an anagram of my own name...

...carved a real stamp to stick in the box, along with a logbook and
a personal stamp for the fake placer...

...and then decided to carve ANOTHER stamp as a "hitchhiker" to put
with the proposal itself because I had a little extra carving
material around.

But then, as has been pointed out before, I'm not a particularly well-
balanced person.

Hodsthorn

P.S. I also placed a limited-time two-box series in honor of our
upcoming wedding ("Going to the Chapel" in Mansfield Center, CT),
which leads me to another question: how many series are exist that
were placed in honor of a couple's wedding or engagement? I know of
two others in New England, but I'm guessing there are more than that
out there...
