a.. concerns over the possibility of inappropriate photos being posted,
b.. storage,

d.. the availability of posting photos on ones' own site or webpage.

From: (john@johnsblog.com) | Date: 2005-08-02 09:32:53 UTC-04:00
Yes, as long as we have room in LbNA group, you can put
your photos there. You can use another website to host the
pictures on the internet then use an HTML link in
your clues to display the photo.

For those of you that aren't familiar with HTML, a link in
your clues to a photo looks like this:
src="http://us.f1.yahoofs.com/groups/g_1114407/__hr_Example+of+Bad+Letterboxing!.jpg?bc4j37CBSE0GmQcw">

I got the stuff in quotes by right clicking on the photo
that I was viewing in Internet Explorer after it was
uploaded and selecting Copy Image Location from the menu.

There's a good primer on puting images on web pages here:
http://www.htmlgoodies.com/primers/html/article.php/3478181

Susan was refering to an option to upload a photo for a
letterbox directly to letterboxing.org. W experimented
with this ability recently but decided to disable it.

Choi (one of the webmasters)
