Bees and letterboxing

From: dmlathrop (pineknoll@snet.net) | Date: 2002-08-09 22:11:34 UTC-04:00
Hi folks!  We just spent a great day at the Tolland County
4-H fair where we met old friends and new ones in the realm of letterboxing.  This is the best hobby ever and meeting other letterboxing enthusiasts is great fun!  Thanks to Leader of the Pack and many others for all their hard work in promoting this wonderful pastime.  I don't normally post to the chat list, but I feel compelled today to share a story.
 
On the way home, we stopped at a couple boxes in the Coventry area  including the Coventry Grammar School Nature Trail, a quick easy kid friendly spot, but as my 8 yr old son reached for the box ('cause it was his 'turn') he stepped on an underground bee's nest and they attacked!  We ran out of there as fast as we could and as far as we could before stopping, but he had been stung five times!!  Thankfully none of us are allergic but he didn't stop screaming for a long time.  If any of you boxers out there are allergic to bees, please be sure to carry your epipens with you.  Despite what may be on your minds (purple socks, lizards, whatever)  be sure to be safe!!!
 
I will contact the school on Monday morning to let them know about the bees on their nature trail.  If you read this, don't go there! 
 
On a happy note, our family found our 200th box today, and even though "Clyde" swears he'll never go letterboxing again, I know that tomorrow is a new day and a new start.
 
Remember to Be Safe!  From the Bonnie and Clyde Gang.
