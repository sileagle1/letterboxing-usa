Re: [LbNA] Re: A WI Newbie

From: moofie (moofie@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-03-30 11:20:28 UTC-06:00
this is the article that appeared in the journal sentinel. not really an
article but a blurb on the bottom of the cue page.

ON WISCONSIN : LIVE : E-MAIL | PRINT THIS STORY


Stuff
Last Updated: Aug. 4, 2002

Hunting down a craze
You have to be careful not to fall into the lake or get run over while
looking for it, according to the instructions. You have to do math and must
be able to keep a secret.

All of this is required to find the "Calatrava/Green One" letterboxes - two
hidden, waterproof boxes with blank books inside that people hunt down with
elaborate clues. Once they find the boxes, people put rubber stamps in the
book. It's part of a national underground hobby craze called letterboxing.

For the "Calatrava/Green One" box, you have to count the number of cables in
the Santiago Calatrava-designed pedestrian bridge at the Milwaukee Art
Museum, add it to the number of nearby flag poles and walk that many paces
north along a breakwater to find another clue.

Clues for boxes nationally (including 15 in Milwaukee) are at
www.letterboxing.org.

Compiled by Kathy Flanigan of the Journal Sentinel staff




Appeared in the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel on Aug. 5, 2002.

rene and ron
