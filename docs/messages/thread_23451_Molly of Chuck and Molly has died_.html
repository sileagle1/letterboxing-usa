The following is from an Ann Landers (or Dear Abby) column from the 70s:

"A former neighbor once asked me, "doesn't it make you sad to know that
your dogs will live and die in your lifetime?"

Of course it does. I can't imagine the grief I will feel when their lives
with us have ended. All we can hope is that they will live long, healthy
lives, unencumbered by debilitating illness. That they won't be taken from
us by an untimely accident. That they will die peacefully and painlessly.
And that we won't cry for too long.

As painful as their loss will be, we will probably invite more dogs into
our lives, make them our companions, share our lives with them, and finally
mourn them as well. At the end of my life perhaps a dog will mourn for me.
I can think of no greater honor."

Mourning your loss with you,

Animal Crackers :'-(
