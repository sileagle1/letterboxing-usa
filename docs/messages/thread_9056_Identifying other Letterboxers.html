Identifying other Letterboxers

From: Alafair (ms_alafair@yahoo.com) | Date: 2002-11-09 17:41:47 UTC-08:00

Jodi,

My LBNA patch is on the small pack I carry with me. I know this sounds weird, but if I am out and I see others who I think might be letterboxes, if I am not comfortable just going up to them and asking, I shift the pack around so that the patch shows. It's brightly colored for its small size, so stands out against my black pack. It is fun to run into others and exchange stamps and friendly hellos, but caution always comes first.

I do go out alone at times (always with cell phone and pepper spray very handy) and I've sadly had to learn to be more wary. If you are out alone, trust your gut and if it feels wrong, try to remove yourself from the area as quickly and safely as possible.

I'm sure others have insights as well.

Linda a/k/a Alafair

From: (Fireflylight@aol.com) | Date: 2002-11-10 16:41:41 UTC-05:00
You can also check out other hikers hands - are they covered in ink?  It's a good indication that they just might be letterboxers!

I have had meetings on the trail twice.  The first time, I ran into Psychomommy and The Turtle.  By some miraculous "Psychommy ESP" she knew exactly who I was and asked "Are you Firefly?"  Good thing it was me, someone else might have looked at her a little funny!

The second meeting was last week.  I met VirginiaWaterBird on the trail.  Scott and I were looking for one of her boxes.  She passed us and said hello.   Then she turned around and said "Are you boxers?"  I think the fact that we were carrying pieces of paper and arguing about compass directions probably clued her in!

Coleen
"Firefly"
