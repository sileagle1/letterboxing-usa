Yes, I've been to Quebec (among other places). Bitter cold, ice on the St.
Lawrence, three and a half feet of snow... and way past winter carnival.
Found the Pink Bullet in good shape. Only the top of the cannon barrel was
protruding from the snow. It was in good shape... but I'd like to suggest a
clarification: The Pink Bullet isn't pink. It's more like a magenta
bullet. This may seem like a trivial detail, but what if, in theory, there
was a pink container in another cannon nearby? Wouldn't that create some
confusion? And what's the deal with the bullet? In a cannon? Shouldn't it
have been named The Magenta Cannonball? Sorry to nitpick, but the details
are always important... beware the pink herring.
