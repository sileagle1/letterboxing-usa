As many have noticed, the webmasters are working on an update to the
LBNA website. The first hints have been a different organization of
boxes in VT, VA, PA, FL, and some others. We're trying to make our
jobs a little easier as letterboxing grows as well as give the site a
fresher look. We hope to have some real eye candy for you soon.

As the southern new england webmaster I've been a little slow in
keeping up with my more adept colleagues. Finally, I put up the newer
format tonight. You'll see a slight difference from the other new
pages but we should have things tweaked and more uniform for you soon.

If you would, please take a look for your letterboxes and let me know
if I've lost any in the shuffle.

Thanks and happy hunting,

Jay in powdery snow CT

From: Bob Hopkins (Hopkinsr1@aol.com) | Date: 2001-01-22 10:12:14 UTC
Jay, two things about the Rhode Island boxes. First, The Ghost Town
at Sundown is actually in South Kingstown. The second thing is that
since the authorities have closed down the old Jamestown Bridge,
maybe it's time to delete those clues completely. Perhaps one day we
can replace it with one in the new Jamestown park, at the end of
Battery Lane. The town and volunteers spent a lot of time clearing
brush and cutting paths so visitors to the park could better view the
Bunkers there. This park is located off of Beavertail Road and a box
there would give folks a chance to get three stamps, starting with
Ft. Getty, then the new Battery Lane box, and ending with Beavertail
Light. After that they'd still have time to do Ft. Wetheril and
Island Sanctuary for a total of 5 stamps in one afternoon. I'll work
on the box for Battery Park, unless you'd like to give it a try and
keep all the Island boxes under Drewclan ownership.
Like the new look, keep up the good work.
Bob
