Hmm - I've never camoflauged my boxes. Excluding the recent microbox series, I've
placed around 60 boxes. To my (current) knowledge - none of them are missing.
(Lavender is still roaming and considered missing in action - but not gone!) This tally also
includes 4 out-of-state boxes currently maintained by myself, 2 in public libraries, one in
a secluded yet very urban public garden, and various others that I consider "well placed".

When it comes right down to it, you've got to consider that even camo won't protect your
box from ruthless visitors and hungry wildlife if the original placement of the box was not
well thought out. My only advice to new letterboxers that are placing out there is to scout
out your location very well. It is like anything else in life, if you do it right the first time,
there is a good chance you won't end up redoing it in the long run.

Think quality not quantity.
Phyto
