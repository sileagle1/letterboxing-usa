New Format and Clues for Race Point Lighthouse Letterbox

From: fred (lonemasswolf@covad.net) | Date: 2001-12-03 19:02:40 UTC-05:00

Letterbox Name: Race Point Lighthouse

Nearest City:   Provincetown, Barnstable County, Cape Cod, MA

Date Placed:  11 November 2001

Placed By:  Lonemasswolf, You Inspire My Spirit and their friend Jerry

 

Accessibility: The lighthouse is not open to the public. Race Point is accessible by four-wheel-drive vehicle, or foot from the Race Point Beach Parking Lot. The best time to go is off-season.

 

For a History and photographs of the lighthouse please visit

hhtp://www.lighthouse.cc/race/index.html

 

Difficulty:  4 mile round trip on sea level terrain.

All questions and comments should be directed to lonemasswolf@covad.net

 

Driving Directions: Travel east on Route 6 to the intersection with Race Point Road. Turn right on Race Point Road. Stay to the right as you pass Province Lands Road. Follow Race Point Road to the parking lot at its end. From here, walk to the ocean and turn left, follow the beach [about 2 miles] to the lighthouse.

 

CLUES TO THE LETTERBOX

Standing at the door to the lighthouse, look over the dunes in the direction of the Provincetown Tower. Dune walk toward the tower until you reach the highest dune along the lighthouse tower line of sight.  From this location you can see a higher dune on a bearing of 60O. Trek over to the tower side of this dune. If you are in the right place, the Provincetown airport runway lights will be at a bearing of 70O, the tower will be at a bearing of 120O, and the lighthouse will be at a bearing of 300O.

Step off 20 paces on a line just to the left of the tower. The Race Point Lighthouse Letterbox lies beneath a collection of stones nestled inside the beach shrubs.
