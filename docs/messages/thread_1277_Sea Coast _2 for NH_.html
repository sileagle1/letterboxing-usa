Here's a couple of letterboxes we placed on our recent trip to the
New Hampshire coast:

Sea Coast
Location: Odiorne Point State Park in Rye, New Hampshire. Seasonal
fees: sorry, no dogs allowed. This little slice of the mountainous
Granite State doesn't know if it's Massachusetts or Maine, and is
insistently marketed as the "Sea Coast," as though saying it twice
reinforces that it is indeed New Hampshire. It's beautiful, but we
wish it was a little more thoughtfully developed.

Difficulty: easy 2 miles with one steep and overgrown scramble. One
or two hours over a counterclockwise shoreside loop.

Directions: On NH Rte. 1A just outside Portsmouth. Park at the main
state park entrance by the Science Center.

Between the parking lot and the Science Center, climb the stairs over
the Battery and follow the trail northwards down, across the road,
and into the woods. The trail follows beautifully northwest along the
coastline. Stick with the wide main path when it curves left a
little, away from shore. At an amazing row of sugar maples, fork
right to a large bunker. Exploring this relic, climb to the top for
views, then turn north again on the path along the "hill." Pass a
smaller, house-like concrete gun emplacement and a bit farther along,
on your left/northwest, find a concrete pier with a sealed steel vent
shaft. The Rye Beach Letterbox is hidden behind.
A short way further is a second, identical vent, but not well-sealed,
and a few yards farther is an open grassy area with views just right
for stamping in. Please replace the letterbox carefuly in this
popular spot as the local party crowd are likely to use it for
kindling if they stumble upon it.
Now you can either turn back, or continue on for some lovely flat
hiking. If you continue north and downhill you'll find a sandy point,
rocky shore, a wonderful jetty, more bunkers, and a boat ramp before
turning south back to the Science Center on the paved roadside bike
trail.
Back at the beginning, continue east past the parking lot to the
picnic area on the point. Follow the path to several ring-shaped gun
sites in line, which point at an overgrown, shoreside, curved stone
bench. The Odiorne Point Letterbox is under a big square flat rock on
your right as you walk up, at the benches' western end.

Hope you get a chance to stamp in to these boxes!
