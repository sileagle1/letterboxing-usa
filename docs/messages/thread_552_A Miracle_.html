From: jjp (exposto1@airmail.net) | Date: 1999-07-03 17:58:36 UTC-05:00
Exciting news from Texas! I had postcards in all 4 of the letterboxes I
put out for the Duck Creek series--and when boxes #1, #3 #4 went missing I
figured they were just part of the loss. (Actually, I found some of the
cards from box #3--whoever had found it had strewn some of the the
contents, torn pages from the book, and left the postcards to become
waterlogged). But #1 and #4 disappeared without a trace! Yesterday one of
the postcards from box #4 turned up in my mailbox! Here's what it says:

"I found your letterbox on 6-28-99. This was the first time I heard of a
letterbox. I think I put it about the same place I found it but it looked
as if it may have washed away. Georgia"

I'm very excited to know the box is out there, but now I want to know WHERE
so I can have accurate clues. I'm thinking I'll leave notices for Georgia
posted on the trees? No return address, so no way to contact her easily....

Any ideas, letterboxers???
