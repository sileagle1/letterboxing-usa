"be ma" wrote:

The Little Ponderosa BBQ claims to have the best BBQ: pork, beef,
chicken and turkey, in the area. On Thrusdays it is open from 8 a.m.
to 4 p.m and Friday and Sat. 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. Reading the sad looking
map from the flier that I have, the BBQ place is located on Potomac
St. which is off of Shenandoah St. which is located down by the river
close to a train station and the parking lot. We enjoyed the food
when we were there in May.The ice cream place is down the street from
the BBQ. At the corner of Shenandoah St. and Potomac St., away from
town towards the river is an overlook. At this location is the AT and
a bridge/ train tressel that crosses the river. The walk over the
bridge is fun to do with kids. Once you crosss the river you are in
Maryland and the trail is the C&O canal. If I remember correctly the
AT heads south for about a mile on the C&O before heading up the hill
(for those who wish to do a little walk).

From: Kittel Greg M (greg.m.kittel@irs.gov) | Date: 2003-06-24 08:15:56 UTC-04:00
The Harper's Ferry Youth Hostel, located on the Appalachian Trail, It's
actually in Maryland in Sandy Hook. I was there in April with our scout
troop when we biked the C&O canal. Current proprietors are Loretta & Ray
Johnson (301) 834-7652 the address is 19123 Sandy Hook Rd Knoxville, Md
21758. They have a huge yard with barbecue pits, a full kitchen etc and
beds for those want to spend the night. $15.00 over 15 and $7.50 for those
under. Bring your own sheets or rent 'em for a buck. oh yeah they have
bathrooms and a shower for Ryan :). They would probably love to have ya'll
come but I'm not sure it's best to call 'em first.

Unfortunately I can't make it. But send my regards.
