From: Otis' Friends (otisfriends@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-04-01 10:29:17 UTC-07:00
The public libraries in York County, Pennsylvania did an "Activity Bug" series last year that ran through the entire summer: thirty boxes in area parks with custom-ordered, self-inking stamps of different insects. Obviously, not everyone who participated had the best boxing technique (and as far as I know, none of the noxers took up letterboxing as a result), but a lot of families got outside and used the parks. I think I prefer St. Louis' approach. This won't create social trails and inexperienced kids won't get poison ivy in the face.
