> Jodi,
> My LBNA patch is on the small pack I carry with me. I know this
sounds weird, but if I am out and I see others who I think might be
letterboxes, if I am not comfortable just going up to them and
asking, I shift the pack around so that the patch shows. It's
brightly colored for its small size, so stands out against my black
pack. It is fun to run into others and exchange stamps and friendly
hellos, but caution always comes first.
> I do go out alone at times (always with cell phone and pepper spray
very handy) and I've sadly had to learn to be more wary. If you are
out alone, trust your gut and if it feels wrong, try to remove
yourself from the area as quickly and safely as possible.
> I'm sure others have insights as well.
