If I weren't so lazy, I would refer you to SIlent Doug's website. He has a
great glossary. Of course, that requires me to find the link in my messy
desk top.


A postal letterbox is one that is mailed through the "regular" mail to
people. It is a lot of fun to get them in your mailbox! Especially when it
is not boxing weather around here.

They typically contain a stamp and log book, so the stamping-in process the
same. (How you count them is entirely up to you--this is a subject of great
debate. SOme count them as finds; some count them seperately. I simply
don't count them and just enjoy the stamp) You then would mail them on to
the next person on the list.

Some have closed lists--you have to get on when they are annonced. OThers
have open lists--people are added periodically. Either way, if you watch
the LBNA list, you wills ee periodic postings.
