Having lived in Richmond for almost 20 years, surrounded by Civil War
history, it took letterboxing and Jay's great mystery to spark an
interest in the Civil War. A trip to the library and a little
resesarch led me to Bartholomew's resting spot. Heading out after
work, I had to make a hard drive like Sheridan to beat the dark.
Upon stamping in, I was joined by two young civil war ghost hunters,
armed with cap guns and rifles. I tucked the box into my pack and
listened to their tales of distant drum beats and musket fire. When
they were satisfied that I was sure I'd heard the same thing, they
headed down the trail wishing me happy trails. The box tucked safely
back in it's hiding spot, I headed home feeling pretty good about
having found this box. Thanks, Jay....nice job!
