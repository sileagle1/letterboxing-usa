Fantastic Letterbox Container!

From: C&K (pedersenteevens@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2005-09-16 11:40:20 UTC-04:00
Hello All --

I have found what might be the best letterbox container! (I'm so
excited, LOL!)

I've been keeping my eyes out for months for a good, solid container...
something with a secure lid and all that good stuff; you know. Well, I
just picked up something called "Snapware" at my local Kroger. They
never carried this before. It's by a company called Komax and it's made
in Korea. I've put more details about it on my web page if you want to
take a look at it.

You'll need to scroll about a quarter of the way down the page to where
it says "UPDATE: Sept. 2005..." and follow the link there to see the
cool container.

http://cablespeed.com/~pedersenteevens/Letterboxing.html

What's exciting is the lid locks on with these side flaps, and it
claims to be 100% airtight and 100% watertight! Pretty cool, eh?

I was wondering if anyone else has used this, and if so... how well has
yours worked and how well has it held up?

Feel free to email me on list, or privately... either way I'd love to
get your feedback! Thanks!!

PennyPenny
South Lyon, MI

(PS... I've posted this to both the new boxers group and the main
group; so sorry to those of you who get this twice.)

(after some have been eaten). I don't throw out stale chips anymore: they
stay fresh for weeks.

For those of you in the NY or Northern PA area...Wegman's stores are
now carrying a complete line of Lock n Lock. I swear by these
containers and the best part about them (besides being air and water
tight because of the rim gasket) is that they are FAST and EASY to
close...meaning that even the most hurried boxer is good about closing
them.

My favorite container is the 1.8 cup 4" x 4" container. PERFECT. I also
use the bigger ones for hostels and geocaching. I have a complete array
for camping stuff as well.
