From: Chuck Straub (woodschuckstraub@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-03-28 06:01:01 UTC-08:00
If you spent all day in Mansfield CT, knew the area
and trails,and didn't tire out too quickly, I'd say
you had a chance of getting 40 just in this one town
in a day. You are right, numbers really mean anything.
People use the numbers as a goal sometimes. When I got
close to 500 PFX I also pushed a little harder just to
get to that number. My biggest plans this year for
hiking is on trails that I won't be finding any new
letterboxes. I do plan on planting some though while
I'm out there. The hike is first for me and the box
count second. I must admit,there are days when I just
go out and get the "easy ones" and I guess thats for
the count and to get some new stamp images. You might
say we all have a collection of stamp art. I still
think that some of our better and tougher walks were
to get just 1 or 2 boxes and I remember the walk
better than the actual box.....Chuck and Molly

Just discovered the list of PFX clubs. I don't know when we passed the
500 PFX mark but we hit F500 on Sep 4, 2005. As of Oct 13, 2005 we
have found 639 boxes, have 53 exchanges, and have adopted one box. All
this is perhaps small potatoes for lots of folks, but we feel good
about our efforts, given that we "officially" started letterboxing
just last February. However, we are not on any PFX list other than
atlasquest.
