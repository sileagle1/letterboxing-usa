Hunting isn't generally allowed in Maryland on Sundays. Here's a snippet
from the MD Department of Natural Resources web site
(http://www.dnr.state.md.us/huntersguide/) (Actually this page:
http://www.dnr.state.md.us/huntersguide/geninfo.html):

>IT IS UNLAWFUL TO:



>Hunt any game bird or mammal on Sunday with the exception of: 1) a person
>who possesses a Falconry Permit, who may hunt specified game birds and
>mammals on Sundays during the open season; 2) an unarmed person
>participating in an organized fox chase, who may chase foxes on Sundays;
>and 3) persons hunting on Regulated Shooting Areas may hunt certain
>pen-reared game birds on Sundays.



>Note: The woodchuck and nutria are classified as "unprotected mammals." As
>such, a hunting license is not required to hunt woodchucks and nutria
>except in Baltimore and Frederick Counties. Woodchucks and nutria may be
>hunted throughout the year, including on Sundays. Hunters must wear
>fluorescent orange while hunting woodchucks. All legal hunting devices may
>be used to hunt woodchucks. Nutria may be hunted with all legal hunting
>devices except that rifles are limited to .22 caliber rimfire.

While I don't make a habit of visiting the same places as hunters during
the season, I bought an inexpensive fluorescent orange vest to wear over my
jacket while hiking on Sundays or in areas where hunters might be illegally
hunting.

Doug
