To That Most Wonderful of Letterboxing Institutions- Butterfly !

Butterfly, you are the most beautiful and amazing Creature I
know ! I admire, I respect, and I adore you. But you must be very
careful in the Winter. We want you out there in that Magical Time of
Spring when the Pixies are about.
Slow Camel knows that Butterfly would not want others to know that
our Butterfly took a hard spill today but Slow Camel "tells on"
Butterfly out of Love and Concern. And if this Camel had been with
Butterfly, we would have been going at a very S-L-O-W pace, perhaps
even holding hands. We would do the "careful walk" in this white
stuff. (Camels prefer sand but we like to letterbox so much, we will
go just about anywhere)
Please, if you are close to Butterfly and you love her, please ask
her to be careful. She cannot refuse us all. Her heart is too BIG to
say "no" to us. We know that Butterfly has gotten over 500 boxes but
there is no rush in Wintertime. We are not asking that you stop, only
that you practice- Easy Does It, My Dear Friend.
Respectfully,
Slow camel

From: Joseph L. Sperrazza (jsperrazza@snet.net) | Date: 2003-01-08 06:07:12 UTC-05:00
O.K. everyone--I must confess that I did not fall while searching for a
letterbox. I was actually going sledding and there just happens to be a
letterbox RIGHT There!
Fact: I never go hiking alone, as Bluebird knows. I was with Angel when
I fell, so what better person to be with. ? This morning I am no stiffer
than usual and that is good news for someone soon to be 72. I do want
to thank Chuck and Aili and Camel and Lobsta and other well wishers for
your kind concern. I have heard tell that Camels spit, well our kind and
wonderful slow Camel was fast to 'spit' out my 'downfall' [grin] . As
for the number of boxes I have found, please know that for me the hikes
and walks and friends I go with, mean much more than that number that
keeps on growing. I love to look through my log books full of colorful
beautiful stamps, all of which have fond memories attached. I truly do
not know my F.or X count, but am proud of my slowly growing P.count and
dream of them giving pleasure to others.
Happy butterfly wishes
Butterfly
