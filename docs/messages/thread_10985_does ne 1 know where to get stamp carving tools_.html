From: Amanda Arkebauer <samanark@yahoo.com> (samanark@yahoo.com) | Date: 2003-03-02 19:51:36 UTC

In Charlotte, NC you can try the Michael's craft stores. They will
sometimes have a "starters kit" from Speedball for carving
rubberstamps. It comes with a carving tool and #1 blade. It also has
a piece of the pink carving medium and tracing paper and instructions
on how to transfer your image to the carving medium. It is usually
about $10 or $11 dollars.

--Amanda from Seattle

>
It comes with a carving tool and #1 blade.

Actually, the blade is a #2 blade, thus the blade in the kit is a
trifle thicker than the #1 blade that purchasers of the starter kit
will have to purchase separately. I found the #2 blade too thick to do
very fine carving and ordered my #1 blade from one of the online
suppliers listed at Mirkwood's wonderful Web site. The name of the
supplier escapes me, but I believe Mirkwood's Web site is on
letterboxing.org as well as in the Links section of the Yahoo! group.

And check out the art supply stores in your town. If you have a
college or university with an Art Department, the students have to buy
supplies from somewhere.

-- mlg
