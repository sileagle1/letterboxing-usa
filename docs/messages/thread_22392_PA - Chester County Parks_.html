Like National Parks, Chester County Parks (and other parks in
Pennsyvania) have rules and regulations about placing anything in the
park without permission.

I would suggest contacting the Pennsylvania Recreation and Park
Society who can get you more information about all of the Pennsylvania
Recreation and Park agencies and main contact information. The
Pennsylvania Recreation and Park Society may be reached at
814-234-4272 or at www.prps.org.

From: Jan (janila@dejazzd.com) | Date: 2005-08-04 20:31:20 UTC
We have been discussing this on the LBMA message board since Chester
County is in our area. I think I may be the one who got the ball
rolling on this when a county park discovered that I had planted boxes
in their park. They simply asked that I send them some info so that
they could store this info in case anyone would report a suspicious
box in the park. The park official forwarded me a form to fill out
which I did and he granted permission immediately. I placed a copy of
this form in the file section of the LBMA board. I have to say that I
am totally pleased with the way that this was handled by the county
park official that I was dealing with, he was courteous, informative
and very supportive and I urge anyone else who wishes to plant a box
in Chester County, PA, to use the form first.

Jan of Team Little Dog
