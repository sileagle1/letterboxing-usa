Re: quick question

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2003-08-22 16:42:28 UTC
A "C" is used by some to denote confiscated boxes, which are boxes
that were planted and then confiscated by the park service (i.e. in
National Parks or other areas that are not letterbox-friendly).

Some people have been using "A" for attempted finds, but you could
use it to indicate adopted boxes if you would like. It is your count
and it really only matters to you... although be prepared to answer
questions if you have letters other than the ordinary.

I don't count boxes that I have adopted at all, as I do not count
boxes that have been sent to me by another boxer to be placed in
Michigan, or boxes that I send to other places to be placed there,
usually as part of a series. But that's just me... I'm not really
into the numbers, so I don't really care if they show up in a count
or not.

Do it however it makes sense to you.

SpringChick
