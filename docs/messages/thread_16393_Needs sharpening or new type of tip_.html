Well, I know if my blade isn't sharp it ends up making a shaggy cut.
This also happens if I try to go back and re-cut an edge. Sometimes if
I support this edge with a finger and carefully cut straight down with
a really sharp blade it works out...I don't think I'm a really skilled
carver (from what I've seen by ScoutDogs & others) but I have gained a
LOT of control by holding just the nib in my fingers and forgetting
the holder. This way it becomes an extension of my hand.

The finest line cutting tool, beside a laser, I have found is the Two
Cherries 0.5 mm gouge. I got mine from Mountain Heritage Crafters. It
runs about $42.

You can also use a hypodermic needle. The side of the point of a 16
gauge is pretty good. I used a smaller one for cutting the whites of
JugglerMouse's eyes.

JugglerMouse, who carves as fine as I have seen, uses the Speedball
set but very lightly and carefully. She has more patience than I do.

Hope this helps,
Sir B
