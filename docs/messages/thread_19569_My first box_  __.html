My first box! :)

From: (davyschris@aol.com) | Date: 2004-07-23 21:56:56 UTC-04:00
Just wanted to share my excitement with you all. :) I planted my first
letterbox tonight, a tribute to my mom. And my next one will be a tribute to my
great grandmother. I bought the stamp tonight. It's perfect. :)

Just wanted to share. I also passed on my first hitchhiker that I found two
days ago, and found my first bonus box. It was a day of letterboxing firsts.
:)

My friend, who I got hooked on letterboxing, came over last night and was
telling me how frustrated she was that she'd found a hitchhiker in a box and
hadn't known what it was until she called me to say that someone had put an
additional stamp in the box she'd found. She didn't know to take it. She was still
kicking herself when she got to my place at 10:30 pm, so we jumped into the
car and found the park, and found the box, so she could retrieve it. ;) I
wouldn't ordinarily recommend letterboxing at 11 pm in a questionable area, but
she was bound and determined. ;)

Chrissy

Re: [LbNA] My first box! :)

From: monkeytoes108 (cruschhaupt@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-24 22:58:46 UTC
A hitchhiker has to have at least a stamp and a logbook. Most do
not have their own inkpad or pencil to save space. Since they are
only found in other letterboxes, you can use that boxes supplies to
stamp in. It's also a good idea to have your own stamp pad. A lot
of boxes don't have one, or they've dried out.

As for the ziploc, a hitchhiker(stamp and logbook) is usually
contained by some kind of container. I put mine in 35mm film
canisters. I've found some that are just in a baggie and others that
are in their own little boxes.

And I say if you find a HH in your own box, log it as a find, and
it's up to you wether to move it along or leave it behind. Take it
if you expect to visit other letterboxes soon. Leave it if you don't
so someone else can relocate it quickly and it doesn't sit in your
backpack for months.


-MonkeyToes
