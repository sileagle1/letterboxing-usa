been changed.

From: California Bear (californiabear@comcast.net) | Date: 2003-08-26 07:08:24 UTC-07:00
I had a teacher from New Jersey tell me that New Jersey stole the Statue of Liberty from New York by virtue of the definition of the boundary between New York and New Jersey. The boundary is defined as the middle of the river. New Jersey has spent many decades dumping garbage along their shoreline and thus have extended it into the river, thereby moving the halfway point closer to the New York shore. At some point, the line moved to the point that the Statue is now in New Jersey!

I'm sure this technicality doesn't change the fact that the Statue is considered to be part of New York but I found it amusing that New Jersey could steal the Statue of Liberty with garbage. : )
